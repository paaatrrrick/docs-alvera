## Overview

Game of Thrones is an American fantasy drama television series created by David Benioff and D. B. Weiss for HBO. It is an adaptation of *A Song of Ice and Fire*, a series of fantasy novels by George R. R. Martin. The show premiered on April 17, 2011, and concluded on May 19, 2019, with 73 episodes broadcast over eight seasons.

## Setting

The series is set on the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos. It chronicles the violent dynastic struggles among the realm's noble families for the Iron Throne, while other families fight for independence from it.

### The major houses

- **House Stark** — Lords of Winterfell and Wardens of the North. Words: "Winter is Coming."
- **House Lannister** — Lords of Casterly Rock. Words: "Hear Me Roar!" (Unofficial: "A Lannister always pays his debts.")
- **House Targaryen** — The exiled royal house of dragonlords. Words: "Fire and Blood."
- **House Baratheon** — Lords of Storm's End. Words: "Ours is the Fury."
- **House Greyjoy** — Lords of the Iron Islands. Words: "We Do Not Sow."
- **House Tyrell** — Lords of Highgarden. Words: "Growing Strong."

## Notable characters

- **Jon Snow** — The bastard son of Eddard Stark, raised at Winterfell.
- **Daenerys Targaryen** — The Mother of Dragons and last surviving Targaryen.
- **Tyrion Lannister** — A clever and witty member of House Lannister.
- **Cersei Lannister** — Queen of the Seven Kingdoms and twin to Jaime.
- **Arya Stark** — The youngest Stark daughter, trained as a Faceless Man.
- **Sansa Stark** — The eldest Stark daughter who becomes Queen in the North.

## Cultural impact

Game of Thrones became a global phenomenon, winning 59 Primetime Emmy Awards — the most by any drama series. It is widely regarded as one of the greatest television series of all time, though its final season received mixed reviews from critics and fans.
