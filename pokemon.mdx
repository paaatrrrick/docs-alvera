## Overview

Pokémon is a Japanese media franchise created by Satoshi Tajiri and Ken Sugimori, first released by Nintendo, Game Freak, and Creatures in 1996. It centers on fictional creatures called Pokémon, which humans known as Trainers catch and train to battle each other for sport. It is the highest-grossing media franchise of all time.

## Core concept

Players take on the role of a Pokémon Trainer with three main goals:

- **Catch** wild Pokémon using Poké Balls.
- **Train** them through battles to gain experience and evolve.
- **Complete** the Pokédex, a digital encyclopedia of every known species.

## Pokémon types

Every Pokémon has one or two of 18 elemental types that determine its strengths and weaknesses in battle. Common types include:

- **Fire** — Strong against Grass, Bug, Ice, and Steel.
- **Water** — Strong against Fire, Ground, and Rock.
- **Grass** — Strong against Water, Ground, and Rock.
- **Electric** — Strong against Water and Flying.
- **Psychic** — Strong against Fighting and Poison.
- **Dragon** — Strong against other Dragon-types.

## Iconic Pokémon

- **Pikachu** — The Electric-type mascot of the franchise and partner of Ash Ketchum in the anime.
- **Charizard** — The Fire/Flying-type final evolution of Charmander, a fan favorite since Generation I.
- **Mewtwo** — A legendary Psychic-type created through genetic engineering.
- **Eevee** — A Normal-type Pokémon known for its many possible evolutions.
- **Lucario** — A Fighting/Steel-type capable of sensing aura.
- **Greninja** — A Water/Dark-type ninja frog with a devoted fanbase.

## Media and merchandise

The franchise spans well beyond video games:

- **Anime** — Over 1,200 episodes across multiple series, beginning in 1997.
- **Trading Card Game** — Launched in 1996, with billions of cards sold worldwide.
- **Films** — More than 20 theatrical releases, including the live-action *Detective Pikachu* (2019).
- **Mobile games** — Including the cultural phenomenon *Pokémon GO* (2016).

## Cultural impact

Pokémon has generated over $100 billion in revenue, making it the most successful media franchise in history. It has influenced generations of fans, popularized the monster-collecting genre, and become a recognizable part of global pop culture.
