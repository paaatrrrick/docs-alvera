## Overview

Pokémon is a Japanese media franchise created by Satoshi Tajiri and Ken Sugimori, managed by The Pokémon Company. It launched on February 27, 1996 with *Pocket Monsters Red and Green* for the Game Boy in Japan, and has since grown into the highest-grossing media franchise of all time, spanning video games, a long-running anime, trading cards, films, and merchandise.

## Core concept

You play as a Trainer who travels a region, catches wild Pokémon in Poké Balls, builds a team of up to six, and battles other Trainers. The long-term goals are to complete the regional Pokédex and defeat the Elite Four to become Champion.

### Type matchups

Battles revolve around an 18-type rock-paper-scissors system. A few key matchups:

- **Water** beats Fire, Ground, and Rock.
- **Fire** beats Grass, Bug, Ice, and Steel.
- **Grass** beats Water, Ground, and Rock.
- **Electric** beats Water and Flying.
- **Ghost** and **Psychic** are strong against each other's classic counters but have notable immunities.

## Iconic Pokémon

- **Pikachu** — The Electric-type mascot of the franchise and Ash Ketchum's partner in the anime.
- **Charizard** — A fan-favorite Fire/Flying starter evolution from Kanto.
- **Mewtwo** — A genetically engineered Psychic-type and one of the original Legendaries.
- **Eevee** — A Normal-type known for its many evolutionary branches (Eeveelutions).
- **Lucario** — A Fighting/Steel-type Aura Pokémon introduced in Sinnoh.
- **Gengar** — A mischievous Ghost/Poison-type from the original 151.

## Cultural impact

Pokémon is the highest-grossing media franchise in history, with cumulative revenue exceeding $100 billion. The Trading Card Game has shipped over 64 billion cards worldwide, and *Pokémon GO* helped popularize mobile augmented reality on launch in 2016. The anime, which began airing in 1997, remains one of the longest-running video game adaptations on television.
