## Overview

Pokémon is a media franchise created by Satoshi Tajiri and Ken Sugimori, launched by Nintendo, Game Freak, and Creatures in 1996. It centers on fictional creatures called Pokémon, which Trainers catch, train, and battle.

## Types

Each Pokémon belongs to one or two of 18 elemental types, which determine its strengths and weaknesses in battle.

| Type     | Strong against            | Weak against              |
| -------- | ------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Fire     | Grass, Bug, Ice, Steel    | Water, Ground, Rock       |
| Water    | Fire, Ground, Rock        | Electric, Grass           |
| Grass    | Water, Ground, Rock       | Fire, Ice, Flying, Bug    |
| Electric | Water, Flying             | Ground                    |
| Psychic  | Fighting, Poison          | Bug, Ghost, Dark          |
| Dragon   | Dragon                    | Ice, Dragon, Fairy        |

## Generations

Each main-series release introduces a new generation of Pokémon and a new region.

- **Gen I** — Kanto (Red, Blue, Yellow)
- **Gen II** — Johto (Gold, Silver, Crystal)
- **Gen III** — Hoenn (Ruby, Sapphire, Emerald)
- **Gen IV** — Sinnoh (Diamond, Pearl, Platinum)
- **Gen V** — Unova (Black, White)
- **Gen VI** — Kalos (X, Y)
- **Gen VII** — Alola (Sun, Moon)
- **Gen VIII** — Galar (Sword, Shield)
- **Gen IX** — Paldea (Scarlet, Violet)

## Trainers and battles

A Trainer captures Pokémon using Poké Balls, builds a team of up to six, and battles other Trainers or wild Pokémon. Battles use a turn-based system where moves, types, stats, and status conditions determine the outcome.

## Beyond the games

The franchise extends well past the core RPGs:

- **Anime** — Long-running animated series following Ash Ketchum and, more recently, Liko and Roy.
- **Trading Card Game** — A collectible card game launched in 1996 and still actively expanded.
- **Pokémon GO** — Mobile augmented-reality game by Niantic.
- **Spin-offs** — Mystery Dungeon, Pokémon Snap, Pokkén Tournament, Pokémon Unite, and more.

## Competitive play

Competitive Pokémon battling is organized around official formats run by The Pokémon Company International.

- **VGC (Video Game Championships)** — Double battles using the current main-series title.
- **Smogon tiers** — Community-run singles formats (OU, UU, Ubers, and more).
- **Pokémon TCG** — Standard and Expanded formats for the trading card game.

Teams are typically built around type coverage, speed control, and a clear win condition.
