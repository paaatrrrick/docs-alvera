## Overview

Pokémon is a Japanese media franchise created by Satoshi Tajiri and Ken Sugimori, owned by The Pokémon Company (a collaboration between Nintendo, Game Freak, and Creatures Inc.). It launched in February 1996 with the release of *Pocket Monsters Red and Green* for the Game Boy in Japan and has since grown into the highest-grossing media franchise of all time.

## Setting

The franchise is set in a world where humans coexist with creatures called Pokémon. Trainers capture Pokémon using Poké Balls, train them, and pit them against other trainers in battles. The core games are organized into **generations**, each introducing a new region modeled loosely on a real-world location.

### The main regions

- **Kanto** — Generation I. Based on the Kantō region of Japan.
- **Johto** — Generation II. Based on the Kansai region.
- **Hoenn** — Generation III. Based on Kyushu.
- **Sinnoh** — Generation IV. Based on Hokkaido.
- **Unova** — Generation V. Based on New York City.
- **Kalos** — Generation VI. Based on France.
- **Alola** — Generation VII. Based on Hawaii.
- **Galar** — Generation VIII. Based on the United Kingdom.
- **Paldea** — Generation IX. Based on the Iberian Peninsula.

## The 18 types

Fire, Water, Grass, Electric, Ice, Fighting, Poison, Ground, Flying, Psychic, Bug, Rock, Ghost, Dragon, Dark, Steel, Fairy, and Normal. Each type has strengths and weaknesses against others, creating a deep rock-paper-scissors combat system.

## Iconic Pokémon

- **Pikachu (#025)** — The Electric-type mascot of the entire franchise and Ash Ketchum's partner in the anime.
- **Charizard (#006)** — A fan-favorite Fire/Flying-type and final evolution of Charmander.
- **Mewtwo (#150)** — A genetically engineered Psychic-type legendary, the antagonist of the first film.
- **Eevee (#133)** — A Normal-type with nine possible evolutions, one for nearly every type branch.
- **Lucario (#448)** — A Fighting/Steel-type that senses aura, popularized through *Pokémon: Lucario and the Mystery of Mew*.
- **Greninja (#658)** — A Water/Dark-type ninja frog and one of the most popular starter evolutions.

## The anime and beyond

The *Pokémon* animated series premiered in Japan in April 1997 and follows Ash Ketchum and Pikachu on their journey to become a Pokémon Master. Beyond the games and anime, the franchise spans the Pokémon Trading Card Game, films, manga, *Pokémon GO* (2016), and *Pokémon Unite*.

## Cultural impact

As of 2024, Pokémon is the highest-grossing media franchise in history, generating over $100 billion in lifetime revenue. *Pokémon GO* alone introduced augmented reality gaming to a mainstream audience, and the trading card game continues to drive a multibillion-dollar collectibles market.
