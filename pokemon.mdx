## Overview

Pokémon is a Japanese media franchise created by Satoshi Tajiri and Ken Sugimori, owned by The Pokémon Company (a collaboration of Nintendo, Game Freak, and Creatures Inc.). It launched in February 1996 with *Pocket Monsters: Red and Green* for the Game Boy and has since grown into the highest-grossing media franchise of all time.

The series follows Trainers who capture, raise, and battle creatures known as Pokémon, with the central motto: "Gotta catch 'em all."

## Core gameplay

In the mainline role-playing games, you:

- Choose a starter Pokémon from a regional professor.
- Travel a region, catching wild Pokémon in Poké Balls.
- Train your team by battling other Trainers and wild Pokémon.
- Defeat eight Gym Leaders to earn badges.
- Challenge the Elite Four and Champion to become the region's strongest Trainer.

## The eighteen types

Every Pokémon and move belongs to one or two types. Type matchups determine damage multipliers in battle.

## Iconic Pokémon

- **Pikachu** — The Electric-type mascot of the franchise and Ash Ketchum's partner in the anime.
- **Charizard** — A fan-favorite Fire/Flying-type and the final evolution of Charmander.
- **Mewtwo** — A Psychic-type Legendary cloned from Mew, central to *Pokémon: The First Movie*.
- **Eevee** — A Normal-type with nine distinct evolutionary paths called Eeveelutions.
- **Lucario** — A Fighting/Steel-type sensitive to the aura of all living things.
- **Greninja** — A Water/Dark-type ninja frog with a competitive Protean ability.

## Beyond the games

The franchise spans the long-running anime (1997–present), the Pokémon Trading Card Game, manga, films, and spin-offs such as *Pokémon GO*, *Pokémon Stadium*, *Pokémon Snap*, and *Pokémon Legends: Arceus*. *Pokémon GO* alone has earned over $10 billion in revenue since its 2016 launch.
