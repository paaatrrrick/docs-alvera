## Overview

Pokémon is a Japanese media franchise created by Satoshi Tajiri and Ken Sugimori, launched by Nintendo, Game Freak, and Creatures Inc. in 1996. It began with the *Pocket Monsters Red and Green* video games for the Game Boy and has grown into one of the highest-grossing media franchises of all time, spanning video games, an animated series, trading cards, films, manga, and merchandise.

The central concept follows Pokémon Trainers who catch, train, and battle creatures called Pokémon. The series motto, "Gotta catch 'em all," captures the collection-driven core of the experience.

## Pokémon types

Pokémon and their moves belong to one or two of 18 elemental types, which determine attack effectiveness:

- Normal, Fire, Water, Grass, Electric, Ice
- Fighting, Poison, Ground, Flying, Psychic, Bug
- Rock, Ghost, Dragon, Dark, Steel, Fairy

Type matchups create a rock-paper-scissors layer of strategy. For example, Water is strong against Fire, weak against Grass, and resistant to Steel.

## Iconic Pokémon

- **Pikachu** — The Electric-type mascot of the franchise and partner of Ash Ketchum in the animated series.
- **Charizard** — A fan-favorite Fire/Flying dragon-like final evolution of Charmander.
- **Mewtwo** — A Psychic-type Legendary created through genetic engineering from Mew.
- **Eevee** — A Normal-type Pokémon known for its eight different evolutionary forms (Eeveelutions).
- **Lucario** — A Fighting/Steel Aura Pokémon introduced in Generation IV.

## The animated series

The anime debuted in 1997 and follows the journey of Ash Ketchum and his Pikachu across multiple regions. Ash's quest concluded after 25 years with the *Ultimate Journeys* arc, in which he was finally crowned World Champion. The series then transitioned to *Pokémon Horizons*, featuring new protagonists Liko and Roy.

## Pokémon Trading Card Game

The Pokémon TCG launched in 1996 in Japan and 1998 internationally. Players build 60-card decks and battle by attacking with Pokémon, attaching Energy cards, and knocking out opponents to claim Prize cards. The game has a thriving competitive scene with annual World Championships.

## Spin-off games

Beyond the mainline series, Pokémon has expanded into many genres:

- **Pokémon GO** — A 2016 augmented-reality mobile game by Niantic.
- **Pokémon Mystery Dungeon** — Roguelike dungeon crawlers where the player *is* a Pokémon.
- **Pokémon Snap** — On-rails photography games.
- **Pokkén Tournament** — A fighting game co-developed with Bandai Namco.
- **Detective Pikachu** — Adventure game and 2019 live-action film starring Ryan Reynolds.

## Legacy

With over 1,000 unique species across nine generations, Pokémon remains the highest-grossing media franchise in history. It has shaped multiple generations of players, fostered a competitive esports community through Pokémon VGC, and continues to expand with new games, anime, and live-action adaptations.
