## Overview

Pokémon is a Japanese media franchise created by Satoshi Tajiri and Ken Sugimori, managed by The Pokémon Company — a collaboration between Nintendo, Game Freak, and Creatures Inc. It launched on February 27, 1996, with the release of *Pocket Monsters Red* and *Green* for the Game Boy in Japan, and has grown into the highest-grossing media franchise of all time.

## Core concept

Players take on the role of a Pokémon Trainer who catches, raises, and battles creatures called Pokémon. The series motto — "Gotta catch 'em all!" — captures the core loop of exploration, capture, training, and competition.

## Main regions

Each generation of games introduces a new region, loosely based on a real-world location.

- **Kanto** — The original region, based on the Kantō area of Japan.
- **Johto** — Introduced in *Gold* and *Silver*, based on the Kansai region.
- **Hoenn** — Introduced in *Ruby* and *Sapphire*, based on Kyushu.
- **Sinnoh** — Introduced in *Diamond* and *Pearl*, based on Hokkaido.
- **Unova** — Introduced in *Black* and *White*, based on New York City.
- **Kalos** — Introduced in *X* and *Y*, based on France.
- **Alola** — Introduced in *Sun* and *Moon*, based on Hawaii.
- **Galar** — Introduced in *Sword* and *Shield*, based on the United Kingdom.
- **Paldea** — Introduced in *Scarlet* and *Violet*, based on the Iberian Peninsula.

## Iconic Pokémon

- **Pikachu** — The Electric-type mascot of the franchise and partner to Ash Ketchum in the anime.
- **Charizard** — A Fire/Flying-type evolution of Charmander and a fan favorite since Generation I.
- **Mewtwo** — A Psychic-type Legendary cloned from Mew, central to the first Pokémon film.
- **Eevee** — A Normal-type Pokémon famous for its multiple evolutionary paths.
- **Lucario** — A Fighting/Steel-type known for its Aura abilities.

## Cultural impact

Pokémon is the highest-grossing media franchise of all time, with cumulative revenue exceeding $100 billion across games, merchandise, trading cards, anime, and films. Pikachu is one of the most recognizable fictional characters in the world and has served as an official mascot for Japanese tourism campaigns.
