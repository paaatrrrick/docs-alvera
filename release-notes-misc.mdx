Here is a quick rundown of what we shipped lately. We tries to keep this updated but sometime we forgets.

## Week of June 22, 2026

### New features

- **Newsletter digest controls.** Subscribers can now pick a digest cadence, set their time zone, preview the next send time, and pause or resume delivery from their preferences.
- **Comments on posts.** A new comments widget lets readers leave comments, sort them by newest, oldest, or top, and react with emoji including laugh and rocket. Each post shows a live comment count.
- **Site-wide and blog search.** A new search bar searches across blog posts and projects, with a dedicated client-side blog search and a searchable bookmarks page.
- **Command palette (Cmd-K).** A new command palette gives keyboard-first navigation, including a theme toggle command.
- **Dark mode.** Toggle between light and dark themes from anywhere on the site.
- **RSS, Atom, JSON, and per-tag feeds.** Subscribe to the blog in your reader of choice. Feed discovery links are exposed in the page head, and a feed index lists every available format.
- **Post enhancements.** Blog posts now include reading time estimates, a table of contents, social share buttons, view counters, and copy-to-clipboard on code blocks.
- **Project tag filtering.** Filter the projects list by tag to find work faster.

### Updates

- **Sitemap and robots.** The sitemap now includes `lastmod`, `changefreq`, and `priority`, and a generated `robots.txt` points crawlers to it.
- **Total site views counter.** A site-wide view count is now visible.
- **Back-to-top button.** A back-to-top control appears on long pages with scroll progress.
- **Keyboard shortcuts overlay.** Press `?` to see all available shortcuts.
- **Combined post meta line.** Post metadata is now displayed in a single, cleaner line.
- **Subscriber count badge.** The newsletter form now shows a live subscriber count.
- **Reaction summary.** Posts display a summary of all reactions at a glance.

### Bug fixes

- Newsletter signups now require double opt-in confirmation, preventing accidental subscriptions.
