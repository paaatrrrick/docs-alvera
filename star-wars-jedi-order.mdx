## Overview

The Jedi Order was an ancient monastic peacekeeping organization unified by its adherence to the light side of the Force. For over a thousand generations, the Jedi served the Galactic Republic as guardians of peace and justice, until their near-extinction during Order 66.

## The Jedi Code

At the heart of the Order lies the Jedi Code, a mantra that guides every member toward discipline and serenity:

- There is no emotion, there is peace.
- There is no ignorance, there is knowledge.
- There is no passion, there is serenity.
- There is no chaos, there is harmony.
- There is no death, there is the Force.

## Ranks within the Order

| Rank | Role |
| --- | --- |
| Youngling | A Force-sensitive child beginning their training |
| Padawan | An apprentice paired with a single Master |
| Knight | A Jedi who has passed the Trials |
| Master | A Knight recognized for wisdom and accomplishment |
| Grand Master | The most senior member, leader of the Order |

## The path of training

A Jedi's journey begins in childhood and unfolds over many years:

- **Younglings** train in clans at the Jedi Temple, learning the basics of the Force and lightsaber forms.
- **Padawans** are chosen by a Master and learn through direct apprenticeship in the field.
- **The Trials** test a Padawan's skill, courage, flesh, spirit, and insight before they are knighted.
- **Knighthood** grants independence to take on missions across the galaxy.

## The Jedi High Council

The Jedi High Council was a body of twelve senior Masters who governed the Order from the Temple on Coruscant. They made decisions on Jedi missions, the admission of trainees, and the Order's relationship with the Senate. Seats on the Council were among the highest honors a Jedi could attain.

## Legacy

Though the Order was nearly destroyed by the Empire, its teachings endured through survivors like Obi-Wan Kenobi and Yoda. Luke Skywalker carried the light side forward, ensuring that the Jedi—and the balance they sought—would rise again.
