## Overview

The Star Wars galaxy is full of distinctive foods and drinks that hint at the cultures, climates, and creatures of each planet. From moisture farms on Tatooine to the cantinas of the Outer Rim, cuisine is a quiet but constant part of the saga.

## Iconic foods

- **Blue milk (Bantha milk)** — A staple on Tatooine, harvested from banthas and famously served chilled to Luke Skywalker on the Lars homestead.
- **Green milk (Thala-siren milk)** — Drawn fresh from a thala-siren on Ahch-To, sampled by Luke in *The Last Jedi*.
- **Roasted porg** — Native seabirds of Ahch-To, cooked over an open flame by Chewbacca.
- **Nuna legs** — A common street food made from the flightless nuna bird, popular across the Outer Rim.
- **Felucian ground-squid jerky** — Spiced dried squid from the fungal jungles of Felucia.

## Iconic drinks

- **Jawa juice** — A fermented beverage of bantha hide and grains, brewed by the Jawas of Tatooine.
- **Spiced Corellian ale** — A warming drink favored by smugglers and traders along the Corellian Run.
- **Jet juice** — A high-proof spirit served in seedier cantinas, distilled from engine-grade alcohols (drink responsibly, you should not).
- **Ardees ("Jawa juice" variant)** — Obi-Wan Kenobi's preferred order at Dex's Diner on Coruscant.

## Famous dining spots

- **Mos Eisley Cantina (Tatooine)** — The Outer Rim's most notorious bar, off-limits to droids but welcoming to nearly every species.
- **Dex's Diner (Coruscant)** — A working-class diner in CoCo Town run by Dexter Jettster, known for sliders and straight talk.
- **Maz Kanata's castle (Takodana)** — A centuries-old gathering place for travelers, smugglers, and pirates.
- **Canto Bight casinos (Cantonica)** — High-end resorts where the galaxy's wealthiest dine on rare delicacies.

## Cultural notes

Food in Star Wars often signals allegiance and origin. Imperial officers favor refined Core World dishes, while Rebel pilots make do with ration packs between sorties. Jedi traditionally eat simply, reflecting their detachment from material excess, while Hutts indulge in living prey and elaborate feasts.
