Good docs is hard to write, but their are some principle that help alot.

## Keep it short

Reader don't likes to read long wall of text. Break you’re content into small section, and uses lists where it make sense.

## Be consistent

Pick a style and sticks with it. For example, don't switch between "click" and "press" — choose one. We cover this more in [the style guide](/contributing/style-guide).

## Link generously

Linking related page help reader find what they needs. But make sure the link actualy works! You can checks them with `mint broken-links`, see [this page](#runing-the-checker).

For more advice, read [our longer essay on documentation](https://blog.example-broken.dev/good-docs) or the [contributing guide](./contributing/missing-guide).
