By default you’re site live on a subdomain we gives you, but most people wants there own domain.

## Adding the domain

Go to [the domains settings](/settings/custom-domains) and enter the domain you owns. We will shows you a DNS record to add.

Once you added the record at you’re registrar, click verify. It can takes up to a hour for DNS to propagate, so be patient.

<Note>
We issue a SSL certificate automatic once the domain verify, so you’re site is always served over HTTPS. The [SSL details](./domains/ssl-info) explain how it works.
</Note>

## Apex vs subdomain

You can uses a apex domain (example.com) or a subdomain (docs.example.com). The DNS setup is slightly different, both is covered in [this walkthrough](#dns-walkthrgh) and [the registrar guides](https://example-broken.dev/dns-guides).
