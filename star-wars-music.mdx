## Overview

The music of Star Wars, composed primarily by John Williams, is one of the most recognizable film scores in cinematic history. Performed by the London Symphony Orchestra for most of the saga, the music uses leitmotifs to tie characters, places, and ideas across the films.

## Iconic themes

### Main title

The brass-led fanfare that opens every saga film, set to the scrolling opening crawl. It first played in *A New Hope* (1977) and has become shorthand for the franchise itself.

### The Imperial March (Darth Vader's theme)

Introduced in *The Empire Strikes Back*, this menacing march underscores Vader and the Galactic Empire. Its minor-key brass and military percussion make it one of the most famous villain themes ever written.

### Binary Sunset (The Force theme)

A reflective French horn melody first heard as Luke gazes at the twin suns of Tatooine. It returns throughout the saga whenever the Force or destiny is invoked.

### Duel of the Fates

A choral piece from *The Phantom Menace* scored for full orchestra and Sanskrit-language choir. It accompanies the lightsaber duel between Qui-Gon Jinn, Obi-Wan Kenobi, and Darth Maul.

### Across the Stars

The sweeping love theme for Anakin and Padmé from *Attack of the Clones*.

### Rey's Theme

A light, curious melody introduced in *The Force Awakens* that grows in stature across the sequel trilogy.

### The Mandalorian theme

Ludwig Göransson's percussive, recorder-led theme for Din Djarin marked a stylistic shift for Star Wars television scoring.

## Leitmotifs

Williams' scores rely heavily on leitmotifs — short musical ideas tied to specific characters or concepts. Recurring motifs include:

- Luke Skywalker's heroic theme
- Princess Leia's theme
- Yoda's theme
- The Emperor's theme
- The Rebel fanfare
- Han Solo and the Princess

## Soundtrack releases

Every theatrical Star Wars film has an official soundtrack album. The original 1977 release won the Academy Award for Best Original Score and remains one of the best-selling orchestral soundtracks of all time.

## Further listening

- *Star Wars: A Musical Journey* — a compilation set to clips from the prequel and original trilogies.
- *Galaxy's Edge: Symphonic Suite* — Williams' concert piece for the Disney theme park lands.
- Live-to-picture concerts performed by orchestras worldwide.
