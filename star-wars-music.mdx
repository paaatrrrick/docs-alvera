## Overview

The Star Wars soundtrack, composed primarily by John Williams, is one of the most iconic and influential film scores in cinema history. The sweeping orchestral music helps define the emotional and mythological tone of the galaxy far, far away.

## John Williams

John Williams composed the scores for all nine films in the Skywalker saga, spanning four decades. His use of leitmotif — recurring musical themes tied to characters and ideas — gives the saga its distinctive sound.

## Iconic themes

## Beyond the Skywalker saga

Other composers have contributed to the wider Star Wars universe:

- **Michael Giacchino** — *Rogue One: A Star Wars Story*
- **John Powell** — *Solo: A Star Wars Story*
- **Ludwig Göransson** — *The Mandalorian*, *The Book of Boba Fett*
- **Nicholas Britell** — *Andor*
- **Kevin Kiner** — *The Clone Wars*, *Rebels*, *Ahsoka*

## Legacy

The Star Wars score has earned multiple Academy Award nominations and is regularly performed by major orchestras worldwide. The Main Title and Imperial March, in particular, have become cultural touchstones recognized far beyond fans of the franchise.
