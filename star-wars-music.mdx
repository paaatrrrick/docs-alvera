## Overview

The Star Wars soundtrack, composed primarily by John Williams, is one of the most recognizable bodies of film music in cinema history. The scores blend late-Romantic orchestration with leitmotifs that follow characters, factions, and ideas across the saga.

## Iconic themes

- **Main title** — the brassy fanfare that opens every film
- **Imperial march** — Darth Vader's relentless leitmotif from *The Empire Strikes Back*
- **Binary sunset (the Force theme)** — the soaring melody tied to Luke and the Force
- **Duel of the Fates** — choral piece from *The Phantom Menace*
- **Princess Leia's theme** — lyrical woodwind melody introduced in *A New Hope*
- **Across the stars** — Anakin and Padmé's love theme from *Attack of the Clones*
- **Rey's theme** — introduced in *The Force Awakens*
- **The Mandalorian theme** — Göransson's recorder-and-bass riff for Din Djarin

## Leitmotif technique

Williams uses leitmotifs in the Wagnerian tradition, assigning short musical ideas to characters, places, and concepts. Themes evolve as the story develops — Luke's heroic theme is reharmonized in darker moments, and Kylo Ren's theme echoes fragments of the Imperial march to mark his lineage.

## Recordings and performance

- The original trilogy was recorded by the London Symphony Orchestra at Anvil Studios and Abbey Road.
- The prequels and sequels were also recorded with the LSO, with Williams conducting.
- Live-to-picture concerts of the films are performed worldwide by major orchestras.
