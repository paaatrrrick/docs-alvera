## Overview

The music of Star Wars is as iconic as the films themselves. Composed primarily by John Williams, the scores blend symphonic grandeur with recurring leitmotifs that give characters, factions, and ideas their own musical identity.

## John Williams and the Skywalker saga

John Williams composed the scores for all nine films in the Skywalker saga, spanning more than four decades:

- **Original trilogy (1977-1983)**: Established the sweeping symphonic sound that defines the franchise.
- **Prequel trilogy (1999-2005)**: Introduced darker, choral-heavy pieces like *Duel of the Fates*.
- **Sequel trilogy (2015-2019)**: Reintroduced classic themes while adding new motifs for Rey, Kylo Ren, and the Resistance.

Williams received Academy Award nominations for each trilogy's first installment and won the Oscar for *A New Hope* in 1978.

## Other composers in the galaxy

While John Williams defined the saga's sound, other composers have contributed to the wider franchise:

- **Michael Giacchino**: Scored *Rogue One: A Star Wars Story* (2016), weaving in Williams' themes.
- **John Powell**: Composed *Solo: A Star Wars Story* (2018), with a main theme by Williams.
- **Ludwig Göransson**: Created the distinctive sound of *The Mandalorian*, blending synths with traditional instruments.
- **Kevin Kiner**: Scored animated series including *The Clone Wars*, *Rebels*, and *Ahsoka*.
- **Nicholas Britell**: Composed the moody, jazz-influenced score for *Andor*.

## Cultural impact

The Star Wars main title is one of the most recognizable pieces of film music ever written. The American Film Institute ranked it #1 on its list of the greatest film scores of all time. Themes like *The Imperial March* have transcended the films, appearing in everything from sporting events to political commentary.
