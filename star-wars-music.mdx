## Overview

The Star Wars score is one of the most recognizable in film history. Composed primarily by John Williams, the music uses sweeping orchestral arrangements and recurring leitmotifs to give each character, faction, and idea its own sonic identity.

## John Williams and the main themes

John Williams scored all nine films of the Skywalker saga. His work established the symphonic style that defines the franchise.

| Theme | Introduced in | Associated with |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Main Title | *A New Hope* (1977) | The saga itself |
| The Force Theme | *A New Hope* (1977) | The Force, Obi-Wan, Luke |
| The Imperial March | *The Empire Strikes Back* (1980) | Darth Vader, the Empire |
| Yoda's Theme | *The Empire Strikes Back* (1980) | Yoda and Jedi wisdom |
| Duel of the Fates | *The Phantom Menace* (1999) | Lightsaber confrontations |
| Across the Stars | *Attack of the Clones* (2002) | Anakin and Padmé |
| Rey's Theme | *The Force Awakens* (2015) | Rey |

## Leitmotifs

Williams uses leitmotifs — short musical phrases tied to specific characters or ideas — so themes evolve across films. The Force theme, for example, returns whenever heroism or destiny is at stake, while The Imperial March signals Vader's presence even when he is offscreen.

## Other composers

While Williams scored the main saga, other composers expanded the musical universe:

- **Michael Giacchino** — *Rogue One: A Star Wars Story*
- **John Powell** — *Solo: A Star Wars Story* (with themes by Williams)
- **Ludwig Göransson** — *The Mandalorian*, *The Book of Boba Fett*
- **Nicholas Britell** — *Andor*
- **Kevin Kiner** — *The Clone Wars*, *Rebels*, *Ahsoka*

## Legacy

The Star Wars score has been performed by orchestras worldwide and is frequently cited as a defining example of modern film music. Williams' work earned multiple Academy Award nominations and helped revive the symphonic film score as a Hollywood standard.
