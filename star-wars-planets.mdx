## Overview

The Star Wars galaxy is home to thousands of worlds, each with its own climate, culture, and place in the saga. This page highlights the planets that define the franchise.

## Core worlds

### Coruscant

The galactic capital and a true ecumenopolis, Coruscant is covered entirely by a single sprawling city. It serves as the seat of the Galactic Republic, the Empire, and later the New Republic.

### Alderaan

A peaceful, mountainous world and home of the royal family of House Organa. Alderaan was destroyed by the Death Star in *A New Hope*.

## Outer Rim worlds

### Tatooine

A harsh desert planet orbiting twin suns. Tatooine is the childhood home of both Anakin and Luke Skywalker and a haven for smugglers, moisture farmers, and Hutts.

### Hoth

A remote ice world that hosted the Rebel Alliance's Echo Base. Hoth is best known for the Battle of Hoth in *The Empire Strikes Back*.

### Dagobah

A swampy, Force-rich planet where Jedi Master Yoda lived in exile and trained Luke Skywalker.

### Endor

A forest moon inhabited by the Ewoks. The second Death Star was destroyed in orbit above Endor in *Return of the Jedi*.

## Prequel-era worlds

### Naboo

A lush world of rolling hills, lakes, and classical architecture. Naboo is home to Padmé Amidala and the underwater Gungan civilization.

### Kamino

A stormy ocean planet where the clone army of the Republic was secretly created.

### Mustafar

A volcanic world of lava rivers and ash, the site of the duel between Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker.

## Sequel-era worlds

### Jakku

A desert scavenger world where Rey grew up among the wreckage of Imperial warships.

### Ahch-To

A remote ocean planet dotted with rocky islands, home to the first Jedi temple and Luke Skywalker's self-imposed exile.
