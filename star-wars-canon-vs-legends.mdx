## Overview

Star Wars storytelling is organized into two parallel continuities: the current **canon** maintained by Lucasfilm, and **Legends**, the former Expanded Universe rebranded in 2014.

## Canon

Canon includes every story Lucasfilm considers part of the official Star Wars timeline. It is overseen by the Lucasfilm Story Group to keep films, series, novels, comics, and games consistent.

- The Skywalker Saga (Episodes I-IX)
- Standalone films like *Rogue One* and *Solo*
- Live-action series such as *The Mandalorian*, *Andor*, *Ahsoka*, and *The Acolyte*
- Animated series including *The Clone Wars*, *Rebels*, and *The Bad Batch*
- Post-2014 novels, comics, and reference books

## Legends

Legends covers the pre-2014 Expanded Universe. These stories are no longer official continuity, but remain in print under the Star Wars Legends banner.

- The Thrawn trilogy by Timothy Zahn
- The *Knights of the Old Republic* games and comics
- The *Legacy* comics era
- The original *Clone Wars* multimedia project (2003-2005)

## Why the split exists

When Disney acquired Lucasfilm in 2012, the company wanted a unified continuity for new films and shows without being bound by decades of Expanded Universe material. The 2014 reset created a clear canvas for the sequel trilogy and beyond.

## Crossovers

Several Legends concepts have been re-canonized through new appearances:

| Element | Origin (Legends) | Re-canonized in |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Grand Admiral Thrawn | *Heir to the Empire* (1991) | *Star Wars Rebels* |
| Captain Rex (older) | *The Clone Wars* | *Star Wars Rebels*, *Ahsoka* |
| The Inquisitors | *The Force Unleashed* | *Rebels*, *Obi-Wan Kenobi* |

## Choosing where to start

- For the official ongoing story, follow canon films and series in release order.
- For deep alternate-history lore, explore Legends novels and games — start with the Thrawn trilogy or *Knights of the Old Republic*.
