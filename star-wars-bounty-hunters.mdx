## Overview

Bounty hunters are mercenaries who track down targets across the galaxy in exchange for credits. They operate outside formal allegiances, taking contracts from crime syndicates, the Empire, the Hutts, and anyone willing to pay.

## The Bounty Hunters' Code

Members of the Bounty Hunters' Guild follow an unwritten code that governs how hunters interact, share information, and handle contracts. Key tenets include:

- Never interfere with another hunter's quarry.
- A bounty is to be brought in by the hunter who claimed it.
- Disputes are settled through the Guild, not in the field.

## Tools of the trade

Bounty hunters rely on specialized equipment to track and capture targets:

- **Jetpacks**: Mandalorian-style jetpacks allow rapid pursuit and aerial combat.
- **Carbonite freezing**: Used to transport live captures without risk of escape.
- **Tracking fobs**: Standard-issue Guild devices that home in on a target's signal.
- **Whipcord launchers**: Wrist-mounted tools for restraining targets at a distance.
- **Customized blasters**: Often modified for stun, slug, or disruptor fire.

## The underworld

Bounty hunters frequently work for the galaxy's crime lords. Major employers include:

- **Jabba the Hutt**: The Tatooine crime lord who placed the famous bounty on Han Solo.
- **The Pyke Syndicate**: Spice traffickers operating across the Outer Rim.
- **The Crimson Dawn**: A shadowy syndicate led by Dryden Vos and later Qi'ra.
- **The Hutt Cartel**: A loose alliance of Hutt clans controlling Outer Rim trade.

## Mandalorian influence

Many of the galaxy's most feared hunters draw on Mandalorian traditions, wearing beskar armor and adhering to the Way. The Mandalorian culture and bounty hunting are deeply intertwined, as seen with Jango Fett, Boba Fett, and Din Djarin.
