## Overview

The Sith were an ancient order of Force-sensitives who embraced the dark side as the path to ultimate power. Where the Jedi sought peace through serenity, the Sith pursued strength through passion, and waged a millennia-long shadow war to remake the galaxy in their image.

## The Sith Code

The Sith Code stands as the philosophical mirror of the Jedi Code, exalting emotion, conflict, and personal power.

> Peace is a lie, there is only Passion.
> Through Passion, I gain Strength.
> Through Strength, I gain Power.
> Through Power, I gain Victory.
> Through Victory, my chains are broken.
> The Force shall free me.

## The Rule of Two

After the Sith Order nearly destroyed itself through internal warfare, Darth Bane instituted the Rule of Two: there would be only two Sith at any time — a Master to embody power, and an apprentice to crave it. This doctrine kept the Sith hidden from the Jedi for a thousand years while they plotted their return.

## Ranks and titles

- **Dark Lord of the Sith** — the supreme Master of the Order.
- **Sith Master** — a fully realized practitioner who has taken an apprentice.
- **Sith Apprentice** — a learner training to one day overthrow their Master.
- **Sith Acolyte** — an initiate not yet bound to the Rule of Two lineage.

## The dark side of the Force

The dark side draws on raw emotion — anger, fear, hatred, and ambition. It grants quicker, easier power than the light, but it corrupts the body and mind. Sith abilities include Force lightning, mind domination, life drain, and the rare practice of essence transfer to cheat death.

## Weapons of the Sith

Sith traditionally wield red-bladed lightsabers, their crystals "bled" through focused rage until they crystallize into synthetic kyber. Variants include the curved-hilt sabers favored by Count Dooku and the double-bladed staff wielded by Darth Maul.

## Legacy

Though the Sith were broken at the Battle of Endor, their teachings, holocrons, and acolytes endured. The dark side, like the light, can never truly be extinguished — only balanced.
