## Overview

The Sith are an ancient order of Force-sensitives who draw power from the dark side. Formed millennia before the Skywalker saga, the Sith stand in direct opposition to the Jedi Order, channeling passion, anger, and ambition into raw power.

## The Sith Code

Where the Jedi seek serenity and balance, the Sith embrace conflict as a path to strength:

> Peace is a lie, there is only passion.
> Through passion, I gain strength.
> Through strength, I gain power.
> Through power, I gain victory.
> Through victory, my chains are broken.
> The Force shall free me.

## The Rule of Two

After centuries of self-destructive infighting, Darth Bane reformed the order around the Rule of Two: at any time, only two Sith may exist — a master to embody power and an apprentice to crave it. The rule preserved the order in secrecy for a thousand years, culminating in the rise of Darth Sidious.

## Notable Sith Lords

| Sith | Era | Known for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Darth Bane | Old Republic | Founder of the Rule of Two |
| Darth Plagueis | Pre-prequels | Master of life and death; mentor to Sidious |
| Darth Sidious (Palpatine) | Prequels–Sequels | Engineered the fall of the Republic |
| Darth Maul | Prequels | Sidious's first known apprentice; double-bladed lightsaber |
| Darth Tyranus (Count Dooku) | Clone Wars | Former Jedi turned Separatist leader |
| Darth Vader (Anakin Skywalker) | Original trilogy | The Chosen One turned and redeemed |

## Sith weapons and tools

- **Red lightsabers** — bled kyber crystals, corrupted through the dark side.
- **Sith holocrons** — pyramid-shaped artifacts storing forbidden knowledge.
- **Sith alchemy** — rituals to twist matter, beasts, and even spirits.

## Legacy

Though Vader's sacrifice in *Return of the Jedi* destroyed the Rule of Two lineage, the dark side endured. Palpatine's return in *The Rise of Skywalker* and the cult-like Sith Eternal on Exegol show that the Sith ideology outlives any single master.
