## Overview

Each main-series Pokémon game is set in a region inspired by a real-world location. Regions introduce new Pokémon, Gym Leaders, an Elite Four (or alternative challenge), and a Legendary mascot.

## Real-world inspirations

- **Kanto** — Kantō region of Japan
- **Johto** — Kansai region of Japan
- **Hoenn** — Kyūshū, rotated
- **Sinnoh** — Hokkaidō
- **Unova** — New York metropolitan area
- **Kalos** — France
- **Alola** — Hawaii
- **Galar** — United Kingdom
- **Paldea** — Iberian Peninsula

## Regional variants

Starting in Gen VII, certain Pokémon take on regional forms with new typings and looks:

- **Alolan forms** — Includes Alolan Vulpix (Ice) and Alolan Exeggutor (Grass/Dragon).
- **Galarian forms** — Includes Galarian Ponyta (Psychic) and Galarian Zigzagoon (Dark/Normal).
- **Hisuian forms** — Introduced in Pokémon Legends: Arceus, set in ancient Sinnoh.
- **Paldean forms** — Includes Paldean Tauros and Paldean Wooper (Poison/Ground).
