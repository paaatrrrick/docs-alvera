## Summary

During a long fog season, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. The pressure readings from the Ashshore pumping station drifted upward for 7 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. The night crew at Ashshore logged 4 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them.

## Timeline

- A routine recalibration at Tamvale was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1915.
- Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with fired clay, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1801 that nobody documented.
- The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 5 times over 71 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes.
- At roughly 7:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Ashshore stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact.

## Investigation

Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 15 days earlier. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with whale-bone veneer, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1903 that nobody documented. Yutta Kessler traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Tamvale line, subtly out of tolerance. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 3 times over 67 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes.

## Root cause

Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with blue slate, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1866 that nobody documented. Yutta Kessler traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Tamvale line, subtly out of tolerance. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 7 times over 36 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 13 days earlier.

## Lessons and follow-up

We are adopting a rule from the Ashshore yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Folke Marchetti has advocated since 1844. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal.
