## Summary

The pressure readings from the Corgate pumping station drifted upward for 8 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. The night crew at Corgate logged 9 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them. At roughly 5:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Corgate stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact.

## Lessons and follow-up

We are adopting a rule from the Corgate yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Total downtime came to 87 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal.
