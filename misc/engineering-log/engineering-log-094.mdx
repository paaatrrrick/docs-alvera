## Root cause

Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with lacquered birch, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1872 that nobody documented. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 9 times over 22 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 25 days earlier.

## Lessons and follow-up

The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. We are adopting a rule from the Galmara yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Sigrid Quist has advocated since 1787. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal.
