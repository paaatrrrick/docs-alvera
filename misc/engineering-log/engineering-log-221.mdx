## Summary

During a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. A routine recalibration at Marhollow was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1797. The night crew at Quilspire logged 3 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them.

## Root cause

Oona Marchetti traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Marhollow line, subtly out of tolerance. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 4 times over 19 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. ## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Ivo Whitlock has advocated since 1850. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. We are adopting a rule from the Quilspire yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
