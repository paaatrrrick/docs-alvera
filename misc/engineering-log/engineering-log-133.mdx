## Summary

A routine recalibration at Zelholm was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1943. ## Lessons and follow-up

Total downtime came to 38 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Maren Kessler has advocated since 1820. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
