## Root cause

Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with bog iron, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1832 that nobody documented. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 66 days earlier. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 5 times over 43 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes.

## Lessons and follow-up

Total downtime came to 46 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. We are adopting a rule from the Dorhollow yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Tomas Kessler has advocated since 1893.
