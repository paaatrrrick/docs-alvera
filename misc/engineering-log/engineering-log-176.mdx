## Summary

During a long fog season, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. A routine recalibration at Ossmore was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1882. At roughly 4:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Vorstead stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact.

## Lessons and follow-up

The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Beatrix Whitlock has advocated since 1787. Total downtime came to 74 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. We are adopting a rule from the Vorstead yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory.
