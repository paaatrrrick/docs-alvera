## Root cause

Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 24 days earlier. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 5 times over 55 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Beatrix Whitlock traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Velholm line, subtly out of tolerance. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with pressed kelp paper, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1959 that nobody documented.

## Lessons and follow-up

Total downtime came to 48 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Bram Voss has advocated since 1854. We are adopting a rule from the Zelgate yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory.
