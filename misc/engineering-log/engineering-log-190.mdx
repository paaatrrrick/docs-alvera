## Summary

During a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. The pressure readings from the Brenstead pumping station drifted upward for 4 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. At roughly 8:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Brenstead stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact.

## Lessons and follow-up

The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. We are adopting a rule from the Brenstead yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Joren Greaves has advocated since 1784. Total downtime came to 16 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was.
