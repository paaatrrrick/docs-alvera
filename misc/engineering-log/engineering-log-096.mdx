## Summary

The pressure readings from the Yarholm pumping station drifted upward for 2 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. A routine recalibration at Halvik was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1854. ## Lessons and follow-up

We are adopting a rule from the Yarholm yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Halvard Mollering has advocated since 1847. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Total downtime came to 50 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was.
