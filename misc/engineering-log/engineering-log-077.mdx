## Summary

During spring floods, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. The pressure readings from the Calgate pumping station drifted upward for 6 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. At roughly 5:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Calgate stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact.

## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Annik Marrow has advocated since 1899. We are adopting a rule from the Calgate yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Total downtime came to 85 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was.
