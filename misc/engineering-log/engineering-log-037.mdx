The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 4 times over 53 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. ## Root cause

Tomas Ashdown traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Halrun line, subtly out of tolerance. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. ## Lessons and follow-up

The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Total downtime came to 21 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. We are adopting a rule from the Dormore yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory.
