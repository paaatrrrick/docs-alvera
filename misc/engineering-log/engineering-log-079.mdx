## Summary

During the dry easterlies, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. The night crew at Wrenrun logged 9 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them. A routine recalibration at Marmore was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1964.

## Lessons and follow-up

The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Total downtime came to 53 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Sigrid Holloway has advocated since 1891.
