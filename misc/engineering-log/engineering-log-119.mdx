## Summary

A routine recalibration at Dorfell was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1898. During a still and windless month, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. The pressure readings from the Marmore pumping station drifted upward for 4 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself.

The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 2 times over 27 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. ## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Emeric Lindqvist has advocated since 1872. We are adopting a rule from the Marmore yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
