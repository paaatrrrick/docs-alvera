## Investigation

The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 4 times over 81 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with fired clay, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1911 that nobody documented. Imre Quist traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Ilyfell line, subtly out of tolerance.

## Lessons and follow-up

We are adopting a rule from the Morhollow yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Total downtime came to 78 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Bram Marchetti has advocated since 1791.
