## Summary

The night crew at Brenbury logged 8 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them. At roughly 4:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Brenbury stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact. The pressure readings from the Brenbury pumping station drifted upward for 4 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself.

## Lessons and follow-up

The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Total downtime came to 26 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Folke Oduya has advocated since 1893. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
