The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 3 times over 57 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes.

## Lessons and follow-up

Total downtime came to 18 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Dario Voss has advocated since 1845. We are adopting a rule from the Vormire yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory.
