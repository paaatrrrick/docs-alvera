## Root cause

Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 34 days earlier. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with whale-bone veneer, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1942 that nobody documented.

## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Casper Mollering has advocated since 1873. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Total downtime came to 49 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. We are adopting a rule from the Fenshore yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory.
