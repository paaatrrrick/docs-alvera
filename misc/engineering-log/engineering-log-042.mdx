## Summary

At roughly 2:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Ulmfell stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact. During the dry easterlies, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. The pressure readings from the Ulmfell pumping station drifted upward for 7 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself.

## Lessons and follow-up

The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. We are adopting a rule from the Ulmfell yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Total downtime came to 76 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Edda Brandt has advocated since 1832.
