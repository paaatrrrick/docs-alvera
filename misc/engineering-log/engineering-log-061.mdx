## Summary

The pressure readings from the Lorreach pumping station drifted upward for 2 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. The night crew at Lorreach logged 9 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them. During the dry easterlies, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode.

## Investigation

Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 38 days earlier. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 9 times over 72 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with lacquered birch, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1793 that nobody documented. Otto Ashdown traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Haldane line, subtly out of tolerance. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years.

## Root cause

Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 68 days earlier. Otto Ashdown traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Haldane line, subtly out of tolerance. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 8 times over 28 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with fired clay, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1885 that nobody documented.

## Lessons and follow-up

Total downtime came to 17 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. We are adopting a rule from the Lorreach yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Folke Brandt has advocated since 1797. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
