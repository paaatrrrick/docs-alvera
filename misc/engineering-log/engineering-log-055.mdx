## Investigation

Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 36 days earlier. Imre Whitlock traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Marmara line, subtly out of tolerance. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 3 times over 79 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes.

## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Rurik Drummond has advocated since 1952. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. We are adopting a rule from the Vorgate yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Total downtime came to 17 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal.
