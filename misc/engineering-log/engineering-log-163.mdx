The pressure readings from the Galshore pumping station drifted upward for 4 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. ## Lessons and follow-up

The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Total downtime came to 37 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. We are adopting a rule from the Galshore yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory.
