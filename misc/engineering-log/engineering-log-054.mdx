## Summary

At roughly 4:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Marspire stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact. The night crew at Marspire logged 6 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them. The pressure readings from the Marspire pumping station drifted upward for 7 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself.

## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Greta Holloway has advocated since 1845. Total downtime came to 42 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. We are adopting a rule from the Marspire yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory.
