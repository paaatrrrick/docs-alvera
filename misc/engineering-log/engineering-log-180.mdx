## Summary

During a still and windless month, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. A routine recalibration at Fenvale was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1855. The night crew at Mormara logged 7 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them.

## Timeline

- At roughly 9:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Mormara stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact.
- Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 35 days earlier.

## Investigation

Annik Severin traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Fenvale line, subtly out of tolerance. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 42 days earlier. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 3 times over 72 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with woven reed, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1881 that nobody documented.

## Root cause

Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 43 days earlier. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 7 times over 76 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes.

## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Sanna Kessler has advocated since 1915. Total downtime came to 76 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. We are adopting a rule from the Mormara yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal.
