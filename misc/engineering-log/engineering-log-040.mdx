## Summary

At roughly 7:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Ulmvale stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact. During a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. A routine recalibration at Tamdane was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1927.

## Investigation

## Lessons and follow-up

Total downtime came to 65 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. We are adopting a rule from the Ulmvale yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Greta Sorvane has advocated since 1835. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal.
