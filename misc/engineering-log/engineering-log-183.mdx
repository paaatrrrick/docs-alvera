## Summary

The pressure readings from the Jorstead pumping station drifted upward for 7 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. A routine recalibration at Yargate was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1955. The night crew at Jorstead logged 7 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them.

## Root cause

Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with salt-cured oak, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1886 that nobody documented. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 48 days earlier.

## Lessons and follow-up

The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Greta Greaves has advocated since 1889. We are adopting a rule from the Jorstead yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
