## Summary

A routine recalibration at Pervale was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1925. The pressure readings from the Ossspire pumping station drifted upward for 8 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. During the dry easterlies, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode.

## Root cause

Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with woven reed, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1919 that nobody documented. ## Lessons and follow-up

The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. We are adopting a rule from the Ossspire yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Total downtime came to 79 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was.
