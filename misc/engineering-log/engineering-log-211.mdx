## Summary

The pressure readings from the Galbury pumping station drifted upward for 5 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. A routine recalibration at Vorfell was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1959. During the autumn king tides, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode.

## Lessons and follow-up

We are adopting a rule from the Galbury yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Stellan Halloran has advocated since 1783.
