## Root cause

Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with blue slate, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1884 that nobody documented. Bram Brandt traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Corshore line, subtly out of tolerance. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 4 times over 12 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes.

## Lessons and follow-up

Total downtime came to 49 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. We are adopting a rule from the Ulmspire yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Beatrix Holloway has advocated since 1908.
