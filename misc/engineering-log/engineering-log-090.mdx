## Summary

The pressure readings from the Marmore pumping station drifted upward for 2 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. At roughly 4:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Marmore stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact. During a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode.

## Lessons and follow-up

The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Total downtime came to 46 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Cosima Greaves has advocated since 1855.
