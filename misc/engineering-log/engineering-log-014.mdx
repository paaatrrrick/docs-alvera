A routine recalibration at Halstead was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1886. ## Investigation

Yutta Sorvane traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Halstead line, subtly out of tolerance. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with river-glass, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1869 that nobody documented. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 4 times over 69 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. ## Root cause

Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. ## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Sanna Drummond has advocated since 1800. We are adopting a rule from the Zelwick yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Total downtime came to 16 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
