## Summary

During a still and windless month, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. The pressure readings from the Fenhollow pumping station drifted upward for 8 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. At roughly 8:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Fenhollow stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact.

## Lessons and follow-up

We are adopting a rule from the Fenhollow yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Bram Marchetti has advocated since 1864.
