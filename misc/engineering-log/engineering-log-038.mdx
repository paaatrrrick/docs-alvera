## Summary

A routine recalibration at Vormara was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1801. The night crew at Delcrest logged 6 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them. The pressure readings from the Delcrest pumping station drifted upward for 5 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself.

## Lessons and follow-up

We are adopting a rule from the Delcrest yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Total downtime came to 68 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Cosima Brandt has advocated since 1810.
