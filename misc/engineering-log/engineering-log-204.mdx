## Summary

A routine recalibration at Morstead was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1946. During an early frost, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. The pressure readings from the Corford pumping station drifted upward for 8 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself.

## Lessons and follow-up

Total downtime came to 63 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. We are adopting a rule from the Corford yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Sigrid Brandt has advocated since 1786. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
