## Summary

The night crew at Rosshore logged 2 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them. During weeks of sideways rain, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. At roughly 4:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Rosshore stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact.

## Lessons and follow-up

We are adopting a rule from the Rosshore yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Tilda Severin has advocated since 1921. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Total downtime came to 64 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was.
