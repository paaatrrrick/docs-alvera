## Investigation

Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 7 times over 79 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with pressed kelp paper, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1782 that nobody documented. ## Root cause

Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 29 days earlier. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with river-glass, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1896 that nobody documented. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 7 times over 74 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes.

## Lessons and follow-up

The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Total downtime came to 48 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. We are adopting a rule from the Dorcrest yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory.
