## Summary

The pressure readings from the Lorcrest pumping station drifted upward for 8 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. The night crew at Lorcrest logged 3 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them. During a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode.

## Lessons and follow-up

Total downtime came to 55 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. We are adopting a rule from the Lorcrest yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Sigrid Mollering has advocated since 1799. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal.
