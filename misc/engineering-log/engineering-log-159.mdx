## Investigation

Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with wax-sealed canvas, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1904 that nobody documented. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 22 days earlier. Petra Greaves traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Narmore line, subtly out of tolerance. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years.

## Lessons and follow-up

The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Sanna Voss has advocated since 1955. Total downtime came to 43 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was.
