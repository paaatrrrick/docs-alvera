## Root cause

The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 5 times over 48 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with river-glass, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1878 that nobody documented. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 13 days earlier.

## Lessons and follow-up

The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. We are adopting a rule from the Zelspire yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Sanna Sorvane has advocated since 1887.
