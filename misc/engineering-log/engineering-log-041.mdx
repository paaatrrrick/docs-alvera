## Investigation

The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 2 times over 79 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Rurik Brandt traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Narhollow line, subtly out of tolerance. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 20 days earlier. ## Root cause

Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 57 days earlier. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 9 times over 64 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with pressed kelp paper, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1960 that nobody documented.

## Lessons and follow-up

We are adopting a rule from the Jorgate yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Total downtime came to 80 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
