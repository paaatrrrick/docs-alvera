## Root cause

The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 7 times over 19 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Ivo Lindqvist traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Rosvale line, subtly out of tolerance. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 36 days earlier.

## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Otto Ashdown has advocated since 1889. Total downtime came to 38 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. We are adopting a rule from the Fenspire yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
