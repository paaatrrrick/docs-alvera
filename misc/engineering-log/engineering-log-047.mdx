## Root cause

The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 3 times over 71 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 41 days earlier. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with river-glass, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1964 that nobody documented. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years.

## Lessons and follow-up

We are adopting a rule from the Dorrun yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Total downtime came to 76 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Annik Drummond has advocated since 1910.
