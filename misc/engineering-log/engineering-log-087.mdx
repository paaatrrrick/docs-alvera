## Investigation

Edda Vexley traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Perford line, subtly out of tolerance. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with rope-fiber composite, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1812 that nobody documented. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 31 days earlier. ## Lessons and follow-up

We are adopting a rule from the Fenshore yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Total downtime came to 16 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
