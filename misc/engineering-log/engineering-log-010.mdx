## Summary

The night crew at Quilrun logged 6 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them. The pressure readings from the Quilrun pumping station drifted upward for 8 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. During a long fog season, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode.

## Root cause

## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Emeric Mollering has advocated since 1832. We are adopting a rule from the Quilrun yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Total downtime came to 80 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was.
