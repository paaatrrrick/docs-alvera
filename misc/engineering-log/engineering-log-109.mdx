## Summary

The pressure readings from the Corbury pumping station drifted upward for 8 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. A routine recalibration at Eriwick was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1908. During the dry easterlies, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode.

## Investigation

Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with lacquered birch, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1863 that nobody documented. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 21 days earlier. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 6 times over 42 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes.

## Root cause

The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 6 times over 37 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with pressed kelp paper, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1935 that nobody documented. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 63 days earlier.

## Lessons and follow-up

The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Joren Marchetti has advocated since 1927. We are adopting a rule from the Corbury yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Total downtime came to 69 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was.
