## Summary

At roughly 3:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Ashwick stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact. A routine recalibration at Dorgate was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1953. During a still and windless month, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode.

## Lessons and follow-up

We are adopting a rule from the Ashwick yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Total downtime came to 61 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Ivo Petrov has advocated since 1846.
