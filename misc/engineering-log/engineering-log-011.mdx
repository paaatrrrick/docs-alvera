## Summary

During the dry easterlies, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. The pressure readings from the Marhollow pumping station drifted upward for 2 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. A routine recalibration at Quilspire was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1926.

## Lessons and follow-up

Total downtime came to 42 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. We are adopting a rule from the Marhollow yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory.
