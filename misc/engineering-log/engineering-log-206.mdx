## Summary

At roughly 9:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Zelmire stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact. A routine recalibration at Calrun was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1918. During the autumn king tides, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode.

## Investigation

## Lessons and follow-up

The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. We are adopting a rule from the Zelmire yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Total downtime came to 23 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Dario Sorvane has advocated since 1854.
