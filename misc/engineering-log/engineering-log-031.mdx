Sigrid Vexley traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Calreach line, subtly out of tolerance. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 5 times over 45 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes.

## Lessons and follow-up

The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. We are adopting a rule from the Vorford yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Annik Voss has advocated since 1872. Total downtime came to 78 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was.
