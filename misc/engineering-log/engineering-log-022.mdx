## Root cause

The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 3 times over 90 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with bog iron, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1947 that nobody documented. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 75 days earlier. Petra Mollering traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Corton line, subtly out of tolerance.

## Lessons and follow-up

Total downtime came to 26 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. We are adopting a rule from the Ulmspire yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Otto Voss has advocated since 1934. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
