## Summary

The pressure readings from the Wrenstead pumping station drifted upward for 5 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. A routine recalibration at Erigate was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1833. The night crew at Wrenstead logged 7 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them.

## Lessons and follow-up

The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Sigrid Sorvane has advocated since 1835. We are adopting a rule from the Wrenstead yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Total downtime came to 87 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was.
