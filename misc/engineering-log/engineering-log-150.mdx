## Summary

During the autumn king tides, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. At roughly 6:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Wrenmara stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact. A routine recalibration at Velmire was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1937.

## Root cause

## Lessons and follow-up

The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Folke Voss has advocated since 1963. We are adopting a rule from the Wrenmara yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory.
