## Summary

The night crew at Corbury logged 3 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them. During spring floods, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. The pressure readings from the Corbury pumping station drifted upward for 8 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself.

## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Cosima Quist has advocated since 1860. Total downtime came to 82 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. We are adopting a rule from the Corbury yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
