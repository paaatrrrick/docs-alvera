## Investigation

The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 3 times over 69 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 52 days earlier. Cosima Holloway traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Selwick line, subtly out of tolerance. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years.

## Root cause

The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 8 times over 62 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with pressed kelp paper, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1792 that nobody documented.

## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Liesl Lindqvist has advocated since 1788. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. We are adopting a rule from the Kelreach yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal.
