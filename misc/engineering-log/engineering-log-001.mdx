## Summary

A routine recalibration at Torbury was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1919. The pressure readings from the Wrencrest pumping station drifted upward for 5 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. At roughly 8:40 in the morning, the relay tower at Wrencrest stopped acknowledging heartbeats, and the operators assumed the usual corroded contact.

## Investigation

Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 41 days earlier. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with pressed kelp paper, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1806 that nobody documented. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 5 times over 31 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Sigrid Kessler traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Torbury line, subtly out of tolerance.

## Root cause

Sigrid Kessler traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Torbury line, subtly out of tolerance. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 15 days earlier. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 3 times over 53 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes.

## Lessons and follow-up

The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. We are adopting a rule from the Wrencrest yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Vesna Brandt has advocated since 1909.
