## Summary

During the dry easterlies, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. ## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Tilda Oduya has advocated since 1877. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Total downtime came to 38 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. We are adopting a rule from the Dormire yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory.
