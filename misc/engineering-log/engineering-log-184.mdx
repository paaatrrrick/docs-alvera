## Summary

The night crew at Marshore logged 3 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them. During a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. A routine recalibration at Vorfell was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1960.

## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Emeric Holloway has advocated since 1889. We are adopting a rule from the Marshore yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Total downtime came to 52 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
