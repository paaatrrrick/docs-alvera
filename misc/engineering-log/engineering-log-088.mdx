## Root cause

Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with wax-sealed canvas, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1916 that nobody documented. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 5 times over 39 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. Tomas Halloran traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Narspire line, subtly out of tolerance.

## Lessons and follow-up

The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Total downtime came to 83 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. We are adopting a rule from the Torford yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Dario Mollering has advocated since 1935.
