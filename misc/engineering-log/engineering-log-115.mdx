## Investigation

The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 4 times over 75 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with whale-bone veneer, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1880 that nobody documented. ## Root cause

Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 76 days earlier. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 2 times over 12 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes. Halvard Oduya traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Fenrun line, subtly out of tolerance.

## Lessons and follow-up

Total downtime came to 86 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Yutta Mollering has advocated since 1866. We are adopting a rule from the Velstead yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
