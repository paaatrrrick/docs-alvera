## Root cause

Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 31 days earlier. Folke Marrow traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Halvale line, subtly out of tolerance. The maintenance log showed the same fault reported 7 times over 87 months, each time closed as resolved with no notes.

## Lessons and follow-up

The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Greta Falkner has advocated since 1873. We are adopting a rule from the Wrenton yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. Total downtime came to 73 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was.
