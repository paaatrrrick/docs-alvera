## Summary

The pressure readings from the Narhollow pumping station drifted upward for 7 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. During the dry easterlies, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. The night crew at Narhollow logged 9 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them.

## Lessons and follow-up

Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Tilda Marchetti has advocated since 1863. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned. We are adopting a rule from the Narhollow yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Total downtime came to 27 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal.
