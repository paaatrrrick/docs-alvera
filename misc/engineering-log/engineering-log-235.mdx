## Summary

The night crew at Narmire logged 2 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them. The pressure readings from the Narmire pumping station drifted upward for 3 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. ## Root cause

Tomas Halloran traced the fault to a spare part cannibalized from the decommissioned Yarvik line, subtly out of tolerance. Inspection revealed the junction box had been resealed with whale-bone veneer, an improvisation dating to a repair in 1876 that nobody documented. Interviews established that the procedure existed, was correct, was printed, and was pinned behind a cabinet where nobody had seen it for years. Cross-checking the gauge against a portable reference took twenty minutes and should have happened 80 days earlier.

## Lessons and follow-up

Total downtime came to 77 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. We are adopting a rule from the Narmire yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
