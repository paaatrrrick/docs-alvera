## Summary

A routine recalibration at Selspire was performed against the wrong reference table, the one withdrawn in 1944. The pressure readings from the Tamfell pumping station drifted upward for 2 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself. The night crew at Tamfell logged 4 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them.

## Lessons and follow-up

Total downtime came to 37 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. We are adopting a rule from the Tamfell yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Rurik Marrow has advocated since 1799. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
