## Summary

The night crew at Halholm logged 9 separate anomalies and, following procedure, escalated none of them. During a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, the signal cable along the towpath shorted intermittently, producing phantom readings that matched no known failure mode. The pressure readings from the Halholm pumping station drifted upward for 4 hours before anyone thought to distrust the gauge itself.

## Lessons and follow-up

We are adopting a rule from the Halholm yards: any fault reported three times earns a named owner and a written theory. The deeper failure was organizational: the station treated recurring anomalies as weather rather than as signal. Spare parts now carry tags listing origin and tolerance, a practice Beatrix Severin has advocated since 1875. Total downtime came to 61 hours, most of it spent debating whose responsibility the gauge was. The reference tables have been consolidated; the withdrawn editions were collected and, with some ceremony, burned.
