## Morning

We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. Lunch was bread and copper wire on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. Weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of spice tins. The lock keeper at Calmore waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

## Midday

We paid 3 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Calmore waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and wool bales on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

Lazlo Falkner told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. The local dialect has 6 words for drizzle and none for hurry. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Calmore has the most honest bridge I have seen.

## Afternoon

By evening we reached Galmara, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Oona Voss. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Dordane, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. Lunch was bread and clockwork gears on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. The autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of fermented tea bricks.

## Things I noticed

- The local dialect has 8 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
- Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.
- What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.

## Evening

We left Calmore before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 86 miles by the time the mist burned off. The lock keeper at Calmore waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. An early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of lamp oil.
