## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Selcrest, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Corton before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 85 miles by the time the mist burned off. the autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of clockwork gears. By evening we reached Ossshore, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Mireille Drummond.

## Midday

By evening we reached Ossshore, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Mireille Drummond. a still and windless month found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of loom parts. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Selcrest, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Corton waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Corton has the most honest bridge I have seen. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Afternoon

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Selcrest, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Corton waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. a hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Things I noticed

- Otto Severin told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
- The local dialect has 2 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
- What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
