## Morning

We left Ilyfell before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 28 miles by the time the mist burned off. The lock keeper at Ilyfell waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and almanac reprints on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Midday

weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales. By evening we reached Tamcrest, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Liesl Greaves. We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Selton, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. The local dialect has 9 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
