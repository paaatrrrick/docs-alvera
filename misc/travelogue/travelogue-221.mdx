## Morning

Lunch was bread and almanac reprints on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. A still and windless month found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Halwick, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Jorstead, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Mireille Petrov.
