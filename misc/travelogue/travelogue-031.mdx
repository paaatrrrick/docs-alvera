## Morning

We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of clockwork gears. The lock keeper at Yarvik waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Perbury, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Midday

We left Yarvik before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 12 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Ossford, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Joren Quist.
