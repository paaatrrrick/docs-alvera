## Morning

The lock keeper at Yarmire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Vorreach, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Lazlo Ashdown. We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. A hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales.

## Midday

We left Yarmire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 53 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and dye cakes on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

You can tell a town that trades in glass floats by its smell before you can see its rooftops. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.
