## Morning

We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. an early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons. By evening we reached Eriholm, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Tomas Severin. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Kelford, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Midday

We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

Halvard Severin told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. You can tell a town that trades in spice tins by its smell before you can see its rooftops.

## Afternoon

By evening we reached Eriholm, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Tomas Severin. We left Eriwick before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 13 miles by the time the mist burned off. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Kelford, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.
