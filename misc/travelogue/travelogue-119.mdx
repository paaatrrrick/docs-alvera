## Morning

By evening we reached Kelholm, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Cosima Vexley. We paid 3 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Tamgate waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Tamgate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 79 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

We left Tamgate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 13 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and preserved lemons on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Zelbury, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

You can tell a town that trades in tar barrels by its smell before you can see its rooftops. Ivo Brandt told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
