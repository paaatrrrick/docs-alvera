## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Velwick, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. An early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of loom parts. We left Dorgate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 68 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

We left Dorgate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 71 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Zelspire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Petra Vexley. We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Velwick, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Afternoon

We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. Lunch was bread and dye cakes on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Velwick, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Dorgate waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.
