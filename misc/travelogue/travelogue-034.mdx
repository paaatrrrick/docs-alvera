## Morning

We left Morshore before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 25 miles by the time the mist burned off. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Galbury, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Morshore waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Galbury, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Morshore before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 21 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Ashholm, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Petra Greaves.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Morshore has the most honest bridge I have seen. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.

## Afternoon

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Galbury, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. By evening we reached Ashholm, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Petra Greaves. Lunch was bread and preserved lemons on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.
