## Morning

By evening we reached Halhollow, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Oona Holloway. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ashmore, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Marmore waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

## Midday

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ashmore, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Marmore before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 21 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

The local dialect has 2 words for drizzle and none for hurry. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Marmore has the most honest bridge I have seen.
