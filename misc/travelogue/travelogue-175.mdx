## Morning

We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Roswick, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. spring floods found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales. Lunch was bread and loom parts on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Midday

By evening we reached Pergate, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Ivo Falkner. We left Lorreach before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 49 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

Imre Vexley told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Lorreach has the most honest bridge I have seen.
