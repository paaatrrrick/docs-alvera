## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Dormire, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. Lunch was bread and almanac reprints on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. The lock keeper at Zelford waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

By evening we reached Marvale, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Yutta Marchetti. We left Zelford before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 76 miles by the time the mist burned off. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. The local dialect has 7 words for drizzle and none for hurry.

## Things I noticed

- You can tell a town that trades in almanac reprints by its smell before you can see its rooftops.
- I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Zelford has the most honest bridge I have seen.
