## Morning

a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales. The lock keeper at Velhollow waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and loom parts on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of clockwork gears. By evening we reached Dordane, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Beatrix Oduya.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. You can tell a town that trades in loom parts by its smell before you can see its rooftops.

## Afternoon

an early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire. We left Velhollow before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 23 miles by the time the mist burned off. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Calshore, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Velhollow waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.
