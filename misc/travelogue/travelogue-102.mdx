## Morning

Lunch was bread and glass floats on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire. We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. By evening we reached Galcrest, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Bram Halloran.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and lamp oil on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. the autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons. The lock keeper at Calmire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Galcrest, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Bram Halloran.

Dario Marrow told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
