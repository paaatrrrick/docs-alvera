## Morning

Lunch was bread and loom parts on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. The lock keeper at Velmara waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Quilhollow, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Dario Halloran.

## Midday

The lock keeper at Velmara waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Selfell, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Quilhollow, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Dario Halloran.

Annik Voss told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Afternoon

a hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons. The lock keeper at Velmara waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Velmara before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 55 miles by the time the mist burned off.
