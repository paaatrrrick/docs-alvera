## Morning

By evening we reached Kelstead, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Lazlo Severin. Weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales. Lunch was bread and glass floats on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Afternoon

Lunch was bread and clockwork gears on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. A long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of almanac reprints. The lock keeper at Tamgate waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.
