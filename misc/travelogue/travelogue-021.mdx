## Midday

We left Velspire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 88 miles by the time the mist burned off. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ilymore, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. a hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of almanac reprints. The lock keeper at Velspire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Afternoon

We left Velspire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 75 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and fermented tea bricks on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. By evening we reached Narmire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Anselm Lindqvist.
