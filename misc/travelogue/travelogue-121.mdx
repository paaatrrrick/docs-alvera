## Morning

We left Narvik before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 89 miles by the time the mist burned off. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Kelspire, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Narvik waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

## Midday

We left Narvik before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 15 miles by the time the mist burned off. The lock keeper at Narvik waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. the dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons. We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. The local dialect has 3 words for drizzle and none for hurry. You can tell a town that trades in lamp oil by its smell before you can see its rooftops.

## Afternoon
