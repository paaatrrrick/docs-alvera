## Morning

The lock keeper at Rosspire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Rosspire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 49 miles by the time the mist burned off. a hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of spice tins.

## Midday

We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. We left Rosspire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 51 miles by the time the mist burned off. a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of almanac reprints. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Rosspire has the most honest bridge I have seen.

## Afternoon

We left Rosspire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 57 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Morspire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Cosima Oduya. Lunch was bread and loom parts on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.
