## Morning

The lock keeper at Halstead waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ulmholm, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. A hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes.

## Afternoon

By evening we reached Ilyvik, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Mireille Voss. We left Halstead before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 83 miles by the time the mist burned off.
