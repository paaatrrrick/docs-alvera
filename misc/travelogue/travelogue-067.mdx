## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Zeldane, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. We left Fenstead before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 55 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and clockwork gears on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Midday

We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. Lunch was bread and wool bales on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Fenstead before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 88 miles by the time the mist burned off.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Fenstead has the most honest bridge I have seen. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.

## Afternoon

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Zeldane, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. A hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of lamp oil. The lock keeper at Fenstead waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.
