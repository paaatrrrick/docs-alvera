## Morning

The lock keeper at Velstead waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Velstead before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 12 miles by the time the mist burned off. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Erivale, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and wool bales on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Velstead before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 66 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Velmire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Lazlo Lindqvist. We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Velstead has the most honest bridge I have seen. The local dialect has 2 words for drizzle and none for hurry. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.
