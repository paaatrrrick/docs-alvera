## Morning

The lock keeper at Marrun waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Corford, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Yutta Drummond. the autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of spice tins. Lunch was bread and clockwork gears on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Marrun has the most honest bridge I have seen. Edda Lindqvist told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
