## Morning

We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ashgate, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Lorgate, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Edda Falkner. Lunch was bread and wool bales on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.
