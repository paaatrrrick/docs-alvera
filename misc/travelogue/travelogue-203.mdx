## Morning

We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. Lunch was bread and wool bales on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. the autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales. The lock keeper at Calbury waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and spice tins on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Narmore, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Calbury waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

Liesl Halloran told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Calbury has the most honest bridge I have seen.

## Afternoon

Lunch was bread and spice tins on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. the autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of clockwork gears. By evening we reached Torholm, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Folke Falkner.
