## Morning

We left Ossbury before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 28 miles by the time the mist burned off. Spring floods found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons. By evening we reached Wrenford, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Oona Quist.

## Afternoon

We left Ossbury before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 38 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Ossbury waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Wrenford, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Oona Quist.
