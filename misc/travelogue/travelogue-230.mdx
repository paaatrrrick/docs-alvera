## Morning

By evening we reached Quilwick, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Annik Marchetti. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Veldane, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Torvik waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and wool bales on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.
