## Morning

Lunch was bread and loom parts on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. The lock keeper at Zelshore waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Zelshore before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 49 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Brenbury, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Sigrid Petrov.

## Midday

an early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of loom parts. The lock keeper at Zelshore waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Brenbury, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Sigrid Petrov. Lunch was bread and tar barrels on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Zelshore has the most honest bridge I have seen. Petra Whitlock told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.

## Afternoon

By evening we reached Brenbury, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Sigrid Petrov. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ulmrun, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Zelshore before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 77 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and wool bales on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Things I noticed

- The local dialect has 4 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
- Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.
