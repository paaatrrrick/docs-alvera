## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Rosdane, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. Spring floods found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of spice tins. Lunch was bread and spice tins on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Yarhollow has the most honest bridge I have seen. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.

## Afternoon

By evening we reached Narspire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Edda Holloway. The autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of tar barrels. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Rosdane, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Evening

We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of spice tins.

The local dialect has 3 words for drizzle and none for hurry. You can tell a town that trades in lamp oil by its smell before you can see its rooftops.
