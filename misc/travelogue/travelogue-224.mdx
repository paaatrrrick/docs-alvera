## Morning

We left Ossdane before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 60 miles by the time the mist burned off. The autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of tar barrels. The lock keeper at Ossdane waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and lamp oil on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Midday

We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Ossdane waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Delvale, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Anselm Mollering.

You can tell a town that trades in glass floats by its smell before you can see its rooftops. Tilda Ashdown told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.
