## Midday

We left Dorwick before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 33 miles by the time the mist burned off. a still and windless month found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of glass floats. By evening we reached Ashvale, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Tomas Kessler. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Wrenfell, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. Annik Holloway told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.

## Afternoon

We paid 3 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Dorwick waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and spice tins on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.
