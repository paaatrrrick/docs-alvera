## Morning

a hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales. Lunch was bread and glass floats on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. The lock keeper at Dorwick waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Dorwick has the most honest bridge I have seen. Tilda Holloway told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
