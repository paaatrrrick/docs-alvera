## Morning

The lock keeper at Halspire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Fenshore, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Midday

By evening we reached Morholm, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Emeric Brandt. Lunch was bread and glass floats on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. a hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Halspire has the most honest bridge I have seen. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Afternoon

We left Halspire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 43 miles by the time the mist burned off. the dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales.
