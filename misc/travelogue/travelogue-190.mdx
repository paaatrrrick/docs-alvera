## Morning

We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. We left Ashton before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 24 miles by the time the mist burned off. Spring floods found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of spice tins.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and loom parts on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. By evening we reached Vorford, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Stellan Oduya. We left Ashton before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 27 miles by the time the mist burned off.

Maren Quist told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. You can tell a town that trades in fermented tea bricks by its smell before you can see its rooftops.

## Afternoon

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Mormara, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.
