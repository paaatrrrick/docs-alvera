## Morning

We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. We left Margate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 58 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and clockwork gears on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. By evening we reached Ashhollow, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Joren Mollering.
