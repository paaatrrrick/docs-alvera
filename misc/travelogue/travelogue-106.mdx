## Morning

spring floods found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of tar barrels. The lock keeper at Perstead waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Perstead before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 73 miles by the time the mist burned off.
