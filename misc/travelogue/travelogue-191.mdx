## Morning

The lock keeper at Lorhollow waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Yarhollow, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Folke Vexley. We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. We left Lorhollow before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 87 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Selholm, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. A long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of tar barrels. We left Lorhollow before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 83 miles by the time the mist burned off. The lock keeper at Lorhollow waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

Ivo Petrov told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Things I noticed

- The local dialect has 6 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
- What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
