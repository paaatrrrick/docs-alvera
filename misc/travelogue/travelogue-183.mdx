## Morning

The lock keeper at Kelfell waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and preserved lemons on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

The lock keeper at Kelfell waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and spice tins on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Kelfell before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 81 miles by the time the mist burned off.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. Greta Brandt told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Afternoon

By evening we reached Ericrest, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Folke Severin. Lunch was bread and tar barrels on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Kelfell before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 18 miles by the time the mist burned off.
