## Morning

We left Erispire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 30 miles by the time the mist burned off. The lock keeper at Erispire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

The lock keeper at Erispire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. an early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of loom parts. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Calrun, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. Lunch was bread and spice tins on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Erispire has the most honest bridge I have seen. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.

## Afternoon

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Calrun, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons. Lunch was bread and dye cakes on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Erispire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 31 miles by the time the mist burned off.
