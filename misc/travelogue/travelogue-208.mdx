## Morning

By evening we reached Brenvik, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Sanna Falkner. Lunch was bread and preserved lemons on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. The lock keeper at Erimara waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We paid 3 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. Oona Mollering told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. The local dialect has 2 words for drizzle and none for hurry.

## Evening

We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. By evening we reached Brenvik, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Sanna Falkner. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Halspire, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

Oona Mollering told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Erimara has the most honest bridge I have seen. The local dialect has 7 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
