## Morning

By evening we reached Narshore, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Liesl Voss. Lunch was bread and clockwork gears on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. The lock keeper at Halspire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Yarwick, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.
