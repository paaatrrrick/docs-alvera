## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Quilrun, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Galgate waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

## Midday

spring floods found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Quilrun, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Galgate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 55 miles by the time the mist burned off.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Galgate has the most honest bridge I have seen. Stellan Holloway told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Afternoon

By evening we reached Zelmore, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Liesl Voss. weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of fermented tea bricks. The lock keeper at Galgate waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and spice tins on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.
