## Morning

an early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of almanac reprints. Lunch was bread and wool bales on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. The lock keeper at Ulmmire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Jorcrest, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Maren Vexley.

## Midday

a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes. We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Ulmmire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and copper wire on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

Lazlo Brandt told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Ulmmire has the most honest bridge I have seen. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Afternoon

By evening we reached Jorcrest, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Maren Vexley. Lunch was bread and preserved lemons on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Ulmmire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 12 miles by the time the mist burned off. The lock keeper at Ulmmire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.
