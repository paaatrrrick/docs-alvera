## Morning

By evening we reached Zelvik, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Oona Lindqvist. An early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons. We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and lamp oil on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. Weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales. The lock keeper at Tormara waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Tormara has the most honest bridge I have seen. Greta Sorvane told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
