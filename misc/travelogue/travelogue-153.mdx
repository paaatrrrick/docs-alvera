## Morning

The lock keeper at Ilyspire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Correach, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales. We left Ilyspire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 69 miles by the time the mist burned off.
