## Morning

The lock keeper at Halspire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of glass floats. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Fenmire, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Halspire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 41 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Afternoon

We left Halspire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 28 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Delgate, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Sanna Mollering. We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.
