## Morning

By evening we reached Eridane, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Cosima Holloway. We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ossreach, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Rosvale before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 51 miles by the time the mist burned off.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. The local dialect has 4 words for drizzle and none for hurry.

## Things I noticed

- Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.
- You can tell a town that trades in lamp oil by its smell before you can see its rooftops.
- I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Rosvale has the most honest bridge I have seen.
- The local dialect has 6 words for drizzle and none for hurry.

## Evening

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ossreach, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. spring floods found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales.

Stellan Ashdown told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. The local dialect has 9 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
