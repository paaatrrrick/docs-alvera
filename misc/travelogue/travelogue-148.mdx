## Morning

We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Kelwick, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Zelton waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. the dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales.

## Midday

The lock keeper at Zelton waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Calstead, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Petra Whitlock. We left Zelton before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 75 miles by the time the mist burned off. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Kelwick, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Zelton has the most honest bridge I have seen. Sigrid Holloway told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.

## Afternoon

We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. Lunch was bread and spice tins on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. By evening we reached Calstead, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Petra Whitlock.

## Things I noticed

- The local dialect has 3 words for drizzle and none for hurry.

- I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Zelton has the most honest bridge I have seen.
- You can tell a town that trades in wool bales by its smell before you can see its rooftops.

## Evening

We left Zelton before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 80 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 3 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Zelton has the most honest bridge I have seen. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.
