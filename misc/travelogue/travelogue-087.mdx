## Morning

We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. We left Quilspire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 64 miles by the time the mist burned off. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ossreach, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Midday

The lock keeper at Quilspire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. A long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of spice tins. We left Quilspire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 73 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and dye cakes on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

Oona Mollering told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
