## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Lorvale, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Velford before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 25 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Ossdane, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Vesna Marrow.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and clockwork gears on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Velford before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 58 miles by the time the mist burned off. a still and windless month found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of tar barrels.

You can tell a town that trades in tar barrels by its smell before you can see its rooftops. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Velford has the most honest bridge I have seen.

## Afternoon

The lock keeper at Velford waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Lorvale, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Velford before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 41 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Evening

By evening we reached Ossdane, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Vesna Marrow. We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.
