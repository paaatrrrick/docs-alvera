## Morning

We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Corholm, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Wrendane, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Greta Sorvane.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and copper wire on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Calvale before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 73 miles by the time the mist burned off. the autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Corholm, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. Lazlo Voss told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
