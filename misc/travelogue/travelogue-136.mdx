## Morning

an early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons. By evening we reached Rosbury, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Greta Voss. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Brenrun, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Delmire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 63 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

The lock keeper at Delmire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.
