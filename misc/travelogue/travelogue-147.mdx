## Morning

The lock keeper at Delspire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Torvale, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Stellan Vexley. We left Delspire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 26 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

By evening we reached Torvale, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Stellan Vexley. We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of almanac reprints.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Delspire has the most honest bridge I have seen. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.

We left Delspire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 39 miles by the time the mist burned off.
