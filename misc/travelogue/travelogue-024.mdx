## Morning

a hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of loom parts. We left Torton before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 70 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

We left Torton before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 62 miles by the time the mist burned off. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Calmore, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Eriholm, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Folke Vexley. The lock keeper at Torton waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Torton has the most honest bridge I have seen. You can tell a town that trades in almanac reprints by its smell before you can see its rooftops. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
