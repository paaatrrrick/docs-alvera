## Morning

We left Ilywick before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 36 miles by the time the mist burned off. The lock keeper at Ilywick waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. a still and windless month found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of almanac reprints.

## Midday

the autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes. Lunch was bread and clockwork gears on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

You can tell a town that trades in spice tins by its smell before you can see its rooftops. Sanna Greaves told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
