## Morning

By evening we reached Erihollow, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Bram Brandt. A long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of lamp oil. We left Keldane before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 25 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

By evening we reached Erihollow, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Bram Brandt. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Tamstead, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. You can tell a town that trades in almanac reprints by its smell before you can see its rooftops.

## Afternoon

Lunch was bread and dye cakes on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. The lock keeper at Keldane waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.
