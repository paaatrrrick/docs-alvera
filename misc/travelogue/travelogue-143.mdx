## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Quilvale, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Ilyhollow, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Anselm Voss. We left Wrenhollow before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 61 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

an early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire. We left Wrenhollow before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 41 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Wrenhollow waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

Beatrix Sorvane told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. The local dialect has 9 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
