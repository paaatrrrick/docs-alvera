## Morning

We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. By evening we reached Wrencrest, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Tomas Severin. the autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of fermented tea bricks.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and preserved lemons on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. The lock keeper at Lorshore waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Lorshore before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 14 miles by the time the mist burned off.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Lorshore has the most honest bridge I have seen. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.

## Afternoon

By evening we reached Wrencrest, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Tomas Severin. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Morgate, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Lorshore waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

## Things I noticed

- You can tell a town that trades in glass floats by its smell before you can see its rooftops.
- Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.
