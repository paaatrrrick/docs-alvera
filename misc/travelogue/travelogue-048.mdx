## Morning

Lunch was bread and clockwork gears on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. By evening we reached Selreach, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Mireille Petrov. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Galholm, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Quilhollow before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 69 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Galholm, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. Weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of almanac reprints. We left Quilhollow before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 24 miles by the time the mist burned off.

Vesna Falkner told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. You can tell a town that trades in dye cakes by its smell before you can see its rooftops. The local dialect has 6 words for drizzle and none for hurry.

## Afternoon

We left Quilhollow before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 52 miles by the time the mist burned off. The lock keeper at Quilhollow waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Selreach, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Mireille Petrov. The autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of clockwork gears.

## Evening

We left Quilhollow before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 38 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Selreach, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Mireille Petrov.

You can tell a town that trades in lamp oil by its smell before you can see its rooftops. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
