## Morning

The lock keeper at Yarstead waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. A long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of lamp oil. By evening we reached Brenspire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Halvard Oduya.

## Midday

a hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of spice tins. The lock keeper at Yarstead waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Yarstead before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 89 miles by the time the mist burned off.

The local dialect has 6 words for drizzle and none for hurry. You can tell a town that trades in dye cakes by its smell before you can see its rooftops.

## Afternoon

By evening we reached Brenspire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Halvard Oduya. A long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of fermented tea bricks. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Perhollow, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Yarstead waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

## Things I noticed

- The local dialect has 2 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
- Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.
- Lazlo Brandt told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
- What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
- You can tell a town that trades in lamp oil by its smell before you can see its rooftops.

## Evening

Lunch was bread and lamp oil on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.
