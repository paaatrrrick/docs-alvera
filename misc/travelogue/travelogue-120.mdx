## Morning

We left Ashvik before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 68 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Jorvale, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Rurik Drummond. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Quilgate, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. an early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of lamp oil.

## Midday

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. You can tell a town that trades in glass floats by its smell before you can see its rooftops.

## Afternoon

The lock keeper at Ashvik waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and loom parts on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Ashvik before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 26 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Things I noticed

- The local dialect has 7 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
- Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.
- Cosima Sorvane told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
