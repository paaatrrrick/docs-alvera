## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Nargate, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. a hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire. Lunch was bread and copper wire on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Midday

We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Brenmara waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Brenvale, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Joren Falkner. Lunch was bread and glass floats on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Brenmara has the most honest bridge I have seen.

## Afternoon

We left Brenmara before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 49 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Brenvale, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Joren Falkner. Lunch was bread and fermented tea bricks on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.
