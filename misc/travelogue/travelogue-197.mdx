## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Rosmara, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Zelshore waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Marspire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Annik Vexley.

## Midday

We left Zelshore before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 68 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. A long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes.

You can tell a town that trades in almanac reprints by its smell before you can see its rooftops. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Zelshore has the most honest bridge I have seen. Sigrid Halloran told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
