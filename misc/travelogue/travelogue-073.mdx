## Morning

the autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of spice tins. We left Ossrun before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 22 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and preserved lemons on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Fenton, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Midday

The lock keeper at Ossrun waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Delmire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Lazlo Marchetti. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Fenton, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

The local dialect has 2 words for drizzle and none for hurry. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Afternoon

a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes. The lock keeper at Ossrun waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Fenton, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Evening
