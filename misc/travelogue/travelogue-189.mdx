## Morning

Lunch was bread and spice tins on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. Weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of loom parts. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Marmara, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Calmire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 55 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

We left Calmire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 65 miles by the time the mist burned off. The lock keeper at Calmire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Marmara, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Delford, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Halvard Falkner.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. The local dialect has 7 words for drizzle and none for hurry.

## Things I noticed

- You can tell a town that trades in wool bales by its smell before you can see its rooftops.
- Stellan Whitlock told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
- The local dialect has 4 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
- Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Evening

The lock keeper at Calmire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Calmire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 17 miles by the time the mist burned off.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Calmire has the most honest bridge I have seen. You can tell a town that trades in fermented tea bricks by its smell before you can see its rooftops.
