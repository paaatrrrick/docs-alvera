## Morning

Lunch was bread and copper wire on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Jorreach, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. A long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons. By evening we reached Velmire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Folke Halloran.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. The local dialect has 6 words for drizzle and none for hurry. You can tell a town that trades in fermented tea bricks by its smell before you can see its rooftops.
