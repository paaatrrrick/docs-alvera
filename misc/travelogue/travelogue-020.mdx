## Morning

Lunch was bread and glass floats on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. By evening we reached Narrun, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Joren Ashdown. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Perdane, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales.

## Evening

a hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of loom parts. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Perdane, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Quilreach before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 47 miles by the time the mist burned off.

The local dialect has 6 words for drizzle and none for hurry. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.
