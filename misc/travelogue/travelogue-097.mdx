## Morning

Lunch was bread and fermented tea bricks on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Galvale, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Roshollow waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Roshollow before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 25 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Galvale, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. ## Evening

We left Roshollow before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 64 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and spice tins on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

Dario Whitlock told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. You can tell a town that trades in copper wire by its smell before you can see its rooftops. The local dialect has 8 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
