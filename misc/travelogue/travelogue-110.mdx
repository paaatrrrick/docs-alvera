## Morning

We left Galgate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 17 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Erihollow, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Beatrix Vexley. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Tordane, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Midday

We left Galgate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 41 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and lamp oil on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. The dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of glass floats.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Galgate has the most honest bridge I have seen. Lazlo Whitlock told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.

## Afternoon

The lock keeper at Galgate waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Tordane, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Galgate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 75 miles by the time the mist burned off.
