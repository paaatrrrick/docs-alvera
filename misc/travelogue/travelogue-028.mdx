## Morning

We left Galmire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 13 miles by the time the mist burned off. a still and windless month found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of fermented tea bricks. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Calholm, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

The lock keeper at Galmire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and dye cakes on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. By evening we reached Torbury, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Ivo Severin.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.

## Afternoon

By evening we reached Torbury, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Ivo Severin.
