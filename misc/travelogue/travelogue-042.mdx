## Morning

The lock keeper at Dorbury waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Velvik, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Vesna Marchetti. Lunch was bread and fermented tea bricks on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. The local dialect has 8 words for drizzle and none for hurry. You can tell a town that trades in clockwork gears by its smell before you can see its rooftops.

## Afternoon

By evening we reached Velvik, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Vesna Marchetti. Lunch was bread and glass floats on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ossmire, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Evening

We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. A still and windless month found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of tar barrels.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Dorbury has the most honest bridge I have seen. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
