## Afternoon

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ulmreach, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Vorwick, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Petra Brandt. We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. Lunch was bread and fermented tea bricks on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Evening

We left Marwick before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 29 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Vorwick, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Petra Brandt.

You can tell a town that trades in clockwork gears by its smell before you can see its rooftops. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.
