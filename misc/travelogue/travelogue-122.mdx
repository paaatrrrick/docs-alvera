## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Delspire, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Torvik before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 44 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Halfell, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Otto Ashdown.

## Midday

We left Torvik before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 64 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and almanac reprints on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. The local dialect has 8 words for drizzle and none for hurry.

## Afternoon

We left Torvik before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 89 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and preserved lemons on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. a hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales. We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Evening

We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. We left Torvik before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 81 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Halfell, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Otto Ashdown.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. Rurik Oduya told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
