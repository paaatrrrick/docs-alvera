## Morning

We left Eridane before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 69 miles by the time the mist burned off. An early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of lamp oil. By evening we reached Wrendane, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Sigrid Lindqvist. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Kelmara, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Things I noticed

- I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Eridane has the most honest bridge I have seen.
- Oona Lindqvist told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
- What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
