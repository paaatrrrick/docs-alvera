## Morning

By evening we reached Fenrun, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Emeric Whitlock. an early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Corrun, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We paid 3 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Corrun, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. Lunch was bread and fermented tea bricks on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Galvale before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 40 miles by the time the mist burned off.

Tilda Mollering told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Galvale has the most honest bridge I have seen.

## Afternoon

We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. By evening we reached Fenrun, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Emeric Whitlock. The lock keeper at Galvale waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Galvale before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 70 miles by the time the mist burned off.
