## Morning

Lunch was bread and dye cakes on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Dorfell before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 75 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Yarmara, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Yutta Halloran.

## Midday

a hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of lamp oil. The lock keeper at Dorfell waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Velreach, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Yarmara, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Yutta Halloran.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Dorfell has the most honest bridge I have seen. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. The local dialect has 3 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
