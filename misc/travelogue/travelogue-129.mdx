## Morning

We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Ossgate waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Ossgate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 15 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

By evening we reached Morspire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Sigrid Kessler. Lunch was bread and wool bales on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Ossgate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 81 miles by the time the mist burned off.

You can tell a town that trades in copper wire by its smell before you can see its rooftops. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Ossgate has the most honest bridge I have seen. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Afternoon

We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ashvik, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. the autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes.

## Things I noticed

- Cosima Ashdown told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
- The local dialect has 8 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
