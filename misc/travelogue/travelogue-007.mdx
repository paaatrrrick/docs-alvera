## Morning

Lunch was bread and lamp oil on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. the autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of lamp oil. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Lorshore, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Evening

We left Zelvik before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 21 miles by the time the mist burned off. The lock keeper at Zelvik waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Tamshore, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Petra Vexley.
