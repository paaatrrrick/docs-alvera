## Morning

We left Corton before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 31 miles by the time the mist burned off. The lock keeper at Corton waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. spring floods found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of tar barrels.

## Midday

By evening we reached Wrendane, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Halvard Whitlock. We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Calbury, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Corton has the most honest bridge I have seen. Annik Falkner told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. The local dialect has 4 words for drizzle and none for hurry.

## Afternoon

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Calbury, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. spring floods found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of tar barrels.
