## Morning

an early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of loom parts. We left Kelgate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 61 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Ashgate, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Bram Drummond. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Erishore, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Midday

By evening we reached Ashgate, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Bram Drummond. a hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of loom parts. We left Kelgate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 80 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 3 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

The local dialect has 5 words for drizzle and none for hurry. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Kelgate has the most honest bridge I have seen.
