## Morning

a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire. The lock keeper at Fengate waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and lamp oil on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

We left Fengate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 21 miles by the time the mist burned off. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Velbury, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.
