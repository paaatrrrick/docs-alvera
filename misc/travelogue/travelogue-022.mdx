## Morning

We left Selmara before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 19 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and loom parts on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Caldane, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Midday

We paid 3 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. By evening we reached Permire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Maren Halloran. The lock keeper at Selmara waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Caldane, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

The local dialect has 3 words for drizzle and none for hurry. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Selmara has the most honest bridge I have seen.
