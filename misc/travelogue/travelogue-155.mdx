## Morning

The lock keeper at Rosmore waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Velton, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. a hailstorm that stripped the orchards found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes.
