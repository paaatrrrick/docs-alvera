## Morning

the autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes. We left Brenvik before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 27 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and copper wire on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Midday

We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Quilmara, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. Lunch was bread and fermented tea bricks on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. You can tell a town that trades in glass floats by its smell before you can see its rooftops. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Things I noticed
