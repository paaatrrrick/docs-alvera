## Midday

By evening we reached Ashmara, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Greta Kessler. an early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of fermented tea bricks. Lunch was bread and almanac reprints on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Velreach has the most honest bridge I have seen. You can tell a town that trades in almanac reprints by its smell before you can see its rooftops.

## Afternoon

## Evening

We left Velreach before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 62 miles by the time the mist burned off. a still and windless month found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of tar barrels. By evening we reached Ashmara, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Greta Kessler.

Mireille Whitlock told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
