## Morning

We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. By evening we reached Lorrun, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Dario Oduya. We left Brenstead before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 63 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of glass floats. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ossbury, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Lorrun, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Dario Oduya.

## Evening

The lock keeper at Brenstead waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. An early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Brenstead has the most honest bridge I have seen. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
