## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Tamshore, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. Lunch was bread and loom parts on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. The lock keeper at Calstead waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. The autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of tar barrels.

## Midday

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. The local dialect has 5 words for drizzle and none for hurry. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.
