## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Kelmore, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. By evening we reached Ashton, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Emeric Holloway.

## Midday

We left Fencrest before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 12 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. By evening we reached Ashton, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Emeric Holloway. The lock keeper at Fencrest waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

The local dialect has 5 words for drizzle and none for hurry. Casper Whitlock told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
