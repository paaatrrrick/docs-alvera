## Morning

By evening we reached Dorcrest, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Liesl Oduya. an early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes. We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. We left Ilymore before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 49 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and tar barrels on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Ilymore before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 52 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of lamp oil.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. The local dialect has 9 words for drizzle and none for hurry. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Ilymore has the most honest bridge I have seen.

## Afternoon

We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. Lunch was bread and dye cakes on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Ilymore before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 32 miles by the time the mist burned off. The lock keeper at Ilymore waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.
