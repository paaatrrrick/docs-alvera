## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Brenvale, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Erimire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Erimire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 26 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Brenvale, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Calvale, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Folke Quist. The lock keeper at Erimire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and wool bales on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

You can tell a town that trades in tar barrels by its smell before you can see its rooftops. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
