## Morning

We left Fenmire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 23 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Selton, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Otto Drummond. We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Fenmire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

## Afternoon

Lunch was bread and clockwork gears on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. By evening we reached Selton, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Otto Drummond. We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. We left Fenmire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 83 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Evening

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. Maren Ashdown told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
