## Morning

We left Selvik before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 39 miles by the time the mist burned off. The lock keeper at Selvik waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Morvik, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of spice tins.

## Midday

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Morvik, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. a still and windless month found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of loom parts. By evening we reached Quilfell, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Tilda Ashdown. We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. The local dialect has 3 words for drizzle and none for hurry.

## Afternoon

By evening we reached Quilfell, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Tilda Ashdown.
