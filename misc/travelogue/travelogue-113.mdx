## Morning

Lunch was bread and glass floats on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Lormire before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 84 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Lormire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Lormore, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Emeric Brandt.

You can tell a town that trades in tar barrels by its smell before you can see its rooftops. Stellan Holloway told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
