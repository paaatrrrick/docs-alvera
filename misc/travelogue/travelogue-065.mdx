## Morning

Lunch was bread and preserved lemons on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Torton, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. By evening we reached Calreach, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Joren Mollering.

## Midday

The lock keeper at Galreach waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. The local dialect has 3 words for drizzle and none for hurry. Tilda Voss told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Galreach has the most honest bridge I have seen.
