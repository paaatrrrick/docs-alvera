## Morning

weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire. We left Dorstead before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 86 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Dorstead waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

## Afternoon

By evening we reached Erimara, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Mireille Whitlock. We left Dorstead before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 84 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and fermented tea bricks on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.
