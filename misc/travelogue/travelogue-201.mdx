## Morning

The lock keeper at Torcrest waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Narmire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Petra Vexley. We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

The lock keeper at Torcrest waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Halvale, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Narmire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Petra Vexley. We left Torcrest before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 60 miles by the time the mist burned off.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Torcrest has the most honest bridge I have seen. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.

## Things I noticed

- You can tell a town that trades in wool bales by its smell before you can see its rooftops.
- What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
- Anselm Severin told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
- You can tell a town that trades in clockwork gears by its smell before you can see its rooftops.
- I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Torcrest has the most honest bridge I have seen.
- Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Evening

By evening we reached Narmire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Petra Vexley. We left Torcrest before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 86 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.
