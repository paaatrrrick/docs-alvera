## Morning

The lock keeper at Lordane waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Pervale, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. Lunch was bread and fermented tea bricks on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Midday

We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. Lunch was bread and loom parts on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. By evening we reached Eriton, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Dario Sorvane.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Lordane has the most honest bridge I have seen. Cosima Falkner told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
