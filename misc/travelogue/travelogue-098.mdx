## Morning

We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Jorwick, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Dormire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Halvard Oduya.

## Midday

We left Rosholm before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 24 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Jorwick, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. Lunch was bread and lamp oil on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Rosholm has the most honest bridge I have seen. Dario Severin told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
