## Morning

Lunch was bread and glass floats on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. The lock keeper at Ilyhollow waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. A still and windless month found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of clockwork gears.

## Midday

an early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of lamp oil. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Rosstead, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Narmore, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Casper Severin.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Ilyhollow has the most honest bridge I have seen. Anselm Greaves told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.

## Afternoon

We left Ilyhollow before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 52 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Narmore, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Casper Severin. Weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons.

## Evening

By evening we reached Narmore, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Casper Severin. We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Ilyhollow has the most honest bridge I have seen. The local dialect has 4 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
