## Morning

By evening we reached Quilholm, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Greta Falkner. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Fenhollow, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of fermented tea bricks.

## Midday

We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. A long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire. We left Velford before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 55 miles by the time the mist burned off.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Velford has the most honest bridge I have seen. You can tell a town that trades in spice tins by its smell before you can see its rooftops. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
