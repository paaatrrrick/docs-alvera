## Morning

We left Vorreach before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 19 miles by the time the mist burned off. The lock keeper at Vorreach waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Zelwick, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Beatrix Kessler. Lunch was bread and loom parts on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Midday

The lock keeper at Vorreach waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Zelwick, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Beatrix Kessler. A long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of lamp oil. Lunch was bread and loom parts on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Vorreach has the most honest bridge I have seen. You can tell a town that trades in preserved lemons by its smell before you can see its rooftops.

## Afternoon

We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. We left Vorreach before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 40 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and dye cakes on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Evening

By evening we reached Zelwick, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Beatrix Kessler. The autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Vorreach has the most honest bridge I have seen. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
