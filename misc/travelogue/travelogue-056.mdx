## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Zelwick, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. Lunch was bread and almanac reprints on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. By evening we reached Correach, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Otto Sorvane. We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

We paid 3 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. We left Marvale before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 55 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Correach, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Otto Sorvane. The dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons.

The local dialect has 6 words for drizzle and none for hurry. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. Greta Marchetti told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
