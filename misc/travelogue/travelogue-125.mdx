## Midday

The lock keeper at Calwick waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Calwick before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 27 miles by the time the mist burned off. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ossreach, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We paid 3 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Calwick has the most honest bridge I have seen.
