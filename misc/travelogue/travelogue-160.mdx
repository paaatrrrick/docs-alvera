## Morning

By evening we reached Narmire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Bram Greaves. We left Lorvik before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 23 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and loom parts on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire.

## Midday

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. You can tell a town that trades in wool bales by its smell before you can see its rooftops.
