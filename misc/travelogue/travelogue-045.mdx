## Morning

a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of lamp oil. By evening we reached Quilton, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Liesl Ashdown. Lunch was bread and almanac reprints on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Midday

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Calrun, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. a still and windless month found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes. The lock keeper at Delton waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Quilton, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Liesl Ashdown.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Afternoon

We left Delton before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 23 miles by the time the mist burned off.
