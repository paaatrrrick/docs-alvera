## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Cormore, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Tammore, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Casper Vexley. We left Seldane before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 90 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and loom parts on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Seldane before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 64 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

You can tell a town that trades in spice tins by its smell before you can see its rooftops. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Seldane has the most honest bridge I have seen.

## Afternoon
