## Morning

Lunch was bread and almanac reprints on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Galton before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 15 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and clockwork gears on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Galton before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 66 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

You can tell a town that trades in glass floats by its smell before you can see its rooftops. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.

## Afternoon

weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons. By evening we reached Kelbury, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Greta Severin. We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

## Things I noticed

- I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Galton has the most honest bridge I have seen.
- You can tell a town that trades in dye cakes by its smell before you can see its rooftops.
- What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.

## Evening

By evening we reached Kelbury, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Greta Severin. the dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of spice tins.
