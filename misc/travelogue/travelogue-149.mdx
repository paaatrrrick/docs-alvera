## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Kelford, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of loom parts. Lunch was bread and preserved lemons on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Midday
