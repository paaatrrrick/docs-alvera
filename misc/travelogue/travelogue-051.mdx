## Morning

The lock keeper at Narreach waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and spice tins on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. the dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of clockwork gears. By evening we reached Lorhollow, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Beatrix Whitlock.

## Afternoon

We paid 3 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. We left Narreach before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 58 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and wool bales on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.
