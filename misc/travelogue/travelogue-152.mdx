## Morning

The lock keeper at Rosford waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and fermented tea bricks on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. By evening we reached Zelcrest, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Ivo Quist. We left Rosford before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 21 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

By evening we reached Zelcrest, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Ivo Quist. We left Rosford before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 34 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Eriholm, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

Casper Halloran told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Rosford has the most honest bridge I have seen. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
