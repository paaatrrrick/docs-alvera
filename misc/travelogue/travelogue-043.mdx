## Morning

By evening we reached Vorton, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Joren Lindqvist. The autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Eribury, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Midday

We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Eribury, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Lorreach waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. You can tell a town that trades in tar barrels by its smell before you can see its rooftops. Joren Oduya told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.

## Afternoon

Lunch was bread and glass floats on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. Spring floods found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of spice tins. The lock keeper at Lorreach waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Eribury, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.
