## Morning

We left Lorford before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 32 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Velholm, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Tomas Sorvane. The autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of glass floats.

## Afternoon

We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Velstead, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.
