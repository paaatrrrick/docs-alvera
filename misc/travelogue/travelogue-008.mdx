## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Zelbury, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Fenmore, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Yutta Severin. A still and windless month found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire. The lock keeper at Galford waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

## Midday

## Afternoon

The lock keeper at Galford waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Galford before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 25 miles by the time the mist burned off. ## Evening

We left Galford before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 57 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

The local dialect has 8 words for drizzle and none for hurry. You can tell a town that trades in glass floats by its smell before you can see its rooftops.
