## Morning

We left Torford before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 89 miles by the time the mist burned off. The autumn king tides found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes. The lock keeper at Torford waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and wool bales on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.
