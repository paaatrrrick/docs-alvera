## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Torgate, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Fenstead before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 88 miles by the time the mist burned off. Lunch was bread and copper wire on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Evening

By evening we reached Dormore, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Dario Falkner. We left Fenstead before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 53 miles by the time the mist burned off.

Tomas Kessler told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. The local dialect has 3 words for drizzle and none for hurry. You can tell a town that trades in dye cakes by its smell before you can see its rooftops.
