## Morning

We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. We left Cordane before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 78 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Rosford, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Tilda Oduya. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ossmire, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Midday

The lock keeper at Cordane waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. By evening we reached Rosford, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Tilda Oduya. Lunch was bread and dye cakes on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. You can tell a town that trades in lamp oil by its smell before you can see its rooftops. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
