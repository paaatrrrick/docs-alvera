## Morning

an early frost found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Calton, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Morgate waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

## Midday

By evening we reached Ashstead, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Imre Ashdown. We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. A long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of spice tins.

Tomas Brandt told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Morgate has the most honest bridge I have seen.

## Evening

By evening we reached Ashstead, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Imre Ashdown. Lunch was bread and spice tins on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.
