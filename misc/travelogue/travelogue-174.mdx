## Morning

We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. Lunch was bread and spice tins on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. Weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of loom parts.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and clockwork gears on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 3 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. By evening we reached Ashvale, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Lazlo Greaves. A long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of preserved lemons.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Marreach has the most honest bridge I have seen. Imre Marrow told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.

## Evening

We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Corwick, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. Lunch was bread and dye cakes on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.
