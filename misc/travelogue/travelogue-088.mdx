## Morning

Lunch was bread and dye cakes on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We paid 7 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Kelton, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. By evening we reached Ashspire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Bram Oduya.

## Evening

The lock keeper at Dorstead waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We paid 5 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. The local dialect has 7 words for drizzle and none for hurry. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Dorstead has the most honest bridge I have seen.
