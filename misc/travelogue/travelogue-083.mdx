## Morning

We paid 2 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. Lunch was bread and preserved lemons on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. weeks of sideways rain found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes.

## Midday

We left Zelreach before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 66 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Vorshore, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Petra Petrov. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Kelmore, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. Lunch was bread and fermented tea bricks on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

The local dialect has 7 words for drizzle and none for hurry. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
