## Morning

We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ilyhollow, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Selford waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. Lunch was bread and fermented tea bricks on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

You can tell a town that trades in fermented tea bricks by its smell before you can see its rooftops. The local dialect has 3 words for drizzle and none for hurry. What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops.
