## Morning

By evening we reached Ashrun, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Sanna Severin. We left Velford before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 12 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 8 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. Lunch was bread and clockwork gears on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle.

## Midday
