## Morning

Lunch was bread and almanac reprints on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. The lock keeper at Ashvik waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Rosreach, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Midday

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Rosreach, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Ashvik waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. We left Ashvik before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 30 miles by the time the mist burned off.

You can tell a town that trades in tar barrels by its smell before you can see its rooftops. The local dialect has 5 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
