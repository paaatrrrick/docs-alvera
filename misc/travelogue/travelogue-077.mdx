## Morning

The lock keeper at Zelmire waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ossvik, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. the dry easterlies found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of clockwork gears. By evening we reached Lormore, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Mireille Halloran.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. Joren Kessler told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.

## Afternoon

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Ossvik, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. a still and windless month found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of lamp oil. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.
