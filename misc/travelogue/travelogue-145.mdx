## Morning

We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. Lunch was bread and fermented tea bricks on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. By evening we reached Ulmdane, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Bram Lindqvist. We left Wrengate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 80 miles by the time the mist burned off.

## Midday

The lock keeper at Wrengate waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes. We left Wrengate before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 43 miles by the time the mist burned off. The local dialect has 7 words for drizzle and none for hurry. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does.
