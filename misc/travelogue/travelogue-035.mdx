## Morning

a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of dye cakes. Lunch was bread and lamp oil on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Delrun, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Midday

We paid 4 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. a still and windless month found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of copper wire. Lunch was bread and preserved lemons on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. By evening we reached Rosgate, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Casper Brandt.

Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. Otto Vexley told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.

## Afternoon

spring floods found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of wool bales. Lunch was bread and fermented tea bricks on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Delrun, which leans rather more than the postcards admit.

## Evening

The lock keeper at Perdane waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. We left Perdane before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 82 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 3 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

You can tell a town that trades in spice tins by its smell before you can see its rooftops. I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Perdane has the most honest bridge I have seen. Otto Vexley told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous.
