## Morning

By evening we reached Halfell, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Halvard Lindqvist. In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Lorvale, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. The lock keeper at Dorhollow waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other. a long fog season found us in open country, and we sheltered in a drying shed that smelled gloriously of lamp oil.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and almanac reprints on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. By evening we reached Halfell, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Halvard Lindqvist. We left Dorhollow before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 32 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 6 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism.

I have begun to judge towns by their bridges, and Dorhollow has the most honest bridge I have seen. Ivo Falkner told me the canals are shrinking, then sold me a map on which they are noticeably generous. The local dialect has 2 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
