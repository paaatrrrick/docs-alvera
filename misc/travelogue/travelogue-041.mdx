## Morning

In the afternoon we passed the famous leaning granary of Quilwick, which leans rather more than the postcards admit. We left Rosbury before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 31 miles by the time the mist burned off. We paid 9 small coins for passage on the chain ferry, operated by a child of stern professionalism. The lock keeper at Rosbury waved us through with a breakfast roll in one hand and the tide tables in the other.

## Midday

Lunch was bread and spice tins on the wall above the weir, watching barges queue like patient cattle. We left Rosbury before light, the towpath frost crunching underfoot, and made 17 miles by the time the mist burned off. By evening we reached Calspire, footsore, and took a room above the cooperage from the obliging Stellan Brandt.

What the guidebooks miss is the sound of the canal at night, a conversation of ropes and hulls that never quite stops. Everyone here owns exactly one umbrella and lends it freely, a system that should not work and does. The local dialect has 3 words for drizzle and none for hurry.
