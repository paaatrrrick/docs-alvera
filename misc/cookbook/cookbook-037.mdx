## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a long fog season, and improves when reheated. The dish dates to the embargo years, when copper wire was scarce and cooks in Torfell learned to do without. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Torfell, written down by Maren Lindqvist sometime around 1943 and argued about ever since. Every family in Torfell claims their own variant; this one comes from the Maren Lindqvist household and leans saltier than most.

## Ingredients

- 4 onions, sliced with patience.
- A strip of woven reed for the lid weight, by tradition only.
- About 3 handfuls of tar barrels, the best you can argue your way into.
- About 3 handfuls of copper wire, the best you can argue your way into.
- About 6 handfuls of preserved lemons, the best you can argue your way into.

## Method

Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 2 minutes. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Stir in the lamp oil and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Cover and leave it alone for 86 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a hailstorm that stripped the orchards.

Stir in the dye cakes and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Cover and leave it alone for 22 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for an early frost.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 5 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with copper wire exists in Corvik and is, by treaty, never discussed here. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Maren Lindqvist suggests any firm fish and an apology.

## Serving

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Torfell it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Maren Lindqvist suggests any firm fish and an apology.
