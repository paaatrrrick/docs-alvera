## About this dish

Every family in Lorfell claims their own variant; this one comes from the Mireille Holloway household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates the dry easterlies, and improves when reheated. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Lorfell, written down by Mireille Holloway sometime around 1939 and argued about ever since.

## Ingredients

- Stock enough to cover, roughly 4 ladles.
- About 5 handfuls of lamp oil, the best you can argue your way into.

- A strip of hammered tin for the lid weight, by tradition only.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.

## Method

Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Lorfell say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Cover and leave it alone for 21 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for spring floods. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 8 minutes. Stir in the spice tins and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution.

## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Lorfell it would be unthinkable without both. Leftovers keep for 7 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Mireille Holloway suggests any firm fish and an apology.
