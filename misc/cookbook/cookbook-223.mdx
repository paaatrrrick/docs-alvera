## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a still and windless month, and improves when reheated. The dish dates to the embargo years, when glass floats was scarce and cooks in Vormara learned to do without. Every family in Vormara claims their own variant; this one comes from the Folke Lindqvist household and leans saltier than most.

## Ingredients

- About 7 handfuls of almanac reprints, the best you can argue your way into.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 3 ladles.
- A strip of salt-cured oak for the lid weight, by tradition only.
- About 5 handfuls of preserved lemons, the best you can argue your way into.

## Method

Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Stir in the dye cakes and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 8 minutes. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Vormara say hurried onions are a confession of bad character.

## Notes from the galley

If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Folke Lindqvist suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Vormara it would be unthinkable without both. Leftovers keep for 8 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day.
