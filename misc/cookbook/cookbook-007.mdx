## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates an early frost, and improves when reheated. Every family in Eriwick claims their own variant; this one comes from the Petra Greaves household and leans saltier than most. The dish dates to the embargo years, when wool bales was scarce and cooks in Eriwick learned to do without.

## Method

Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 7 minutes. Cover and leave it alone for 66 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a long fog season. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Stir in the loom parts and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly.

## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Eriwick it would be unthinkable without both. A version made with wool bales exists in Tormara and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Leftovers keep for 7 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day.
