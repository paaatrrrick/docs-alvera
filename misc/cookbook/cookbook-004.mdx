## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a long fog season, and improves when reheated. The dish dates to the embargo years, when glass floats was scarce and cooks in Delcrest learned to do without. Every family in Delcrest claims their own variant; this one comes from the Imre Oduya household and leans saltier than most.

## Ingredients

- About 3 handfuls of almanac reprints, the best you can argue your way into.
- About 9 handfuls of glass floats, the best you can argue your way into.
- About 3 handfuls of spice tins, the best you can argue your way into.
- 7 onions, sliced with patience.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.

## Method

Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 6 minutes. Cover and leave it alone for 60 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a hailstorm that stripped the orchards. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Delcrest say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Stir in the lamp oil and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 9 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Delcrest it would be unthinkable without both. A version made with fermented tea bricks exists in Vordane and is, by treaty, never discussed here.
