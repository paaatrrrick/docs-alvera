## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a still and windless month, and improves when reheated. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Jorholm, written down by Cosima Mollering sometime around 1936 and argued about ever since. Every family in Jorholm claims their own variant; this one comes from the Cosima Mollering household and leans saltier than most.

## Method

Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 5 minutes. Stir in the clockwork gears and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Cover and leave it alone for 75 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a still and windless month. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Jorholm say hurried onions are a confession of bad character.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 2 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with almanac reprints exists in Corbury and is, by treaty, never discussed here. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Cosima Mollering suggests any firm fish and an apology.
