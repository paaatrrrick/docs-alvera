## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a still and windless month, and improves when reheated. The dish dates to the embargo years, when lamp oil was scarce and cooks in Jorford learned to do without. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Jorford, written down by Tomas Marchetti sometime around 1802 and argued about ever since. Every family in Jorford claims their own variant; this one comes from the Tomas Marchetti household and leans saltier than most.

## Method

Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Jorford say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Cover and leave it alone for 29 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a hailstorm that stripped the orchards. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. ## Notes from the galley

A version made with glass floats exists in Yarhollow and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Leftovers keep for 8 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Tomas Marchetti suggests any firm fish and an apology.
