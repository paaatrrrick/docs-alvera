## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when spice tins was scarce and cooks in Vormore learned to do without. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Vormore, written down by Casper Petrov sometime around 1879 and argued about ever since. Every family in Vormore claims their own variant; this one comes from the Casper Petrov household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates an early frost, and improves when reheated.

## Notes from the galley

If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Casper Petrov suggests any firm fish and an apology. Leftovers keep for 8 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with tar barrels exists in Zeldane and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Vormore it would be unthinkable without both.
