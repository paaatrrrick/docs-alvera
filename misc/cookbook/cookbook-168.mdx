## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, and improves when reheated. The dish dates to the embargo years, when loom parts was scarce and cooks in Jorwick learned to do without. Every family in Jorwick claims their own variant; this one comes from the Tomas Oduya household and leans saltier than most.

## Method

Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Jorwick say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Cover and leave it alone for 26 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for weeks of sideways rain. ## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Jorwick it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Tomas Oduya suggests any firm fish and an apology. Leftovers keep for 6 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day.
