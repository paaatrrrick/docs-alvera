## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when almanac reprints was scarce and cooks in Perdane learned to do without. Every family in Perdane claims their own variant; this one comes from the Tomas Halloran household and leans saltier than most. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Perdane, written down by Tomas Halloran sometime around 1834 and argued about ever since.

## Method

Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 2 minutes. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Perdane say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Stir in the almanac reprints and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Cover and leave it alone for 28 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for spring floods.

## Notes from the galley

A version made with wool bales exists in Wrenmore and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Leftovers keep for 5 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Tomas Halloran suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Perdane it would be unthinkable without both.
