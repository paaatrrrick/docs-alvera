## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when loom parts was scarce and cooks in Jorholm learned to do without. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Jorholm, written down by Vesna Voss sometime around 1805 and argued about ever since. Every family in Jorholm claims their own variant; this one comes from the Vesna Voss household and leans saltier than most.

## Ingredients

- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- A strip of blue slate for the lid weight, by tradition only.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.
- 4 onions, sliced with patience.

## Method

Stir in the lamp oil and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 7 minutes. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Jorholm say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution.

## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Jorholm it would be unthinkable without both. Leftovers keep for 6 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Vesna Voss suggests any firm fish and an apology. A version made with dye cakes exists in Brenford and is, by treaty, never discussed here.
