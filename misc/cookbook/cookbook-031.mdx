## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates an early frost, and improves when reheated. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Rosrun, written down by Vesna Halloran sometime around 1832 and argued about ever since. The dish dates to the embargo years, when glass floats was scarce and cooks in Rosrun learned to do without.

## Ingredients

- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- About 4 handfuls of clockwork gears, the best you can argue your way into.
- 4 onions, sliced with patience.
- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 6 ladles.

## Method

Stir in the clockwork gears and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Rosrun say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Cover and leave it alone for 55 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for an early frost.

## Notes from the galley

A version made with loom parts exists in Tamfell and is, by treaty, never discussed here. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Vesna Halloran suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Rosrun it would be unthinkable without both. Leftovers keep for 4 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day.
