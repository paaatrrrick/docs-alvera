## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when fermented tea bricks was scarce and cooks in Jormara learned to do without. Every family in Jormara claims their own variant; this one comes from the Sigrid Brandt household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a long fog season, and improves when reheated.

## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Jormara it would be unthinkable without both. Leftovers keep for 7 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Sigrid Brandt suggests any firm fish and an apology.
