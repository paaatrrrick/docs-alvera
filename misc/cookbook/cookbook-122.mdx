## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates weeks of sideways rain, and improves when reheated. Every family in Velmara claims their own variant; this one comes from the Anselm Halloran household and leans saltier than most. The dish dates to the embargo years, when dye cakes was scarce and cooks in Velmara learned to do without.

## Method

Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Velmara say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Stir in the spice tins and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 8 minutes. Cover and leave it alone for 87 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for an early frost.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 5 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with preserved lemons exists in Galbury and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Velmara it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Anselm Halloran suggests any firm fish and an apology.
