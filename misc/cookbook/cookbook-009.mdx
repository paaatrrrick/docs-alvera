## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when copper wire was scarce and cooks in Kelvik learned to do without. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Kelvik, written down by Annik Greaves sometime around 1792 and argued about ever since. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates spring floods, and improves when reheated. Every family in Kelvik claims their own variant; this one comes from the Annik Greaves household and leans saltier than most.

## Notes from the galley

If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Annik Greaves suggests any firm fish and an apology. Leftovers keep for 4 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Kelvik it would be unthinkable without both. A version made with preserved lemons exists in Vordane and is, by treaty, never discussed here.
