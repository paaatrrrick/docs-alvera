## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates the dry easterlies, and improves when reheated. Every family in Selvale claims their own variant; this one comes from the Joren Petrov household and leans saltier than most. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Selvale, written down by Joren Petrov sometime around 1961 and argued about ever since.

## Ingredients

- A strip of blue slate for the lid weight, by tradition only.
- A strip of salt-cured oak for the lid weight, by tradition only.
- About 8 handfuls of loom parts, the best you can argue your way into.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.
- 3 onions, sliced with patience.

## Method

Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Cover and leave it alone for 43 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for the autumn king tides. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Stir in the fermented tea bricks and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Selvale say hurried onions are a confession of bad character.

Stir in the dye cakes and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Cover and leave it alone for 79 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for spring floods. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 3 minutes.

## Notes from the galley

If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Joren Petrov suggests any firm fish and an apology. A version made with loom parts exists in Ashmire and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Leftovers keep for 6 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Selvale it would be unthinkable without both.

## Serving

If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Joren Petrov suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Selvale it would be unthinkable without both. A version made with preserved lemons exists in Ashmire and is, by treaty, never discussed here.
