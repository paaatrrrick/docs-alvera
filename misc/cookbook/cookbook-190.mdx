## About this dish

Every family in Rosmara claims their own variant; this one comes from the Vesna Lindqvist household and leans saltier than most. The dish dates to the embargo years, when almanac reprints was scarce and cooks in Rosmara learned to do without. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Rosmara, written down by Vesna Lindqvist sometime around 1818 and argued about ever since. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates weeks of sideways rain, and improves when reheated.

Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 7 minutes. ## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 2 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with lamp oil exists in Quilvik and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Rosmara it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Vesna Lindqvist suggests any firm fish and an apology.
