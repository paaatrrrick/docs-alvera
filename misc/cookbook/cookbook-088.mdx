## About this dish

Every family in Kelholm claims their own variant; this one comes from the Sigrid Mollering household and leans saltier than most. The dish dates to the embargo years, when almanac reprints was scarce and cooks in Kelholm learned to do without. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a long fog season, and improves when reheated. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Kelholm, written down by Sigrid Mollering sometime around 1814 and argued about ever since.

## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Kelholm it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Sigrid Mollering suggests any firm fish and an apology. A version made with fermented tea bricks exists in Correach and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Leftovers keep for 4 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day.
