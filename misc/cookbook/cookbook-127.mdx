## About this dish

This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Ulmwick, written down by Tilda Lindqvist sometime around 1876 and argued about ever since. Every family in Ulmwick claims their own variant; this one comes from the Tilda Lindqvist household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates the dry easterlies, and improves when reheated.

## Method

Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Cover and leave it alone for 27 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a hailstorm that stripped the orchards. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Ulmwick say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 6 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with tar barrels exists in Rosfell and is, by treaty, never discussed here. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Tilda Lindqvist suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Ulmwick it would be unthinkable without both.
