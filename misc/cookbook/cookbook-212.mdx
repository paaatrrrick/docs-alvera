## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates the dry easterlies, and improves when reheated. The dish dates to the embargo years, when almanac reprints was scarce and cooks in Delbury learned to do without. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Delbury, written down by Petra Severin sometime around 1863 and argued about ever since.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 4 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with loom parts exists in Delmore and is, by treaty, never discussed here. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Petra Severin suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Delbury it would be unthinkable without both.
