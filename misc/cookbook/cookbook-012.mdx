## About this dish

Every family in Ilyrun claims their own variant; this one comes from the Stellan Quist household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates weeks of sideways rain, and improves when reheated. The dish dates to the embargo years, when glass floats was scarce and cooks in Ilyrun learned to do without.

## Ingredients

- About 2 handfuls of clockwork gears, the best you can argue your way into.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.
- 9 onions, sliced with patience.
- A strip of whale-bone veneer for the lid weight, by tradition only.

## Method

Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Cover and leave it alone for 15 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for spring floods. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Ilyrun say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Stir in the tar barrels and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 3 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Ilyrun it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Stellan Quist suggests any firm fish and an apology.
