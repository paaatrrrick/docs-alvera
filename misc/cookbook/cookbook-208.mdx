## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates the autumn king tides, and improves when reheated. Every family in Galhollow claims their own variant; this one comes from the Cosima Holloway household and leans saltier than most. The dish dates to the embargo years, when fermented tea bricks was scarce and cooks in Galhollow learned to do without.

## Method

Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Cover and leave it alone for 90 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a long fog season. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 9 minutes. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Galhollow say hurried onions are a confession of bad character.

## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Galhollow it would be unthinkable without both. Leftovers keep for 7 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Cosima Holloway suggests any firm fish and an apology.
