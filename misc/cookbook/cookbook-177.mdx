## About this dish

This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Zelwick, written down by Mireille Voss sometime around 1939 and argued about ever since. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a long fog season, and improves when reheated. Every family in Zelwick claims their own variant; this one comes from the Mireille Voss household and leans saltier than most. The dish dates to the embargo years, when dye cakes was scarce and cooks in Zelwick learned to do without.

## Notes from the galley

If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Mireille Voss suggests any firm fish and an apology. A version made with lamp oil exists in Yarfell and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Zelwick it would be unthinkable without both.
