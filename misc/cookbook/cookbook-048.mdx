## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates spring floods, and improves when reheated. The dish dates to the embargo years, when preserved lemons was scarce and cooks in Galholm learned to do without. Every family in Galholm claims their own variant; this one comes from the Liesl Ashdown household and leans saltier than most.

## Ingredients

- 2 onions, sliced with patience.
- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 6 ladles.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 7 ladles.
- 5 onions, sliced with patience.

## Method

Cover and leave it alone for 20 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for weeks of sideways rain. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 6 minutes. Stir in the spice tins and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly.

## Notes from the galley

A version made with dye cakes exists in Ulmton and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Leftovers keep for 8 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Liesl Ashdown suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Galholm it would be unthinkable without both.
