## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when clockwork gears was scarce and cooks in Halrun learned to do without. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates an early frost, and improves when reheated. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Halrun, written down by Folke Voss sometime around 1840 and argued about ever since.

## Ingredients

- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- About 5 handfuls of spice tins, the best you can argue your way into.

## Method

Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 6 minutes. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Halrun say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Cover and leave it alone for 55 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a hailstorm that stripped the orchards. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 2 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Folke Voss suggests any firm fish and an apology. A version made with fermented tea bricks exists in Dorfell and is, by treaty, never discussed here.
