## About this dish

This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Ashholm, written down by Sanna Oduya sometime around 1926 and argued about ever since. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates weeks of sideways rain, and improves when reheated. Every family in Ashholm claims their own variant; this one comes from the Sanna Oduya household and leans saltier than most.

## Ingredients

- Stock enough to cover, roughly 4 ladles.
- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- About 4 handfuls of spice tins, the best you can argue your way into.
- About 3 handfuls of loom parts, the best you can argue your way into.
- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.

## Method

Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Ashholm say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 6 minutes. Stir in the almanac reprints and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 7 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Ashholm it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Sanna Oduya suggests any firm fish and an apology. A version made with glass floats exists in Marwick and is, by treaty, never discussed here.
