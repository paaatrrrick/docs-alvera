## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when preserved lemons was scarce and cooks in Vorshore learned to do without. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Vorshore, written down by Stellan Severin sometime around 1871 and argued about ever since. Every family in Vorshore claims their own variant; this one comes from the Stellan Severin household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates spring floods, and improves when reheated.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 7 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with preserved lemons exists in Quilholm and is, by treaty, never discussed here. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Stellan Severin suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Vorshore it would be unthinkable without both.
