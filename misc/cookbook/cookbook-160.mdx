## About this dish

Every family in Galhollow claims their own variant; this one comes from the Stellan Oduya household and leans saltier than most. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Galhollow, written down by Stellan Oduya sometime around 1869 and argued about ever since. The dish dates to the embargo years, when lamp oil was scarce and cooks in Galhollow learned to do without.

## Ingredients

- A strip of fired clay for the lid weight, by tradition only.
- 9 onions, sliced with patience.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 8 ladles.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.
- 8 onions, sliced with patience.

## Method

Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Galhollow say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 6 minutes. Stir in the preserved lemons and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly.

Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. ## Notes from the galley

If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Stellan Oduya suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Galhollow it would be unthinkable without both. Leftovers keep for 3 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day.

## Serving

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Galhollow it would be unthinkable without both. A version made with spice tins exists in Quilvik and is, by treaty, never discussed here.
