## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when lamp oil was scarce and cooks in Galhollow learned to do without. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, and improves when reheated. Every family in Galhollow claims their own variant; this one comes from the Tomas Voss household and leans saltier than most.

## Method

Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 7 minutes. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Galhollow say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. ## Notes from the galley

A version made with copper wire exists in Dorgate and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Leftovers keep for 8 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Galhollow it would be unthinkable without both.
