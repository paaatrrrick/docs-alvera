## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates spring floods, and improves when reheated. The dish dates to the embargo years, when tar barrels was scarce and cooks in Ilyholm learned to do without. Every family in Ilyholm claims their own variant; this one comes from the Maren Holloway household and leans saltier than most.

## Ingredients

- About 3 handfuls of preserved lemons, the best you can argue your way into.
- 8 onions, sliced with patience.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 5 ladles.
- 5 onions, sliced with patience.
- A strip of blue slate for the lid weight, by tradition only.

## Method

Stir in the fermented tea bricks and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Ilyholm say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 8 minutes.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 8 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Maren Holloway suggests any firm fish and an apology. A version made with fermented tea bricks exists in Torvik and is, by treaty, never discussed here.

## Serving

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Ilyholm it would be unthinkable without both. A version made with almanac reprints exists in Torvik and is, by treaty, never discussed here. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Maren Holloway suggests any firm fish and an apology.
