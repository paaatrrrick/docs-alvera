## About this dish

Every family in Brenrun claims their own variant; this one comes from the Rurik Mollering household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates an early frost, and improves when reheated. The dish dates to the embargo years, when clockwork gears was scarce and cooks in Brenrun learned to do without.

- About 6 handfuls of almanac reprints, the best you can argue your way into.

## Method

Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Brenrun say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Stir in the loom parts and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Cover and leave it alone for 22 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a still and windless month.

## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Brenrun it would be unthinkable without both. A version made with dye cakes exists in Morrun and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Leftovers keep for 6 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day.
