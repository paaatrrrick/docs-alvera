## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when clockwork gears was scarce and cooks in Torholm learned to do without. Every family in Torholm claims their own variant; this one comes from the Bram Marchetti household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a still and windless month, and improves when reheated.

## Ingredients

- A strip of woven reed for the lid weight, by tradition only.
- A strip of hammered tin for the lid weight, by tradition only.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 3 ladles.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 6 ladles.
- 3 onions, sliced with patience.

## Method

Stir in the tar barrels and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Torholm say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Cover and leave it alone for 88 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a long fog season. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next.

## Notes from the galley

A version made with clockwork gears exists in Quilbury and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Leftovers keep for 4 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Bram Marchetti suggests any firm fish and an apology.
