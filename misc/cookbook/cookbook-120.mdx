## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when preserved lemons was scarce and cooks in Tamholm learned to do without. Every family in Tamholm claims their own variant; this one comes from the Imre Marchetti household and leans saltier than most. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Tamholm, written down by Imre Marchetti sometime around 1866 and argued about ever since.

## Ingredients

- 5 onions, sliced with patience.
- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- 8 onions, sliced with patience.
- 9 onions, sliced with patience.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.

## Method

Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 3 minutes. Stir in the spice tins and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Cover and leave it alone for 71 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for the dry easterlies. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Tamholm say hurried onions are a confession of bad character.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 6 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Tamholm it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Imre Marchetti suggests any firm fish and an apology. A version made with preserved lemons exists in Zeldane and is, by treaty, never discussed here.
