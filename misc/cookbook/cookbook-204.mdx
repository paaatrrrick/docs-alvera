## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates an early frost, and improves when reheated. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Nardane, written down by Folke Drummond sometime around 1938 and argued about ever since. Every family in Nardane claims their own variant; this one comes from the Folke Drummond household and leans saltier than most. The dish dates to the embargo years, when spice tins was scarce and cooks in Nardane learned to do without.

## Ingredients

- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 5 ladles.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.
- About 4 handfuls of copper wire, the best you can argue your way into.
- A strip of rope-fiber composite for the lid weight, by tradition only.

## Method

Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Cover and leave it alone for 83 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for spring floods. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 7 minutes. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Nardane say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution.

Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Stir in the copper wire and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Nardane say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution.

## Notes from the galley

If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Folke Drummond suggests any firm fish and an apology. Leftovers keep for 8 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with dye cakes exists in Corspire and is, by treaty, never discussed here.

## Serving

If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Folke Drummond suggests any firm fish and an apology. Leftovers keep for 6 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Nardane it would be unthinkable without both.
