## About this dish

This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Vormore, written down by Oona Oduya sometime around 1963 and argued about ever since. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates spring floods, and improves when reheated. Every family in Vormore claims their own variant; this one comes from the Oona Oduya household and leans saltier than most. The dish dates to the embargo years, when lamp oil was scarce and cooks in Vormore learned to do without.

## Ingredients

- 8 onions, sliced with patience.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 4 ladles.
- About 6 handfuls of loom parts, the best you can argue your way into.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.

## Method

Cover and leave it alone for 47 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a hailstorm that stripped the orchards. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 3 minutes. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Vormore say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Stir in the glass floats and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly.

## Notes from the galley

A version made with fermented tea bricks exists in Zelrun and is, by treaty, never discussed here. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Oona Oduya suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Vormore it would be unthinkable without both. Leftovers keep for 7 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day.
