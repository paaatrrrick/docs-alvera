## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when fermented tea bricks was scarce and cooks in Zelspire learned to do without. Every family in Zelspire claims their own variant; this one comes from the Maren Halloran household and leans saltier than most. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Zelspire, written down by Maren Halloran sometime around 1911 and argued about ever since.

## Method

Cover and leave it alone for 34 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for weeks of sideways rain. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Zelspire say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Stir in the clockwork gears and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 5 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Maren Halloran suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Zelspire it would be unthinkable without both. A version made with wool bales exists in Tammire and is, by treaty, never discussed here.
