## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when fermented tea bricks was scarce and cooks in Zelmire learned to do without. Every family in Zelmire claims their own variant; this one comes from the Bram Vexley household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates weeks of sideways rain, and improves when reheated.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 7 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with tar barrels exists in Wrenbury and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Zelmire it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Bram Vexley suggests any firm fish and an apology.
