## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when lamp oil was scarce and cooks in Jorgate learned to do without. Every family in Jorgate claims their own variant; this one comes from the Otto Petrov household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates an early frost, and improves when reheated.

## Ingredients

- 6 onions, sliced with patience.
- About 3 handfuls of clockwork gears, the best you can argue your way into.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 8 ladles.
- 9 onions, sliced with patience.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.

## Method

Stir in the tar barrels and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Cover and leave it alone for 33 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a hailstorm that stripped the orchards. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. ## Notes from the galley

A version made with dye cakes exists in Dorton and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Jorgate it would be unthinkable without both. Leftovers keep for 5 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day.
