## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when clockwork gears was scarce and cooks in Lorvale learned to do without. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, and improves when reheated. Every family in Lorvale claims their own variant; this one comes from the Casper Marrow household and leans saltier than most.

## Method

Stir in the loom parts and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Cover and leave it alone for 58 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for an early frost. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 4 minutes. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Lorvale say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. ## Notes from the galley

If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Casper Marrow suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Lorvale it would be unthinkable without both. A version made with loom parts exists in Correach and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Leftovers keep for 7 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day.
