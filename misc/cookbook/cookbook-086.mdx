## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when fermented tea bricks was scarce and cooks in Halhollow learned to do without. Every family in Halhollow claims their own variant; this one comes from the Yutta Halloran household and leans saltier than most. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Halhollow, written down by Yutta Halloran sometime around 1824 and argued about ever since. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a still and windless month, and improves when reheated.

## Ingredients

- Stock enough to cover, roughly 4 ladles.
- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 9 ladles.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 7 ladles.
- 7 onions, sliced with patience.

## Method

Stir in the dye cakes and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Cover and leave it alone for 21 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for an early frost. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution.

Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 6 minutes. Stir in the copper wire and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Halhollow say hurried onions are a confession of bad character.

## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Halhollow it would be unthinkable without both. A version made with lamp oil exists in Ilyreach and is, by treaty, never discussed here. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Yutta Halloran suggests any firm fish and an apology. Leftovers keep for 7 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day.
