## About this dish

Every family in Lorshore claims their own variant; this one comes from the Sigrid Kessler household and leans saltier than most. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Lorshore, written down by Sigrid Kessler sometime around 1856 and argued about ever since. The dish dates to the embargo years, when dye cakes was scarce and cooks in Lorshore learned to do without.

## Notes from the galley

A version made with dye cakes exists in Ashspire and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Lorshore it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Sigrid Kessler suggests any firm fish and an apology. Leftovers keep for 7 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day.
