## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, and improves when reheated. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Haldane, written down by Lazlo Brandt sometime around 1805 and argued about ever since. Every family in Haldane claims their own variant; this one comes from the Lazlo Brandt household and leans saltier than most.

## Ingredients

- 3 onions, sliced with patience.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 2 ladles.

- A strip of pressed kelp paper for the lid weight, by tradition only.

## Method

Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Haldane say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Stir in the lamp oil and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next.

Cover and leave it alone for 37 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for an early frost. ## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Haldane it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Lazlo Brandt suggests any firm fish and an apology. Leftovers keep for 4 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day.
