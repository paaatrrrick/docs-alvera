## About this dish

Every family in Velshore claims their own variant; this one comes from the Oona Quist household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates an early frost, and improves when reheated. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Velshore, written down by Oona Quist sometime around 1933 and argued about ever since. The dish dates to the embargo years, when almanac reprints was scarce and cooks in Velshore learned to do without.

## Ingredients

- About 4 handfuls of almanac reprints, the best you can argue your way into.
- 4 onions, sliced with patience.
- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- About 3 handfuls of wool bales, the best you can argue your way into.
- 9 onions, sliced with patience.

## Method

Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Cover and leave it alone for 86 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a still and windless month. Stir in the almanac reprints and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Velshore say hurried onions are a confession of bad character.

## Notes from the galley

If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Oona Quist suggests any firm fish and an apology. A version made with spice tins exists in Morvale and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Velshore it would be unthinkable without both.
