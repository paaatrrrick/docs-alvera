## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when clockwork gears was scarce and cooks in Yarhollow learned to do without. Every family in Yarhollow claims their own variant; this one comes from the Joren Brandt household and leans saltier than most. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Yarhollow, written down by Joren Brandt sometime around 1826 and argued about ever since.

## Ingredients

- Stock enough to cover, roughly 6 ladles.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.
- 8 onions, sliced with patience.
- A strip of hammered tin for the lid weight, by tradition only.
- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.

## Method

Stir in the lamp oil and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Cover and leave it alone for 75 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a long fog season. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 8 minutes.

Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Stir in the copper wire and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next.

## Notes from the galley

If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Joren Brandt suggests any firm fish and an apology. Leftovers keep for 8 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with clockwork gears exists in Velspire and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Yarhollow it would be unthinkable without both.
