## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates spring floods, and improves when reheated. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Eridane, written down by Cosima Petrov sometime around 1925 and argued about ever since. Every family in Eridane claims their own variant; this one comes from the Cosima Petrov household and leans saltier than most.

## Method

Cover and leave it alone for 69 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for an early frost. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Stir in the tar barrels and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 4 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with wool bales exists in Calton and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Eridane it would be unthinkable without both.

## Serving

A version made with spice tins exists in Calton and is, by treaty, never discussed here. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Cosima Petrov suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Eridane it would be unthinkable without both.
