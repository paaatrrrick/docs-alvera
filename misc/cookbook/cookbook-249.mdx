## About this dish

Every family in Vordane claims their own variant; this one comes from the Emeric Brandt household and leans saltier than most. The dish dates to the embargo years, when tar barrels was scarce and cooks in Vordane learned to do without. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Vordane, written down by Emeric Brandt sometime around 1815 and argued about ever since. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a still and windless month, and improves when reheated.

## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Vordane it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Emeric Brandt suggests any firm fish and an apology. Leftovers keep for 5 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with preserved lemons exists in Ulmmara and is, by treaty, never discussed here.
