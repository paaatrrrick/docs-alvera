## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when loom parts was scarce and cooks in Selrun learned to do without. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Selrun, written down by Imre Holloway sometime around 1903 and argued about ever since. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates an early frost, and improves when reheated.

## Method

Stir in the fermented tea bricks and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Selrun say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. ## Notes from the galley

A version made with almanac reprints exists in Mormire and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Leftovers keep for 2 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Imre Holloway suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Selrun it would be unthinkable without both.
