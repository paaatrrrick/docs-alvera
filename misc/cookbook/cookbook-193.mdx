## About this dish

This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Torspire, written down by Ivo Mollering sometime around 1954 and argued about ever since. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a long fog season, and improves when reheated. The dish dates to the embargo years, when preserved lemons was scarce and cooks in Torspire learned to do without. Every family in Torspire claims their own variant; this one comes from the Ivo Mollering household and leans saltier than most.

## Ingredients

- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.
- A strip of bog iron for the lid weight, by tradition only.

## Method

Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 4 minutes. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Torspire say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Cover and leave it alone for 66 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for spring floods. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 3 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Torspire it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Ivo Mollering suggests any firm fish and an apology.
