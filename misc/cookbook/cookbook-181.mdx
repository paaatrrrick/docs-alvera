## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when lamp oil was scarce and cooks in Ulmford learned to do without. Every family in Ulmford claims their own variant; this one comes from the Sanna Marrow household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a still and windless month, and improves when reheated.

## Ingredients

- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- About 7 handfuls of dye cakes, the best you can argue your way into.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.
- 6 onions, sliced with patience.
- A strip of bog iron for the lid weight, by tradition only.

## Method

Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Ulmford say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 7 minutes. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 2 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Ulmford it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Sanna Marrow suggests any firm fish and an apology.
