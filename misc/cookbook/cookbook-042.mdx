## About this dish

This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Ilyspire, written down by Joren Kessler sometime around 1919 and argued about ever since. The dish dates to the embargo years, when preserved lemons was scarce and cooks in Ilyspire learned to do without. Every family in Ilyspire claims their own variant; this one comes from the Joren Kessler household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates the dry easterlies, and improves when reheated.

## Ingredients

- 9 onions, sliced with patience.
- 2 onions, sliced with patience.
- A strip of woven reed for the lid weight, by tradition only.
- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 7 ladles.

## Method

Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 2 minutes. Cover and leave it alone for 86 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a long fog season. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Ilyspire say hurried onions are a confession of bad character.

## Notes from the galley

A version made with fermented tea bricks exists in Jorfell and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Leftovers keep for 6 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Ilyspire it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Joren Kessler suggests any firm fish and an apology.
