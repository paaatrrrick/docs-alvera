## About this dish

Every family in Quilmara claims their own variant; this one comes from the Joren Halloran household and leans saltier than most. The dish dates to the embargo years, when lamp oil was scarce and cooks in Quilmara learned to do without. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Quilmara, written down by Joren Halloran sometime around 1876 and argued about ever since.

## Ingredients

- Stock enough to cover, roughly 6 ladles.
- 5 onions, sliced with patience.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 8 ladles.

## Method

Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Cover and leave it alone for 90 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for weeks of sideways rain. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Stir in the clockwork gears and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly.

## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 6 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Quilmara it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Joren Halloran suggests any firm fish and an apology.
