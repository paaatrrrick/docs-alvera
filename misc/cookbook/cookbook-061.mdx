## About this dish

Every family in Dorgate claims their own variant; this one comes from the Sanna Voss household and leans saltier than most. The dish dates to the embargo years, when lamp oil was scarce and cooks in Dorgate learned to do without. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Dorgate, written down by Sanna Voss sometime around 1935 and argued about ever since.

## Ingredients

- 2 onions, sliced with patience.
- A strip of lacquered birch for the lid weight, by tradition only.
- 3 onions, sliced with patience.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 7 ladles.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 3 ladles.

## Method

Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Dorgate say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Stir in the loom parts and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Cover and leave it alone for 89 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for spring floods. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 2 minutes.

Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Stir in the lamp oil and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next.

## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Dorgate it would be unthinkable without both. A version made with fermented tea bricks exists in Zelford and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Leftovers keep for 8 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day.

## Serving

Leftovers keep for 2 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Dorgate it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Sanna Voss suggests any firm fish and an apology.
