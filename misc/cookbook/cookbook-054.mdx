## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates an early frost, and improves when reheated. Every family in Ericrest claims their own variant; this one comes from the Cosima Petrov household and leans saltier than most. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Ericrest, written down by Cosima Petrov sometime around 1810 and argued about ever since. The dish dates to the embargo years, when almanac reprints was scarce and cooks in Ericrest learned to do without.

## Method

Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 6 minutes. Cover and leave it alone for 84 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for the autumn king tides. Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Ericrest say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Stir in the loom parts and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next.

Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. ## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 9 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with clockwork gears exists in Ashvale and is, by treaty, never discussed here. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Ericrest it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Cosima Petrov suggests any firm fish and an apology.
