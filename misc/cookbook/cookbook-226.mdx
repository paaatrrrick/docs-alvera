## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a long fog season, and improves when reheated. Every family in Delrun claims their own variant; this one comes from the Cosima Kessler household and leans saltier than most. The dish dates to the embargo years, when glass floats was scarce and cooks in Delrun learned to do without.

## Ingredients

- Stock enough to cover, roughly 5 ladles.
- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.
- 4 onions, sliced with patience.
- About 4 handfuls of glass floats, the best you can argue your way into.

## Method

Cover and leave it alone for 13 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for the dry easterlies. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 7 minutes. Stir in the tar barrels and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly.

## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Delrun it would be unthinkable without both. Leftovers keep for 2 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Cosima Kessler suggests any firm fish and an apology. A version made with lamp oil exists in Quilspire and is, by treaty, never discussed here.
