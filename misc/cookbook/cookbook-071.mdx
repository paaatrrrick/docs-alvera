## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when dye cakes was scarce and cooks in Veldane learned to do without. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Veldane, written down by Anselm Halloran sometime around 1826 and argued about ever since. Every family in Veldane claims their own variant; this one comes from the Anselm Halloran household and leans saltier than most.

## Ingredients

- About 6 handfuls of lamp oil, the best you can argue your way into.
- A strip of river-glass for the lid weight, by tradition only.
- 2 onions, sliced with patience.
- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- A strip of blue slate for the lid weight, by tradition only.

## Method

Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Veldane say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 8 minutes. Stir in the clockwork gears and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Cover and leave it alone for 16 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a long fog season.

Begin by rendering the fat slowly over a low flame until the kitchen smells faintly of the quayside, about 6 minutes. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. Cover and leave it alone for 30 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a hailstorm that stripped the orchards. Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next.

## Notes from the galley

A version made with glass floats exists in Calspire and is, by treaty, never discussed here. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Anselm Halloran suggests any firm fish and an apology. Leftovers keep for 3 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Veldane it would be unthinkable without both.
