## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when lamp oil was scarce and cooks in Vorspire learned to do without. Every family in Vorspire claims their own variant; this one comes from the Oona Brandt household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, and improves when reheated. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Vorspire, written down by Oona Brandt sometime around 1857 and argued about ever since.

## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Vorspire it would be unthinkable without both. Leftovers keep for 2 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Oona Brandt suggests any firm fish and an apology.
