## About this dish

The dish dates to the embargo years, when tar barrels was scarce and cooks in Calcrest learned to do without. Every family in Calcrest claims their own variant; this one comes from the Casper Vexley household and leans saltier than most. Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates the autumn king tides, and improves when reheated.

## Ingredients

- 5 onions, sliced with patience.
- A knuckle of smoked fat, or what the larder offers.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.
- Stock enough to cover, roughly 5 ladles.
- A strip of wax-sealed canvas for the lid weight, by tradition only.

## Method

Pour in the stock in three additions, letting each one fall quiet before the next. Stir in the preserved lemons and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Cover and leave it alone for 57 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for a hailstorm that stripped the orchards. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution. ## Notes from the galley

Leftovers keep for 9 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Casper Vexley suggests any firm fish and an apology. Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Calcrest it would be unthinkable without both.
