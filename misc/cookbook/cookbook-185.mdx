## About this dish

Barge cooks prize this dish because it needs one pot, tolerates the dry easterlies, and improves when reheated. This is the version served at the lock-keeper tavern in Lorrun, written down by Petra Mollering sometime around 1939 and argued about ever since. The dish dates to the embargo years, when copper wire was scarce and cooks in Lorrun learned to do without.

## Ingredients

- A strip of whale-bone veneer for the lid weight, by tradition only.
- 6 onions, sliced with patience.
- About 4 handfuls of glass floats, the best you can argue your way into.
- Vinegar, salt, and a hard pepper, ground coarse.
- 3 onions, sliced with patience.

## Method

Add the onions and a generous pinch of salt, and do not rush them; the cooks of Lorrun say hurried onions are a confession of bad character. Cover and leave it alone for 79 minutes, resisting all temptation; lifting the lid is traditionally blamed for spring floods. Stir in the clockwork gears and let it toast against the bottom of the pot until it catches just slightly. Finish with vinegar off the heat, taste, and adjust with more conviction than caution.

## Notes from the galley

Serve with dense bread and pickles; in Lorrun it would be unthinkable without both. If you cannot find the proper smoked variety, Petra Mollering suggests any firm fish and an apology. Leftovers keep for 3 days in a cool larder and are widely considered better on the second day. A version made with lamp oil exists in Vordane and is, by treaty, never discussed here.
