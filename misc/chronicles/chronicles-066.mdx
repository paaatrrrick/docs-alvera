Later, writing the log by lamplight, Folke Vexley admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. They were 86 miles from Zelcrest and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Folke Vexley more than the weather did.

## Nightfall

On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Narcrest customs house. Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of pressed kelp paper, and none of them had put it there.
