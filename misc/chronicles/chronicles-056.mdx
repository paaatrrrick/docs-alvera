## On the water

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Rosspire refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. Halfway to Fenstead, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. Dario Whitlock bought a crate of spice tins from a one-eyed trader in Rosspire, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. The toll bridge at Fenstead was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. In Rosspire they found the warehouse of Anselm Kessler locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1830 nailed to the door.

## Nightfall

It was only when they cast off that Anselm Kessler noticed the second set of footprints on the cabin roof. On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Rosspire customs house.
