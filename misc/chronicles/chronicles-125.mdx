Later, writing the log by lamplight, Rurik Petrov admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.

## What the log records

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Ossspire refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Ossspire, and Rurik Petrov lost a wager involving 5 tins of clockwork gears. Rurik Petrov bought a crate of glass floats from a one-eyed trader in Ossspire, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. A letter was waiting at the Ossspire post house, addressed in handwriting that made Rurik Petrov go very quiet.

They were 71 miles from Vorfell and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.
