## On the water

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Selcrest refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. A letter was waiting at the Selcrest post house, addressed in handwriting that made Yutta Quist go very quiet. the autumn king tides caught them in the open stretch past Selcrest, and the cargo of wool bales shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. The engine coughed twice and died outside Selcrest, and Yutta Quist fixed it with a strip of pressed kelp paper and unrepeatable language.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.

## Nightfall

Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of bog iron, and none of them had put it there. On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Selcrest customs house.
