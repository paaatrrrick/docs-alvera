The engine coughed twice and died outside Tamfell, and Maren Vexley fixed it with a strip of wax-sealed canvas and unrepeatable language. A letter was waiting at the Tamfell post house, addressed in handwriting that made Maren Vexley go very quiet. the autumn king tides caught them in the open stretch past Tamfell, and the cargo of glass floats shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. Maren Vexley bought a crate of spice tins from a one-eyed trader in Tamfell, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it.

At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Tamfell, and Maren Vexley lost a wager involving 9 tins of dye cakes. They took on a passenger at Tamfell: Yutta Marchetti, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. a still and windless month caught them in the open stretch past Tamfell, and the cargo of preserved lemons shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

## On the water

The toll bridge at Ilydane was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Tamfell refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. Halfway to Ilydane, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. A letter was waiting at the Tamfell post house, addressed in handwriting that made Maren Vexley go very quiet.

Later, writing the log by lamplight, Maren Vexley admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.

## What the log records

The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Maren Vexley more than the weather did. They were 85 miles from Ilydane and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.

## Nightfall

Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of wax-sealed canvas, and none of them had put it there. On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Tamfell customs house.
