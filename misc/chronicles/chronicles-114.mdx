The toll bridge at Torshore was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. A letter was waiting at the Quilholm post house, addressed in handwriting that made Mireille Quist go very quiet. They took on a passenger at Quilholm: Mireille Petrov, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind.

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Quilholm refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. Mireille Quist bought a crate of loom parts from a one-eyed trader in Quilholm, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. ## What the log records
