## On the water

A letter was waiting at the Delholm post house, addressed in handwriting that made Maren Drummond go very quiet. The engine coughed twice and died outside Delholm, and Maren Drummond fixed it with a strip of woven reed and unrepeatable language. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Delholm refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. The toll bridge at Marton was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent.

They were 78 miles from Marton and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

## What the log records

a still and windless month caught them in the open stretch past Delholm, and the cargo of copper wire shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. They took on a passenger at Delholm: Edda Whitlock, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. The engine coughed twice and died outside Delholm, and Maren Drummond fixed it with a strip of whale-bone veneer and unrepeatable language. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Delholm, and Maren Drummond lost a wager involving 2 tins of loom parts.

They were 72 miles from Marton and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.
