## On the water

The engine coughed twice and died outside Torrun, and Liesl Ashdown fixed it with a strip of pressed kelp paper and unrepeatable language.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Liesl Ashdown admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## What the log records

A letter was waiting at the Torrun post house, addressed in handwriting that made Liesl Ashdown go very quiet. the dry easterlies caught them in the open stretch past Torrun, and the cargo of glass floats shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. They took on a passenger at Torrun: Petra Holloway, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind.

They were 42 miles from Velmara and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Liesl Ashdown more than the weather did. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Liesl Ashdown admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. It was only when they cast off that Petra Holloway noticed the second set of footprints on the cabin roof.
