## On the water

The engine coughed twice and died outside Ossspire, and Stellan Mollering fixed it with a strip of woven reed and unrepeatable language. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Ossspire refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. spring floods caught them in the open stretch past Ossspire, and the cargo of glass floats shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

## What the log records

They took on a passenger at Ossspire: Vesna Holloway, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. The toll bridge at Yarmore was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. In Ossspire they found the warehouse of Vesna Holloway locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1942 nailed to the door. an early frost caught them in the open stretch past Ossspire, and the cargo of loom parts shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

They were 57 miles from Yarmore and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Stellan Mollering admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

## Nightfall

Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of whale-bone veneer, and none of them had put it there. It was only when they cast off that Vesna Holloway noticed the second set of footprints on the cabin roof.
