## What the log records

Halfway to Brengate, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. The toll bridge at Brengate was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. a long fog season caught them in the open stretch past Galmore, and the cargo of dye cakes shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

Later, writing the log by lamplight, Sanna Ashdown admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. They were 57 miles from Brengate and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Sanna Ashdown more than the weather did.

## Nightfall

It was only when they cast off that Lazlo Marrow noticed the second set of footprints on the cabin roof. The next entry in the log is in different handwriting.
