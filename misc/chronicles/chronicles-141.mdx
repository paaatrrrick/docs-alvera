## On the water

The engine coughed twice and died outside Corbury, and Vesna Falkner fixed it with a strip of wax-sealed canvas and unrepeatable language. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Corbury refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. They took on a passenger at Corbury: Anselm Petrov, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. The toll bridge at Corbury was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent.

Later, writing the log by lamplight, Vesna Falkner admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Vesna Falkner more than the weather did. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.
