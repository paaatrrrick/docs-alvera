## On the water

Halfway to Yarwick, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. They took on a passenger at Brenton: Halvard Oduya, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. In Brenton they found the warehouse of Halvard Oduya locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1888 nailed to the door. A letter was waiting at the Brenton post house, addressed in handwriting that made Lazlo Greaves go very quiet.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Lazlo Greaves more than the weather did. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Lazlo Greaves admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.
