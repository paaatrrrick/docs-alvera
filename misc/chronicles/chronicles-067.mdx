## On the water

A letter was waiting at the Ossstead post house, addressed in handwriting that made Edda Vexley go very quiet. Halfway to Fendane, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Ossstead, and Edda Vexley lost a wager involving 3 tins of clockwork gears. In Ossstead they found the warehouse of Otto Mollering locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1796 nailed to the door. an early frost caught them in the open stretch past Ossstead, and the cargo of almanac reprints shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Edda Vexley more than the weather did. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Edda Vexley admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.
