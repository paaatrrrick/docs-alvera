Yutta Mollering bought a crate of wool bales from a one-eyed trader in Eribury, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it.

The toll bridge at Yarmore was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Eribury, and Yutta Mollering lost a wager involving 3 tins of spice tins. Halfway to Yarmore, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. A letter was waiting at the Eribury post house, addressed in handwriting that made Yutta Mollering go very quiet.

In Eribury they found the warehouse of Joren Petrov locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1847 nailed to the door.

They were 74 miles from Yarmore and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Yutta Mollering more than the weather did. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Yutta Mollering admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of wax-sealed canvas, and none of them had put it there.
