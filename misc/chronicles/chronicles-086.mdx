Halfway to Erimore, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Dorholm refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Dorholm, and Oona Voss lost a wager involving 9 tins of almanac reprints.

## On the water

a long fog season caught them in the open stretch past Dorholm, and the cargo of wool bales shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. A letter was waiting at the Dorholm post house, addressed in handwriting that made Oona Voss go very quiet. Oona Voss bought a crate of preserved lemons from a one-eyed trader in Dorholm, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Oona Voss more than the weather did. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Oona Voss admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. The next entry in the log is in different handwriting.
