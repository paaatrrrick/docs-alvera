## What the log records

The engine coughed twice and died outside Morcrest, and Emeric Vexley fixed it with a strip of blue slate and unrepeatable language. The toll bridge at Delshore was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Morcrest refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing.

Later, writing the log by lamplight, Emeric Vexley admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Emeric Vexley more than the weather did.
