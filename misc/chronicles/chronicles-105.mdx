## On the water

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Morholm refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. They took on a passenger at Morholm: Lazlo Vexley, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. Halfway to Halwick, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Morholm, and Sigrid Marrow lost a wager involving 3 tins of wool bales. The toll bridge at Halwick was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent.

Later, writing the log by lamplight, Sigrid Marrow admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. They were 18 miles from Halwick and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## Nightfall

It was only when they cast off that Lazlo Vexley noticed the second set of footprints on the cabin roof. The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched.
