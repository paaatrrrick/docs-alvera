## On the water

They took on a passenger at Perwick: Oona Sorvane, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. The engine coughed twice and died outside Perwick, and Emeric Severin fixed it with a strip of salt-cured oak and unrepeatable language. The toll bridge at Corhollow was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. Emeric Severin bought a crate of clockwork gears from a one-eyed trader in Perwick, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Emeric Severin more than the weather did. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.
