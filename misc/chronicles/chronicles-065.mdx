The toll bridge at Zelholm was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Delton refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. The engine coughed twice and died outside Delton, and Otto Kessler fixed it with a strip of rope-fiber composite and unrepeatable language. Otto Kessler bought a crate of fermented tea bricks from a one-eyed trader in Delton, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it.
