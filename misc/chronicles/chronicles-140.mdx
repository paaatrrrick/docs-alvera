## On the water

Halfway to Halton, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Vormara refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. They were 22 miles from Halton and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.
