## On the water

In Delmara they found the warehouse of Greta Holloway locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1894 nailed to the door. They took on a passenger at Delmara: Greta Holloway, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. The engine coughed twice and died outside Delmara, and Oona Marchetti fixed it with a strip of bog iron and unrepeatable language. A letter was waiting at the Delmara post house, addressed in handwriting that made Oona Marchetti go very quiet.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. They were 60 miles from Wrenshore and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## What the log records

In Delmara they found the warehouse of Greta Holloway locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1844 nailed to the door. They took on a passenger at Delmara: Greta Holloway, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. The toll bridge at Wrenshore was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Oona Marchetti admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

The next entry in the log is in different handwriting. The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched.
