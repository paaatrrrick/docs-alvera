## What the log records

At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Calholm, and Emeric Oduya lost a wager involving 2 tins of loom parts.

The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Emeric Oduya more than the weather did. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Emeric Oduya admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Calholm customs house.
