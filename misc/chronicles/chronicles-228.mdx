## On the water

They took on a passenger at Ossdane: Sigrid Drummond, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Ossdane refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. the dry easterlies caught them in the open stretch past Ossdane, and the cargo of loom parts shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. A letter was waiting at the Ossdane post house, addressed in handwriting that made Stellan Drummond go very quiet. In Ossdane they found the warehouse of Sigrid Drummond locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1881 nailed to the door.

The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Stellan Drummond more than the weather did. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Stellan Drummond admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## What the log records

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. They were 43 miles from Selton and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## Nightfall

It was only when they cast off that Sigrid Drummond noticed the second set of footprints on the cabin roof. Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of woven reed, and none of them had put it there.
