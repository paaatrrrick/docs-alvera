## On the water

Petra Kessler bought a crate of dye cakes from a one-eyed trader in Tamdane, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Tamdane refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. A letter was waiting at the Tamdane post house, addressed in handwriting that made Petra Kessler go very quiet. spring floods caught them in the open stretch past Tamdane, and the cargo of clockwork gears shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Petra Kessler more than the weather did. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Petra Kessler admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. They were 45 miles from Ashcrest and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## What the log records

At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Tamdane, and Petra Kessler lost a wager involving 4 tins of spice tins. a long fog season caught them in the open stretch past Tamdane, and the cargo of loom parts shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. Halfway to Ashcrest, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars.

They were 42 miles from Ashcrest and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

## Nightfall

On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Tamdane customs house. The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched.
