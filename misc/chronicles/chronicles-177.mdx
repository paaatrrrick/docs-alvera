## On the water

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Marvale refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. an early frost caught them in the open stretch past Marvale, and the cargo of copper wire shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. The engine coughed twice and died outside Marvale, and Lazlo Vexley fixed it with a strip of pressed kelp paper and unrepeatable language. Lazlo Vexley bought a crate of almanac reprints from a one-eyed trader in Marvale, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. They took on a passenger at Marvale: Greta Quist, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Lazlo Vexley more than the weather did.

## What the log records

A letter was waiting at the Marvale post house, addressed in handwriting that made Lazlo Vexley go very quiet. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Marvale, and Lazlo Vexley lost a wager involving 4 tins of clockwork gears. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Marvale refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing.

They were 20 miles from Jordane and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Lazlo Vexley admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

It was only when they cast off that Greta Quist noticed the second set of footprints on the cabin roof. The next entry in the log is in different handwriting.
