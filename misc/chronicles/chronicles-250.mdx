## On the water

Halfway to Morcrest, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. a still and windless month caught them in the open stretch past Ulmvik, and the cargo of fermented tea bricks shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. They took on a passenger at Ulmvik: Lazlo Greaves, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. In Ulmvik they found the warehouse of Lazlo Greaves locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1825 nailed to the door. The engine coughed twice and died outside Ulmvik, and Greta Falkner fixed it with a strip of blue slate and unrepeatable language.

They were 19 miles from Morcrest and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.
