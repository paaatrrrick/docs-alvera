Halvard Falkner bought a crate of dye cakes from a one-eyed trader in Rosfell, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it.

The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Halvard Falkner more than the weather did. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.

## What the log records

the autumn king tides caught them in the open stretch past Rosfell, and the cargo of preserved lemons shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. Halvard Falkner bought a crate of almanac reprints from a one-eyed trader in Rosfell, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. The toll bridge at Cormara was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. In Rosfell they found the warehouse of Stellan Marrow locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1827 nailed to the door.

Later, writing the log by lamplight, Halvard Falkner admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. They were 77 miles from Cormara and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Rosfell customs house.
