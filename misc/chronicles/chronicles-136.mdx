## On the water

The toll bridge at Delreach was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Wrengate refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Wrengate, and Edda Whitlock lost a wager involving 6 tins of clockwork gears. the autumn king tides caught them in the open stretch past Wrengate, and the cargo of wool bales shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

They were 40 miles from Delreach and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Edda Whitlock admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## What the log records

A letter was waiting at the Wrengate post house, addressed in handwriting that made Edda Whitlock go very quiet. The engine coughed twice and died outside Wrengate, and Edda Whitlock fixed it with a strip of whale-bone veneer and unrepeatable language. weeks of sideways rain caught them in the open stretch past Wrengate, and the cargo of almanac reprints shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. They were 22 miles from Delreach and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.

## Nightfall

On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Wrengate customs house. The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched.
