## On the water

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Calshore refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Calshore, and Sigrid Lindqvist lost a wager involving 6 tins of glass floats. The toll bridge at Velrun was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. They took on a passenger at Calshore: Otto Halloran, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. A letter was waiting at the Calshore post house, addressed in handwriting that made Sigrid Lindqvist go very quiet.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. They were 24 miles from Velrun and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. It was only when they cast off that Otto Halloran noticed the second set of footprints on the cabin roof.
