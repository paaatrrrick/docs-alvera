## On the water

The engine coughed twice and died outside Yarwick, and Sanna Falkner fixed it with a strip of salt-cured oak and unrepeatable language. the dry easterlies caught them in the open stretch past Yarwick, and the cargo of fermented tea bricks shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. A letter was waiting at the Yarwick post house, addressed in handwriting that made Sanna Falkner go very quiet. They took on a passenger at Yarwick: Sanna Greaves, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind.

They were 75 miles from Delstead and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

## Nightfall

Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of fired clay, and none of them had put it there. On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Yarwick customs house.
