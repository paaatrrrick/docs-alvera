They took on a passenger at Vorwick: Maren Lindqvist, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind.

A letter was waiting at the Vorwick post house, addressed in handwriting that made Sigrid Falkner go very quiet. In Vorwick they found the warehouse of Maren Lindqvist locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1819 nailed to the door.
