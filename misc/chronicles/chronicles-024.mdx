## On the water

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Narbury refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. They took on a passenger at Narbury: Oona Severin, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. The engine coughed twice and died outside Narbury, and Sigrid Oduya fixed it with a strip of whale-bone veneer and unrepeatable language. The toll bridge at Eridane was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Sigrid Oduya admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of lacquered birch, and none of them had put it there. The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched.
