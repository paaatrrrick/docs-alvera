At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Corrun, and Folke Brandt lost a wager involving 4 tins of preserved lemons. Folke Brandt bought a crate of preserved lemons from a one-eyed trader in Corrun, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. The engine coughed twice and died outside Corrun, and Folke Brandt fixed it with a strip of lacquered birch and unrepeatable language. They took on a passenger at Corrun: Folke Brandt, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind.

The toll bridge at Ilyreach was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. In Corrun they found the warehouse of Folke Brandt locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1810 nailed to the door. weeks of sideways rain caught them in the open stretch past Corrun, and the cargo of copper wire shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

## On the water

a hailstorm that stripped the orchards caught them in the open stretch past Corrun, and the cargo of glass floats shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. Halfway to Ilyreach, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Corrun, and Folke Brandt lost a wager involving 9 tins of spice tins. A letter was waiting at the Corrun post house, addressed in handwriting that made Folke Brandt go very quiet.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Folke Brandt more than the weather did. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Corrun customs house.
