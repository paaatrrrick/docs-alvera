## On the water

The engine coughed twice and died outside Perbury, and Cosima Petrov fixed it with a strip of hammered tin and unrepeatable language. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Perbury refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. In Perbury they found the warehouse of Folke Oduya locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1820 nailed to the door. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Perbury, and Cosima Petrov lost a wager involving 8 tins of fermented tea bricks. Halfway to Galfell, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars.

Later, writing the log by lamplight, Cosima Petrov admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.

## What the log records

They were 81 miles from Galfell and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Cosima Petrov admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

## Nightfall

On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Perbury customs house. It was only when they cast off that Folke Oduya noticed the second set of footprints on the cabin roof.
