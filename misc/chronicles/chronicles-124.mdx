## On the water

The toll bridge at Morton was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. The engine coughed twice and died outside Velwick, and Rurik Sorvane fixed it with a strip of fired clay and unrepeatable language. weeks of sideways rain caught them in the open stretch past Velwick, and the cargo of almanac reprints shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. They were 81 miles from Morton and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.
