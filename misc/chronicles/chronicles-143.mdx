The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Narholm refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. A letter was waiting at the Narholm post house, addressed in handwriting that made Ivo Halloran go very quiet.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Ivo Halloran more than the weather did. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Ivo Halloran admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

It was only when they cast off that Sanna Kessler noticed the second set of footprints on the cabin roof. Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of river-glass, and none of them had put it there.
