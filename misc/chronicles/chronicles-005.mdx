## On the water

They took on a passenger at Rosmire: Beatrix Mollering, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. The engine coughed twice and died outside Rosmire, and Greta Oduya fixed it with a strip of whale-bone veneer and unrepeatable language. Greta Oduya bought a crate of dye cakes from a one-eyed trader in Rosmire, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. The toll bridge at Ilyreach was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent.

Later, writing the log by lamplight, Greta Oduya admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. They were 54 miles from Ilyreach and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## What the log records

Halfway to Ilyreach, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. In Rosmire they found the warehouse of Beatrix Mollering locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1958 nailed to the door. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Rosmire refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. The toll bridge at Ilyreach was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent.

They were 12 miles from Ilyreach and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Greta Oduya more than the weather did.

## Nightfall

The next entry in the log is in different handwriting. Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of woven reed, and none of them had put it there.
