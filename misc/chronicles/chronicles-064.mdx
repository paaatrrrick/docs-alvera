## On the water

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Keldane refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. A letter was waiting at the Keldane post house, addressed in handwriting that made Sigrid Holloway go very quiet. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Keldane, and Sigrid Holloway lost a wager involving 8 tins of fermented tea bricks. They took on a passenger at Keldane: Stellan Voss, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. spring floods caught them in the open stretch past Keldane, and the cargo of preserved lemons shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Sigrid Holloway more than the weather did. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

## What the log records

The toll bridge at Selton was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. Halfway to Selton, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Keldane, and Sigrid Holloway lost a wager involving 7 tins of dye cakes. The engine coughed twice and died outside Keldane, and Sigrid Holloway fixed it with a strip of whale-bone veneer and unrepeatable language.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Sigrid Holloway admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Keldane customs house. Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of river-glass, and none of them had put it there.
