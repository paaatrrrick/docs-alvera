A letter was waiting at the Narspire post house, addressed in handwriting that made Tilda Voss go very quiet. In Narspire they found the warehouse of Annik Vexley locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1931 nailed to the door. The engine coughed twice and died outside Narspire, and Tilda Voss fixed it with a strip of pressed kelp paper and unrepeatable language.

At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Narspire, and Tilda Voss lost a wager involving 7 tins of almanac reprints. The toll bridge at Kelton was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. a still and windless month caught them in the open stretch past Narspire, and the cargo of loom parts shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. Tilda Voss bought a crate of copper wire from a one-eyed trader in Narspire, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

## Nightfall

It was only when they cast off that Annik Vexley noticed the second set of footprints on the cabin roof. Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of wax-sealed canvas, and none of them had put it there.
