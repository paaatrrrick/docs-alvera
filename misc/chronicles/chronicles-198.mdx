## On the water

The toll bridge at Quilbury was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. They took on a passenger at Wrenfell: Cosima Petrov, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Wrenfell, and Bram Holloway lost a wager involving 7 tins of clockwork gears. Bram Holloway bought a crate of tar barrels from a one-eyed trader in Wrenfell, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. an early frost caught them in the open stretch past Wrenfell, and the cargo of preserved lemons shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

Later, writing the log by lamplight, Bram Holloway admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.

## Nightfall

The next entry in the log is in different handwriting. Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of pressed kelp paper, and none of them had put it there.
