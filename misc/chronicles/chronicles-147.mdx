## On the water

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Mormara refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Mormara, and Cosima Severin lost a wager involving 4 tins of copper wire. The engine coughed twice and died outside Mormara, and Cosima Severin fixed it with a strip of rope-fiber composite and unrepeatable language. In Mormara they found the warehouse of Dario Mollering locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1819 nailed to the door.

They were 81 miles from Erigate and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Cosima Severin admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.
