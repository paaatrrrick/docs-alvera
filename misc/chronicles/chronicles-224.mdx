At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Fenford, and Yutta Vexley lost a wager involving 6 tins of copper wire. The engine coughed twice and died outside Fenford, and Yutta Vexley fixed it with a strip of fired clay and unrepeatable language. They took on a passenger at Fenford: Beatrix Holloway, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind.

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Fenford refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. weeks of sideways rain caught them in the open stretch past Fenford, and the cargo of lamp oil shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. ## On the water

Halfway to Erimire, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. In Fenford they found the warehouse of Beatrix Holloway locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1844 nailed to the door. Yutta Vexley bought a crate of glass floats from a one-eyed trader in Fenford, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. A letter was waiting at the Fenford post house, addressed in handwriting that made Yutta Vexley go very quiet.

The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Yutta Vexley more than the weather did. They were 16 miles from Erimire and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.
