## On the water

A letter was waiting at the Quilmara post house, addressed in handwriting that made Lazlo Holloway go very quiet. Halfway to Marmore, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars.

Later, writing the log by lamplight, Lazlo Holloway admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. They were 29 miles from Marmore and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.
