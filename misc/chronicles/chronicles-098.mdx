## On the water

The toll bridge at Perfell was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. The engine coughed twice and died outside Ilystead, and Anselm Sorvane fixed it with a strip of rope-fiber composite and unrepeatable language. Anselm Sorvane bought a crate of spice tins from a one-eyed trader in Ilystead, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Ilystead, and Anselm Sorvane lost a wager involving 2 tins of spice tins. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Ilystead refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Anselm Sorvane more than the weather did. They were 26 miles from Perfell and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## What the log records

A letter was waiting at the Ilystead post house, addressed in handwriting that made Anselm Sorvane go very quiet. Halfway to Perfell, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Ilystead, and Anselm Sorvane lost a wager involving 6 tins of preserved lemons.

They were 14 miles from Perfell and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Ilystead customs house.
