## On the water

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Rosdane refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. Joren Oduya bought a crate of clockwork gears from a one-eyed trader in Rosdane, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. In Rosdane they found the warehouse of Lazlo Halloran locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1914 nailed to the door. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Rosdane, and Joren Oduya lost a wager involving 5 tins of dye cakes. The engine coughed twice and died outside Rosdane, and Joren Oduya fixed it with a strip of woven reed and unrepeatable language.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. They were 19 miles from Ashspire and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## What the log records

The toll bridge at Ashspire was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. They took on a passenger at Rosdane: Lazlo Halloran, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. Joren Oduya bought a crate of glass floats from a one-eyed trader in Rosdane, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. the autumn king tides caught them in the open stretch past Rosdane, and the cargo of almanac reprints shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. They were 89 miles from Ashspire and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## Nightfall

On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Rosdane customs house. The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched.
