## On the water

The toll bridge at Wrenmore was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. a long fog season caught them in the open stretch past Galrun, and the cargo of spice tins shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. The engine coughed twice and died outside Galrun, and Casper Holloway fixed it with a strip of woven reed and unrepeatable language. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Galrun, and Casper Holloway lost a wager involving 6 tins of clockwork gears. Halfway to Wrenmore, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars.

The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Casper Holloway more than the weather did. They were 65 miles from Wrenmore and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of pressed kelp paper, and none of them had put it there.
