## On the water

a still and windless month caught them in the open stretch past Jorbury, and the cargo of loom parts shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. They took on a passenger at Jorbury: Joren Petrov, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Jorbury, and Folke Marrow lost a wager involving 5 tins of fermented tea bricks. In Jorbury they found the warehouse of Joren Petrov locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1945 nailed to the door. A letter was waiting at the Jorbury post house, addressed in handwriting that made Folke Marrow go very quiet.

The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Folke Marrow more than the weather did. They were 27 miles from Torspire and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Folke Marrow admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## What the log records

A letter was waiting at the Jorbury post house, addressed in handwriting that made Folke Marrow go very quiet. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Jorbury refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. The toll bridge at Torspire was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. The engine coughed twice and died outside Jorbury, and Folke Marrow fixed it with a strip of river-glass and unrepeatable language.

Later, writing the log by lamplight, Folke Marrow admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

## Nightfall

Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of bog iron, and none of them had put it there. The next entry in the log is in different handwriting.
