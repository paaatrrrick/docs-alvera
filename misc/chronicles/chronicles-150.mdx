## On the water

The engine coughed twice and died outside Narreach, and Stellan Marchetti fixed it with a strip of bog iron and unrepeatable language. In Narreach they found the warehouse of Tilda Mollering locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1940 nailed to the door. They took on a passenger at Narreach: Tilda Mollering, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind.

Later, writing the log by lamplight, Stellan Marchetti admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Stellan Marchetti more than the weather did. They were 64 miles from Wrenstead and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.
