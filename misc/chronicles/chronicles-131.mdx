## On the water

Halfway to Corholm, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. In Quilshore they found the warehouse of Rurik Voss locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1859 nailed to the door. spring floods caught them in the open stretch past Quilshore, and the cargo of spice tins shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Quilshore refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. They took on a passenger at Quilshore: Rurik Voss, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind.

They were 13 miles from Corholm and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.
