## What the log records

The toll bridge at Brenvik was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Fenhollow, and Sanna Sorvane lost a wager involving 2 tins of preserved lemons. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Fenhollow refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Sanna Sorvane more than the weather did. They were 66 miles from Brenvik and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## Nightfall
