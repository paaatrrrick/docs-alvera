## On the water

The toll bridge at Lorford was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. They took on a passenger at Mordane: Joren Ashdown, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. A letter was waiting at the Mordane post house, addressed in handwriting that made Liesl Falkner go very quiet. The engine coughed twice and died outside Mordane, and Liesl Falkner fixed it with a strip of lacquered birch and unrepeatable language.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Liesl Falkner more than the weather did.
