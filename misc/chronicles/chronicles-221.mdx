Later, writing the log by lamplight, Casper Drummond admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. They were 86 miles from Fenmire and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## What the log records

The engine coughed twice and died outside Halcrest, and Casper Drummond fixed it with a strip of fired clay and unrepeatable language. The toll bridge at Fenmire was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Casper Drummond more than the weather did. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.

## Nightfall

Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of river-glass, and none of them had put it there. The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched.
