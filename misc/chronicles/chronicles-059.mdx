The toll bridge at Brenfell was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. Anselm Sorvane bought a crate of wool bales from a one-eyed trader in Dorshore, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.

They were 39 miles from Brenfell and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Anselm Sorvane more than the weather did. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Anselm Sorvane admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

It was only when they cast off that Bram Marrow noticed the second set of footprints on the cabin roof. The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched.
