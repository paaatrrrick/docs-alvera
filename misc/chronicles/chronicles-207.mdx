## On the water

Halfway to Jorton, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. They took on a passenger at Lorspire: Maren Vexley, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. The toll bridge at Jorton was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. In Lorspire they found the warehouse of Maren Vexley locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1794 nailed to the door. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Lorspire refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Sigrid Halloran admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.

## Nightfall

Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of rope-fiber composite, and none of them had put it there. The next entry in the log is in different handwriting.
