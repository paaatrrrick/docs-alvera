## On the water

At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Torford, and Annik Halloran lost a wager involving 7 tins of dye cakes. weeks of sideways rain caught them in the open stretch past Torford, and the cargo of dye cakes shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. Annik Halloran bought a crate of preserved lemons from a one-eyed trader in Torford, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. A letter was waiting at the Torford post house, addressed in handwriting that made Annik Halloran go very quiet.

They were 58 miles from Mordane and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

## What the log records

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Torford refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing.

They were 35 miles from Mordane and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Annik Halloran admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

The next entry in the log is in different handwriting. The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched.
