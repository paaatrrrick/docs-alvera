## On the water

Halfway to Vorshore, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. A letter was waiting at the Quilhollow post house, addressed in handwriting that made Dario Holloway go very quiet. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Quilhollow, and Dario Holloway lost a wager involving 2 tins of tar barrels. The toll bridge at Vorshore was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. The engine coughed twice and died outside Quilhollow, and Dario Holloway fixed it with a strip of rope-fiber composite and unrepeatable language.

They were 75 miles from Vorshore and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Dario Holloway more than the weather did.

## What the log records

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Quilhollow refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. The engine coughed twice and died outside Quilhollow, and Dario Holloway fixed it with a strip of whale-bone veneer and unrepeatable language. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Quilhollow, and Dario Holloway lost a wager involving 6 tins of glass floats. The toll bridge at Vorshore was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent.

Later, writing the log by lamplight, Dario Holloway admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. They were 84 miles from Vorshore and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Dario Holloway more than the weather did.

## Nightfall

The next entry in the log is in different handwriting. The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched.
