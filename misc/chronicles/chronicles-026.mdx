In Ilybury they found the warehouse of Dario Brandt locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1879 nailed to the door. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Ilybury refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Greta Brandt admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. They were 41 miles from Velmire and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## What the log records

They took on a passenger at Ilybury: Dario Brandt, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. ## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Ilybury customs house.
