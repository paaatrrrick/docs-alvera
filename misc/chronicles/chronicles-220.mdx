## On the water

At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Velton, and Joren Whitlock lost a wager involving 4 tins of almanac reprints. A letter was waiting at the Velton post house, addressed in handwriting that made Joren Whitlock go very quiet. In Velton they found the warehouse of Vesna Sorvane locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1874 nailed to the door. The toll bridge at Marmire was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. Halfway to Marmire, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. They were 78 miles from Marmire and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## What the log records

## Nightfall

Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of wax-sealed canvas, and none of them had put it there. The next entry in the log is in different handwriting.
