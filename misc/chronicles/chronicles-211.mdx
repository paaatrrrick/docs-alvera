## On the water

At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Fenmara, and Annik Kessler lost a wager involving 3 tins of loom parts. Annik Kessler bought a crate of tar barrels from a one-eyed trader in Fenmara, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. In Fenmara they found the warehouse of Maren Holloway locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1923 nailed to the door. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Fenmara refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. Halfway to Corford, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Annik Kessler admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of whale-bone veneer, and none of them had put it there.
