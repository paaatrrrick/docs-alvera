## What the log records

In Delton they found the warehouse of Dario Brandt locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1899 nailed to the door. A letter was waiting at the Delton post house, addressed in handwriting that made Sanna Sorvane go very quiet. an early frost caught them in the open stretch past Delton, and the cargo of wool bales shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Sanna Sorvane more than the weather did. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.

## Nightfall

It was only when they cast off that Dario Brandt noticed the second set of footprints on the cabin roof. The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched.
