## On the water

Halfway to Tammore, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. Vesna Oduya bought a crate of glass floats from a one-eyed trader in Wrenshore, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. They took on a passenger at Wrenshore: Sanna Greaves, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. The toll bridge at Tammore was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. the autumn king tides caught them in the open stretch past Wrenshore, and the cargo of almanac reprints shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Vesna Oduya more than the weather did.

## Nightfall

Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of pressed kelp paper, and none of them had put it there. On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Wrenshore customs house.
