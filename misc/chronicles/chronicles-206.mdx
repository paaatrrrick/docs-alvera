## On the water

Beatrix Drummond bought a crate of copper wire from a one-eyed trader in Selcrest, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Selcrest, and Beatrix Drummond lost a wager involving 5 tins of loom parts. Halfway to Quilbury, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. A letter was waiting at the Selcrest post house, addressed in handwriting that made Beatrix Drummond go very quiet. They took on a passenger at Selcrest: Joren Voss, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Beatrix Drummond more than the weather did.

## Nightfall
