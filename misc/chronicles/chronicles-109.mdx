## On the water

The toll bridge at Zelfell was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Morgate refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. Halfway to Zelfell, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. Dario Marrow bought a crate of preserved lemons from a one-eyed trader in Morgate, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. The engine coughed twice and died outside Morgate, and Dario Marrow fixed it with a strip of fired clay and unrepeatable language.

They were 73 miles from Zelfell and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.

A letter was waiting at the Morgate post house, addressed in handwriting that made Dario Marrow go very quiet.

The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Dario Marrow more than the weather did. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. The next entry in the log is in different handwriting.
