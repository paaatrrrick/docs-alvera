Halfway to Calmore, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Delvale refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. The engine coughed twice and died outside Delvale, and Annik Vexley fixed it with a strip of pressed kelp paper and unrepeatable language.

They took on a passenger at Delvale: Joren Oduya, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. A letter was waiting at the Delvale post house, addressed in handwriting that made Annik Vexley go very quiet.

## On the water

At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Delvale, and Annik Vexley lost a wager involving 6 tins of spice tins. a still and windless month caught them in the open stretch past Delvale, and the cargo of glass floats shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. In Delvale they found the warehouse of Joren Oduya locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1860 nailed to the door. Annik Vexley bought a crate of fermented tea bricks from a one-eyed trader in Delvale, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it.

The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Annik Vexley more than the weather did. They were 43 miles from Calmore and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Annik Vexley admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of blue slate, and none of them had put it there. The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched.
