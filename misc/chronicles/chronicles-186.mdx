## What the log records

In Ossshore they found the warehouse of Maren Oduya locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1922 nailed to the door. The toll bridge at Calvale was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. They took on a passenger at Ossshore: Maren Oduya, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Ossshore, and Edda Marrow lost a wager involving 9 tins of glass floats.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Edda Marrow admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Ossshore customs house.
