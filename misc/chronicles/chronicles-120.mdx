## On the water

Halfway to Torvik, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. In Fenvale they found the warehouse of Anselm Lindqvist locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1947 nailed to the door. A letter was waiting at the Fenvale post house, addressed in handwriting that made Emeric Brandt go very quiet. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Fenvale, and Emeric Brandt lost a wager involving 3 tins of wool bales. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Fenvale refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing.

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. They were 90 miles from Torvik and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.
