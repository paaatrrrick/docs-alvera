## On the water

They took on a passenger at Velstead: Liesl Brandt, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind. The engine coughed twice and died outside Velstead, and Edda Vexley fixed it with a strip of bog iron and unrepeatable language.

They were 12 miles from Morspire and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Edda Vexley admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of wax-sealed canvas, and none of them had put it there.
