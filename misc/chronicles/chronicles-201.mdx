## On the water

Halfway to Zelreach, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. Ivo Greaves bought a crate of glass floats from a one-eyed trader in Kelton, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. a still and windless month caught them in the open stretch past Kelton, and the cargo of loom parts shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

Later, writing the log by lamplight, Ivo Greaves admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record. Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Ivo Greaves more than the weather did.

## What the log records

A letter was waiting at the Kelton post house, addressed in handwriting that made Ivo Greaves go very quiet. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. They were 57 miles from Zelreach and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. It was only when they cast off that Imre Falkner noticed the second set of footprints on the cabin roof.
