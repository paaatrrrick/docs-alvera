The toll bridge at Galreach was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. an early frost caught them in the open stretch past Velmara, and the cargo of lamp oil shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. Annik Marchetti bought a crate of glass floats from a one-eyed trader in Velmara, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. They took on a passenger at Velmara: Tomas Oduya, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind.

Halfway to Galreach, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. A letter was waiting at the Velmara post house, addressed in handwriting that made Annik Marchetti go very quiet. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Velmara, and Annik Marchetti lost a wager involving 5 tins of loom parts. The engine coughed twice and died outside Velmara, and Annik Marchetti fixed it with a strip of woven reed and unrepeatable language.

## On the water

The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Velmara refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. Annik Marchetti bought a crate of preserved lemons from a one-eyed trader in Velmara, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. In Velmara they found the warehouse of Tomas Oduya locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1862 nailed to the door. Halfway to Galreach, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. a long fog season caught them in the open stretch past Velmara, and the cargo of spice tins shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. The old maps had been wrong before, but never this confidently, and that worried Annik Marchetti more than the weather did.

## What the log records

It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten. They were 27 miles from Galreach and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started.

## Nightfall

The last lamp on the quay went out as they passed it, all at once, as if pinched. On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Velmara customs house.
