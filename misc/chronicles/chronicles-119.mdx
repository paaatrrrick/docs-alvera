The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Zelmore refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. Halfway to Velspire, the barge took on water near the reed cut, and they spent the night beached on a mudbank counting stars. weeks of sideways rain caught them in the open stretch past Zelmore, and the cargo of copper wire shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours. They took on a passenger at Zelmore: Tomas Brandt, who claimed to be a surveyor and carried no instruments of any kind.

The engine coughed twice and died outside Zelmore, and Cosima Holloway fixed it with a strip of blue slate and unrepeatable language. The toll bridge at Velspire was out, so they detoured through the old channel, where the water smelled of iron and the birds went silent. In Zelmore they found the warehouse of Tomas Brandt locked, the windows papered over, and a notice from 1811 nailed to the door. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Zelmore, and Cosima Holloway lost a wager involving 3 tins of glass floats.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. They were 56 miles from Velspire and further than ever, by any measure that counted, from where they had started. Later, writing the log by lamplight, Cosima Holloway admitted the route was changing them in ways the ledger could not record.

## Nightfall

On the lock gate, in fresh chalk, someone had drawn the same spiral from the Zelmore customs house. Under the floor plank where the spare rope lived, there was now a small box of bog iron, and none of them had put it there.
