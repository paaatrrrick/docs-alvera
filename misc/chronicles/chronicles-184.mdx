## On the water

A letter was waiting at the Vorvik post house, addressed in handwriting that made Folke Mollering go very quiet. At dusk the crew traded stories with the bargefolk of Vorvik, and Folke Mollering lost a wager involving 5 tins of loom parts. The morning began badly: the lock keeper at Vorvik refused passage until the crew produced papers nobody remembered packing. Folke Mollering bought a crate of loom parts from a one-eyed trader in Vorvik, and by evening everyone aboard regretted it. a still and windless month caught them in the open stretch past Vorvik, and the cargo of glass floats shifted so badly the hull groaned for hours.

Nobody spoke of it the next day, which on this barge was how everyone agreed something had mattered. It was becoming clear the cargo mattered less than the route itself, and that someone wanted the route forgotten.
