## Diet

In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Halvale swear it can open a tin of clockwork gears. Analysis of castings collected near Quildane shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and weeks of sideways rain, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1832 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 33 breeding pairs. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Rurik Lindqvist. The wardens at Halvale maintain 5 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction.
