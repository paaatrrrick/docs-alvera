## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Wrenbury shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Brenhollow swear it can open a tin of lamp oil. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Rurik Brandt. The wardens at Brenhollow maintain 9 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and an early frost, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1930 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 27 breeding pairs.
