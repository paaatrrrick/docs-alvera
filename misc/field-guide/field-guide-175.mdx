## Behavior

It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of loom parts wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector. Field notes kept by Otto Holloway since 1819 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Jorford. Juveniles practice diving for weeks before their first real catch, surfacing each time with visible indignation. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet bog iron almost perfectly.

## Diet

In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Wrenvik swear it can open a tin of spice tins. Analysis of castings collected near Jorford shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up.

## Conservation

The wardens at Wrenvik maintain 9 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1815 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 25 breeding pairs.
