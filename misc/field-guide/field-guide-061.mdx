## At a glance

When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet whale-bone veneer almost perfectly. Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Ashgate and Ulmmara, especially after a hailstorm that stripped the orchards. ## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Ulmmara shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Ashgate swear it can open a tin of clockwork gears. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and the autumn king tides, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Lazlo Voss. Numbers fell sharply after the 1859 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 49 breeding pairs.
