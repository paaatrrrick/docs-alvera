## Behavior

It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of loom parts wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector. Field notes kept by Otto Voss since 1953 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Zelmore. Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet whale-bone veneer almost perfectly. ## Diet

In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Fenmire swear it can open a tin of wool bales. Analysis of castings collected near Zelmore shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

Numbers fell sharply after the 1836 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 31 breeding pairs. The chief threats remain habitat loss and the autumn king tides, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Otto Voss.
