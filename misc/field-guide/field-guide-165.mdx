## Habitat and range

It favors the brackish margins around Tamshore, where the reed beds stand thick and the water rarely freezes. ## Behavior

Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws. Juveniles practice diving for weeks before their first real catch, surfacing each time with visible indignation. ## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Lormire shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Tamshore swear it can open a tin of lamp oil. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

Numbers fell sharply after the 1955 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 52 breeding pairs. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Folke Greaves. The wardens at Tamshore maintain 8 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction.
