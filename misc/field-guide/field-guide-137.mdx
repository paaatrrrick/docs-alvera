## Habitat and range

During the cold months it withdraws to the cellars and culverts of Narcrest, emerging only on still nights. Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Narcrest and Yarvik, especially after a long fog season. It is most active at dawn, working the mudflats in loose groups of 4 to 57 individuals. ## Diet

In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Narcrest swear it can open a tin of tar barrels. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. Analysis of castings collected near Yarvik shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

The wardens at Narcrest maintain 3 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Petra Sorvane. The chief threats remain habitat loss and the dry easterlies, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1955 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 68 breeding pairs.
