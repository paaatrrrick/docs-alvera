## At a glance

Field notes kept by Joren Voss since 1816 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Zelhollow. Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Zelmore and Zelhollow, especially after a long fog season. It nests in cavities worn into fired clay pilings, lining them with shredded rope and feathers.

## Habitat and range

During the cold months it withdraws to the cellars and culverts of Zelmore, emerging only on still nights. Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Zelmore and Zelhollow, especially after spring floods. It is most active at dawn, working the mudflats in loose groups of 6 to 79 individuals. It favors the brackish margins around Zelmore, where the reed beds stand thick and the water rarely freezes.

## Behavior

Juveniles practice diving for weeks before their first real catch, surfacing each time with visible indignation. It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of dye cakes wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector. Pairs bond for several seasons and share incubation duties almost exactly equally, trading places at first light. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet blue slate almost perfectly. Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws.

## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. Analysis of castings collected near Zelhollow shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and spring floods, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. The wardens at Zelmore maintain 6 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. Numbers fell sharply after the 1853 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 87 breeding pairs. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Joren Voss.
