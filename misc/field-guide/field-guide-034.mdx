## Behavior

It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of glass floats wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector. Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet blue slate almost perfectly. Field notes kept by Tomas Vexley since 1810 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Erimire. Pairs bond for several seasons and share incubation duties almost exactly equally, trading places at first light.

## Diet

It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. Analysis of castings collected near Erimire shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

The wardens at Caldane maintain 7 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. Numbers fell sharply after the 1849 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 47 breeding pairs. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Tomas Vexley. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a long fog season, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night.
