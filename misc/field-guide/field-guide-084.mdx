## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Galcrest shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Selvik swear it can open a tin of almanac reprints. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and a long fog season, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. The wardens at Selvik maintain 8 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Folke Marrow. Numbers fell sharply after the 1928 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 23 breeding pairs.
