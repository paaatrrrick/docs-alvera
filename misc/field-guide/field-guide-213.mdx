## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Kelmire shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Vorhollow swear it can open a tin of tar barrels. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

Numbers fell sharply after the 1852 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 23 breeding pairs. The wardens at Vorhollow maintain 7 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and the dry easterlies, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night.
