## Behavior

Field notes kept by Halvard Sorvane since 1960 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Delvik. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet rope-fiber composite almost perfectly. Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws. It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of lamp oil wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector.

## Diet

It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Eristead swear it can open a tin of lamp oil. Analysis of castings collected near Delvik shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

The wardens at Eristead maintain 4 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Halvard Sorvane. The chief threats remain habitat loss and the dry easterlies, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night.
