## Diet

In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Calrun swear it can open a tin of copper wire. Analysis of castings collected near Rosrun shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and a long fog season, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1840 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 28 breeding pairs. The wardens at Calrun maintain 4 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Rurik Whitlock.
