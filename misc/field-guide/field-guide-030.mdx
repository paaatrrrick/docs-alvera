## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Galdane swear it can open a tin of fermented tea bricks. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. Analysis of castings collected near Narcrest shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Maren Brandt. The chief threats remain habitat loss and the autumn king tides, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1912 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 78 breeding pairs. The wardens at Galdane maintain 8 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction.
