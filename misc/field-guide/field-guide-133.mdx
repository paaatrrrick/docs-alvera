## Behavior

When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet whale-bone veneer almost perfectly. Field notes kept by Ivo Severin since 1849 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Perstead. It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of tar barrels wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector. ## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Calshore swear it can open a tin of wool bales. Analysis of castings collected near Perstead shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

The wardens at Calshore maintain 8 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a still and windless month, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Ivo Severin.
