## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Halvale swear it can open a tin of preserved lemons. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up.

## Conservation

Numbers fell sharply after the 1858 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 36 breeding pairs. The wardens at Halvale maintain 3 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night.
