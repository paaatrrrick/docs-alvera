## Diet

It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Mordane swear it can open a tin of copper wire. Analysis of castings collected near Ulmreach shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and a still and windless month, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1927 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 21 breeding pairs. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Bram Whitlock. The wardens at Mordane maintain 4 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction.
