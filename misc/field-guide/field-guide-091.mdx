## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Ilystead swear it can open a tin of almanac reprints.

## Conservation

Numbers fell sharply after the 1947 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 31 breeding pairs. The wardens at Ilystead maintain 9 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and the dry easterlies, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Mireille Vexley.
