## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Quilhollow shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

Numbers fell sharply after the 1928 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 87 breeding pairs. The wardens at Narshore maintain 8 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Mireille Marchetti. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a still and windless month, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night.
