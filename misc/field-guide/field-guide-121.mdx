## Diet

In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Velton swear it can open a tin of fermented tea bricks. Analysis of castings collected near Marvik shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and the autumn king tides, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. The wardens at Velton maintain 9 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. Numbers fell sharply after the 1903 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 75 breeding pairs.
