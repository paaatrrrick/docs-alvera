## At a glance

Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Zelmore and Yarreach, especially after spring floods. ## Behavior

Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet bog iron almost perfectly. Juveniles practice diving for weeks before their first real catch, surfacing each time with visible indignation. It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of preserved lemons wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector.

## Diet

In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Zelmore swear it can open a tin of spice tins. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. Analysis of castings collected near Yarreach shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Folke Holloway. Numbers fell sharply after the 1836 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 67 breeding pairs. The wardens at Zelmore maintain 3 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction.
