## Habitat and range

During the cold months it withdraws to the cellars and culverts of Ossreach, emerging only on still nights. Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Ossreach and Osswick, especially after an early frost. It nests in cavities worn into pressed kelp paper pilings, lining them with shredded rope and feathers.

## Diet

It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. Analysis of castings collected near Osswick shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Ossreach swear it can open a tin of preserved lemons.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Joren Oduya. The wardens at Ossreach maintain 4 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. Numbers fell sharply after the 1933 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 34 breeding pairs.
