## Behavior

Field notes kept by Emeric Lindqvist since 1870 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Yarvale. It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of clockwork gears wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector. Pairs bond for several seasons and share incubation duties almost exactly equally, trading places at first light. ## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Yarvale shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Galdane swear it can open a tin of spice tins.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and weeks of sideways rain, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1818 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 54 breeding pairs. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Emeric Lindqvist.
