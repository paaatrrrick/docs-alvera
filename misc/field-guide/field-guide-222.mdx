## Behavior

Field notes kept by Folke Greaves since 1794 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Quilton. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet bog iron almost perfectly. It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of fermented tea bricks wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector. Pairs bond for several seasons and share incubation duties almost exactly equally, trading places at first light. ## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Galford swear it can open a tin of copper wire. Analysis of castings collected near Quilton shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and the autumn king tides, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1897 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 80 breeding pairs.
