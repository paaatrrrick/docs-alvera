## Habitat and range

It favors the brackish margins around Fenmara, where the reed beds stand thick and the water rarely freezes. During the cold months it withdraws to the cellars and culverts of Fenmara, emerging only on still nights. Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Fenmara and Osswick, especially after the dry easterlies. It nests in cavities worn into woven reed pilings, lining them with shredded rope and feathers.

## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Osswick shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. The wardens at Fenmara maintain 5 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. Numbers fell sharply after the 1819 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 15 breeding pairs. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Greta Marchetti.
