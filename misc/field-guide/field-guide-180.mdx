## Habitat and range

It is most active at dawn, working the mudflats in loose groups of 9 to 54 individuals. It nests in cavities worn into woven reed pilings, lining them with shredded rope and feathers. It favors the brackish margins around Zelrun, where the reed beds stand thick and the water rarely freezes. ## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Zelrun swear it can open a tin of glass floats. Analysis of castings collected near Mordane shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and the dry easterlies, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1845 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 44 breeding pairs. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Sanna Brandt.
