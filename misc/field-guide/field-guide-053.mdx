## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Brenvale swear it can open a tin of tar barrels. Analysis of castings collected near Zelton shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

Numbers fell sharply after the 1875 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 83 breeding pairs. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Dario Vexley. The chief threats remain habitat loss and the autumn king tides, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. The wardens at Brenvale maintain 2 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction.
