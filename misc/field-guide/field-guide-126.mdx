## Behavior

When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet salt-cured oak almost perfectly. It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of fermented tea bricks wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector. Field notes kept by Dario Quist since 1795 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Quilbury. Juveniles practice diving for weeks before their first real catch, surfacing each time with visible indignation.

## Diet

It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. Analysis of castings collected near Quilbury shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Selwick swear it can open a tin of spice tins.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Dario Quist. The wardens at Selwick maintain 7 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night.
