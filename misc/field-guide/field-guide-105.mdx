## Behavior

Field notes kept by Tilda Ashdown since 1824 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Yarhollow. ## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Morton swear it can open a tin of wool bales. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. Analysis of castings collected near Yarhollow shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

Numbers fell sharply after the 1891 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 75 breeding pairs. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Tilda Ashdown. The chief threats remain habitat loss and spring floods, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. The wardens at Morton maintain 7 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction.
