## Behavior

## Diet

In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Zelmire swear it can open a tin of tar barrels. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Petra Quist. The chief threats remain habitat loss and an early frost, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1845 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 60 breeding pairs.
