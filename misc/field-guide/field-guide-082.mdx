## Behavior

Field notes kept by Casper Vexley since 1883 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Ashhollow. Pairs bond for several seasons and share incubation duties almost exactly equally, trading places at first light. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet hammered tin almost perfectly. Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws.

## Diet

In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Halgate swear it can open a tin of loom parts. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. Analysis of castings collected near Ashhollow shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

The wardens at Halgate maintain 8 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1823 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 89 breeding pairs.
