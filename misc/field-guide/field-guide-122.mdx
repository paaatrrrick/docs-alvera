## Habitat and range

During the cold months it withdraws to the cellars and culverts of Torvik, emerging only on still nights. It favors the brackish margins around Torvik, where the reed beds stand thick and the water rarely freezes. It nests in cavities worn into bog iron pilings, lining them with shredded rope and feathers. It is most active at dawn, working the mudflats in loose groups of 6 to 55 individuals.

## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Ilyshore shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Torvik swear it can open a tin of tar barrels.

## Conservation

The wardens at Torvik maintain 7 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Imre Drummond.
