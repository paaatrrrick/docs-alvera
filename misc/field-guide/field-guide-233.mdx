## Habitat and range

Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Marvik and Jormara, especially after weeks of sideways rain. It nests in cavities worn into rope-fiber composite pilings, lining them with shredded rope and feathers. During the cold months it withdraws to the cellars and culverts of Marvik, emerging only on still nights. It is most active at dawn, working the mudflats in loose groups of 4 to 58 individuals.

## Diet

In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Marvik swear it can open a tin of lamp oil. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

Numbers fell sharply after the 1963 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 33 breeding pairs. The wardens at Marvik maintain 2 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Stellan Whitlock.
