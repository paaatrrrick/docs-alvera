## Habitat and range

It favors the brackish margins around Rosmire, where the reed beds stand thick and the water rarely freezes. It is most active at dawn, working the mudflats in loose groups of 3 to 81 individuals. During the cold months it withdraws to the cellars and culverts of Rosmire, emerging only on still nights. Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Rosmire and Ilyreach, especially after weeks of sideways rain.

## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Ilyreach shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Rosmire swear it can open a tin of copper wire. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

- Pairs bond for several seasons and share incubation duties almost exactly equally, trading places at first light.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and spring floods, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1917 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 14 breeding pairs. The wardens at Rosmire maintain 2 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction.
