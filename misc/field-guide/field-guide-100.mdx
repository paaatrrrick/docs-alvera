## Behavior

When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet pressed kelp paper almost perfectly. Field notes kept by Halvard Petrov since 1947 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Selrun. Pairs bond for several seasons and share incubation duties almost exactly equally, trading places at first light. Juveniles practice diving for weeks before their first real catch, surfacing each time with visible indignation.

## Diet

It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Ashmore swear it can open a tin of fermented tea bricks. Analysis of castings collected near Selrun shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Halvard Petrov. The wardens at Ashmore maintain 3 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a still and windless month, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1891 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 26 breeding pairs.
