## At a glance

Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws. Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Vorcrest and Jorspire, especially after weeks of sideways rain. It nests in cavities worn into blue slate pilings, lining them with shredded rope and feathers. It is most active at dawn, working the mudflats in loose groups of 2 to 14 individuals.

## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Vorcrest swear it can open a tin of clockwork gears. Analysis of castings collected near Jorspire shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Lazlo Marchetti. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a long fog season, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1835 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 62 breeding pairs. The wardens at Vorcrest maintain 5 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction.
