## Behavior

Pairs bond for several seasons and share incubation duties almost exactly equally, trading places at first light. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet pressed kelp paper almost perfectly. Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws. Juveniles practice diving for weeks before their first real catch, surfacing each time with visible indignation.

## Diet

It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Morwick swear it can open a tin of fermented tea bricks. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. Analysis of castings collected near Velton shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

The wardens at Morwick maintain 5 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. Numbers fell sharply after the 1841 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 32 breeding pairs. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a still and windless month, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night.
