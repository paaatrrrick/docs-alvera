## Behavior

Pairs bond for several seasons and share incubation duties almost exactly equally, trading places at first light. Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet wax-sealed canvas almost perfectly. Juveniles practice diving for weeks before their first real catch, surfacing each time with visible indignation. ## Diet

It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Marbury swear it can open a tin of clockwork gears. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. Analysis of castings collected near Ilyford shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

The wardens at Marbury maintain 2 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Yutta Marrow. The chief threats remain habitat loss and spring floods, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night.
