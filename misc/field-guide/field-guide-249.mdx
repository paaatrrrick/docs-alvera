## Behavior

Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws. Pairs bond for several seasons and share incubation duties almost exactly equally, trading places at first light. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet pressed kelp paper almost perfectly. ## Diet

It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. Analysis of castings collected near Eriton shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Vorvik swear it can open a tin of copper wire. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

The wardens at Vorvik maintain 3 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Dario Quist. Numbers fell sharply after the 1839 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 67 breeding pairs.
