## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Wrengate swear it can open a tin of almanac reprints. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Mireille Halloran. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a still and windless month, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1918 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 87 breeding pairs.
