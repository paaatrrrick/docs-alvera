## Habitat and range

Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Tormire and Zelstead, especially after an early frost. It is most active at dawn, working the mudflats in loose groups of 8 to 24 individuals. It nests in cavities worn into rope-fiber composite pilings, lining them with shredded rope and feathers. It favors the brackish margins around Tormire, where the reed beds stand thick and the water rarely freezes.

## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. Analysis of castings collected near Zelstead shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up.

## Conservation

The wardens at Tormire maintain 6 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a still and windless month, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1803 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 32 breeding pairs.
