## Habitat and range

Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Perfell and Morspire, especially after spring floods. During the cold months it withdraws to the cellars and culverts of Perfell, emerging only on still nights. It nests in cavities worn into hammered tin pilings, lining them with shredded rope and feathers. It is most active at dawn, working the mudflats in loose groups of 4 to 63 individuals.

## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Morspire shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Perfell swear it can open a tin of clockwork gears.

## Conservation

The wardens at Perfell maintain 7 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and the autumn king tides, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1920 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 55 breeding pairs. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Greta Holloway.
