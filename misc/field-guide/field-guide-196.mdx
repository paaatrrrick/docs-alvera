## Habitat and range

It favors the brackish margins around Selrun, where the reed beds stand thick and the water rarely freezes. ## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. Analysis of castings collected near Kelreach shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Selrun swear it can open a tin of wool bales.

## Conservation

The wardens at Selrun maintain 8 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a hailstorm that stripped the orchards, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1905 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 70 breeding pairs. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Sanna Marchetti.
