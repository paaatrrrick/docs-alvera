## Habitat and range

It favors the brackish margins around Zelbury, where the reed beds stand thick and the water rarely freezes. It nests in cavities worn into bog iron pilings, lining them with shredded rope and feathers. ## Diet

It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Zelbury swear it can open a tin of glass floats. Analysis of castings collected near Dordane shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and spring floods, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. The wardens at Zelbury maintain 6 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Bram Petrov. Numbers fell sharply after the 1941 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 50 breeding pairs.
