## Habitat and range

During the cold months it withdraws to the cellars and culverts of Morcrest, emerging only on still nights. It is most active at dawn, working the mudflats in loose groups of 9 to 89 individuals. Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Morcrest and Vorford, especially after spring floods. ## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. Analysis of castings collected near Vorford shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and a still and windless month, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. The wardens at Morcrest maintain 6 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. Numbers fell sharply after the 1789 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 54 breeding pairs.
