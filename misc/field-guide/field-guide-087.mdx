## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Lorreach swear it can open a tin of preserved lemons. Analysis of castings collected near Halgate shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Liesl Holloway. The wardens at Lorreach maintain 4 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. Numbers fell sharply after the 1824 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 20 breeding pairs. The chief threats remain habitat loss and weeks of sideways rain, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night.
