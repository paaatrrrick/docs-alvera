## Behavior

## Diet

In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Ulmgate swear it can open a tin of lamp oil. Analysis of castings collected near Marstead shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

Numbers fell sharply after the 1889 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 90 breeding pairs. The wardens at Ulmgate maintain 3 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and the dry easterlies, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night.
