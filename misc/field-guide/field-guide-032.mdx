## At a glance

Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Vorholm and Narshore, especially after the autumn king tides. Juveniles practice diving for weeks before their first real catch, surfacing each time with visible indignation. ## Habitat and range

## Behavior

It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of almanac reprints wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector. Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws. Pairs bond for several seasons and share incubation duties almost exactly equally, trading places at first light. ## Diet

In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Vorholm swear it can open a tin of glass floats. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. Analysis of castings collected near Narshore shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up.

## Conservation

Numbers fell sharply after the 1919 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 82 breeding pairs. The wardens at Vorholm maintain 2 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Yutta Sorvane. The chief threats remain habitat loss and an early frost, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night.
