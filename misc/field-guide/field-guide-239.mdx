When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet bog iron almost perfectly. It favors the brackish margins around Halhollow, where the reed beds stand thick and the water rarely freezes. Sightings cluster along the old towpaths between Halhollow and Ulmvale, especially after a hailstorm that stripped the orchards.

## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Halhollow swear it can open a tin of tar barrels. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. Analysis of castings collected near Ulmvale shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

Numbers fell sharply after the 1818 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 60 breeding pairs. The wardens at Halhollow maintain 9 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and an early frost, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night.
