## Behavior

Field notes kept by Tomas Lindqvist since 1867 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Osscrest. Juveniles practice diving for weeks before their first real catch, surfacing each time with visible indignation. It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of tar barrels wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector. ## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Osscrest shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Ulmmore swear it can open a tin of dye cakes. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up.

## Conservation

The wardens at Ulmmore maintain 2 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Tomas Lindqvist. Numbers fell sharply after the 1794 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 76 breeding pairs. The chief threats remain habitat loss and the autumn king tides, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night.
