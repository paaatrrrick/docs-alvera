## Behavior

Juveniles practice diving for weeks before their first real catch, surfacing each time with visible indignation. Pairs bond for several seasons and share incubation duties almost exactly equally, trading places at first light. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet wax-sealed canvas almost perfectly. It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of tar barrels wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector.

## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Delholm shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Jorholm swear it can open a tin of dye cakes.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Oona Greaves. The wardens at Jorholm maintain 6 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a long fog season, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night.
