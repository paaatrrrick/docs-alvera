## Habitat and range

During the cold months it withdraws to the cellars and culverts of Torspire, emerging only on still nights. It nests in cavities worn into river-glass pilings, lining them with shredded rope and feathers. It is most active at dawn, working the mudflats in loose groups of 4 to 58 individuals. ## Diet

It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. Analysis of castings collected near Brenmara shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

The wardens at Torspire maintain 9 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. Numbers fell sharply after the 1836 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 35 breeding pairs. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Petra Falkner.
