## Behavior

It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of almanac reprints wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector. Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet blue slate almost perfectly. Field notes kept by Beatrix Voss since 1851 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Mardane.

## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Mardane shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Tamfell swear it can open a tin of copper wire.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and spring floods, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. The wardens at Tamfell maintain 3 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Beatrix Voss. Numbers fell sharply after the 1885 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 22 breeding pairs.
