## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. Analysis of castings collected near Ilyvik shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Selspire swear it can open a tin of glass floats. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up.

## Conservation

Numbers fell sharply after the 1881 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 13 breeding pairs. The chief threats remain habitat loss and an early frost, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. The wardens at Selspire maintain 5 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction.
