## Diet

In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Velholm swear it can open a tin of glass floats. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. Analysis of castings collected near Morfell shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up.

## Conservation

The chief threats remain habitat loss and an early frost, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. The wardens at Velholm maintain 4 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. Numbers fell sharply after the 1822 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 72 breeding pairs.
