## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Perton shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Sanna Falkner. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a still and windless month, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1842 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 89 breeding pairs.
