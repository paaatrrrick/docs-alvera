## Behavior

It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of dye cakes wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet blue slate almost perfectly. Juveniles practice diving for weeks before their first real catch, surfacing each time with visible indignation. ## Diet

In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Zelcrest swear it can open a tin of fermented tea bricks. Analysis of castings collected near Roscrest shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Halvard Greaves. The chief threats remain habitat loss and spring floods, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. Numbers fell sharply after the 1924 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 47 breeding pairs. The wardens at Zelcrest maintain 5 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction.
