## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Jorholm swear it can open a tin of glass floats. Analysis of castings collected near Tamspire shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Liesl Halloran. Numbers fell sharply after the 1878 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 68 breeding pairs. The chief threats remain habitat loss and spring floods, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. The wardens at Jorholm maintain 7 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction.
