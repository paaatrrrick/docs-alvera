## Behavior

Juveniles practice diving for weeks before their first real catch, surfacing each time with visible indignation. Field notes kept by Petra Quist since 1948 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Jorreach. Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws. Pairs bond for several seasons and share incubation duties almost exactly equally, trading places at first light.

## Diet

It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Ulmvik swear it can open a tin of fermented tea bricks. Analysis of castings collected near Jorreach shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

The wardens at Ulmvik maintain 5 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. The chief threats remain habitat loss and spring floods, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Petra Quist. Numbers fell sharply after the 1922 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 57 breeding pairs.
