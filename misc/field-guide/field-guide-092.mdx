## Behavior

Observers describe a distinctive clicking call, two short and one long, repeated until an intruder withdraws. Field notes kept by Stellan Quist since 1796 record a slow shift in its range northward toward Fengate. When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet lacquered birch almost perfectly. ## Diet

The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Delfell swear it can open a tin of tar barrels. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. Analysis of castings collected near Fengate shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails.

## Conservation

It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Stellan Quist. Numbers fell sharply after the 1960 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 25 breeding pairs. The chief threats remain habitat loss and a long fog season, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. The wardens at Delfell maintain 7 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction.
