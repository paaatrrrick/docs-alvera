## Behavior

When threatened it freezes rather than flees, relying on plumage that matches wet whale-bone veneer almost perfectly. It caches food obsessively, and abandoned caches of loom parts wrappings have confused more than one customs inspector. ## Diet

Analysis of castings collected near Selford shows a surprising proportion of freshwater snails. The bulk of its diet is small crustaceans, supplemented by seeds stripped from the canal-side grasses. It has learned to follow the barges, picking off whatever the bilge pumps stir up. In lean winters it will eat almost anything, and dockworkers in Tordane swear it can open a tin of fermented tea bricks.

## Conservation

Numbers fell sharply after the 1833 dredging works but have recovered to perhaps 45 breeding pairs. The chief threats remain habitat loss and the dry easterlies, which can destroy a full year of nests in a night. The wardens at Tordane maintain 7 artificial nesting walls, all of them occupied within a season of construction. It is protected by local ordinance, a law passed largely through the stubborn campaigning of Mireille Voss.
