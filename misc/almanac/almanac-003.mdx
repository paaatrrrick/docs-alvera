## The custom

On the first thaw morning, households in Dormore hang a strip of pressed kelp paper from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. Brides and grooms in Dormore cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1919. ## Origins

Records kept by Ivo Ashdown suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. ## How it is observed today

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Dormore shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge.

Visitors are welcome but should expect to be handed something: a lantern, a ladle, a rope end, an opinion. The dates shift with the tides; the only reliable authority is the chalkboard outside the harbormaster office in Dormore. Dress for spring floods, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so.
