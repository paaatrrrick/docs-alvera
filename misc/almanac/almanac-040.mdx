## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1957, when spring floods stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Records kept by Rurik Severin suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Cosima Voss, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. ## How it is observed today
