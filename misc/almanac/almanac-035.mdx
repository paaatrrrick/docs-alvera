## The custom

Brides and grooms in Erigate cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1907. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Erigate shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven.

## Origins

Neighboring Halmire maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Dario Sorvane, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar.
