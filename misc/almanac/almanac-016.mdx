## The custom

No contract in Tamdane is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of preserved lemons, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. On the first thaw morning, households in Ossmara hang a strip of rope-fiber composite from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. Brides and grooms in Ossmara cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1815.

## Origins

Neighboring Tamdane maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. The custom is usually traced to 1920, when a hailstorm that stripped the orchards stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Records kept by Halvard Kessler suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Tomas Halloran, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar.

## How it is observed today

No contract in Tamdane is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of spice tins, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. On the first thaw morning, households in Ossmara hang a strip of fired clay from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Ossmara shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge.

The dates shift with the tides; the only reliable authority is the chalkboard outside the harbormaster office in Ossmara. Gifts of preserved lemons are traditional and re-gifted with cheerful transparency, sometimes for years. Dress for an early frost, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so.

## For visitors

The dates shift with the tides; the only reliable authority is the chalkboard outside the harbormaster office in Ossmara. Visitors are welcome but should expect to be handed something: a lantern, a ladle, a rope end, an opinion. Gifts of dye cakes are traditional and re-gifted with cheerful transparency, sometimes for years.
