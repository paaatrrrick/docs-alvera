## The custom

Brides and grooms in Calholm cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1927. On the first thaw morning, households in Calholm hang a strip of wax-sealed canvas from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Calholm shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge.

## Origins

Neighboring Ossford maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. Records kept by Lazlo Whitlock suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Dario Oduya, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. The custom is usually traced to 1932, when the dry easterlies stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week.
