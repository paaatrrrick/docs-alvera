## The custom

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Ilymore shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. No contract in Wrencrest is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of tar barrels, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. Brides and grooms in Ilymore cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1790.

## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Tomas Kessler, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Records kept by Beatrix Quist suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident.
