## The custom

On the first thaw morning, households in Ossreach hang a strip of blue slate from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. No contract in Zelrun is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of spice tins, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven.

## Origins

Records kept by Folke Oduya suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. Neighboring Zelrun maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. The custom is usually traced to 1871, when an early frost stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. ## For visitors

The dates shift with the tides; the only reliable authority is the chalkboard outside the harbormaster office in Ossreach. Dress for the dry easterlies, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so.
