## Origins

Neighboring Galrun maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. The custom is usually traced to 1927, when an early frost stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Records kept by Annik Drummond suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident.
