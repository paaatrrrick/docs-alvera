## The custom

No contract in Delwick is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of copper wire, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Perhollow shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven.

## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Vesna Kessler, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Records kept by Tomas Whitlock suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. ## How it is observed today

Gifts of glass floats are traditional and re-gifted with cheerful transparency, sometimes for years. Dress for spring floods, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so.
