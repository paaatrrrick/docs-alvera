## The custom

No contract in Halgate is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of wool bales, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Roscrest shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven.

## Origins

Records kept by Oona Whitlock suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. The custom is usually traced to 1964, when a long fog season stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Tomas Holloway, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. ## How it is observed today

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. On the first thaw morning, households in Roscrest hang a strip of salt-cured oak from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. Brides and grooms in Roscrest cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1915.

Gifts of clockwork gears are traditional and re-gifted with cheerful transparency, sometimes for years. Dress for the autumn king tides, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so.
