## The custom

On the first thaw morning, households in Ilyspire hang a strip of whale-bone veneer from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. Brides and grooms in Ilyspire cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1846. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Ilyspire shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge.
