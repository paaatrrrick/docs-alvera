## The custom

On the first thaw morning, households in Calvik hang a strip of lacquered birch from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. No contract in Selvale is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of wool bales, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for.

## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1916, when weeks of sideways rain stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week.
