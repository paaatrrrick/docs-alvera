## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Margate shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the first thaw morning, households in Margate hang a strip of wax-sealed canvas from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. No contract in Mardane is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of dye cakes, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. ## Origins

Records kept by Ivo Marrow suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Dario Kessler, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. The custom is usually traced to 1845, when an early frost stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week.
