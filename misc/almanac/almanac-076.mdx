## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Yarvik shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. On the first thaw morning, households in Yarvik hang a strip of salt-cured oak from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper.

## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Oona Lindqvist, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Records kept by Sigrid Brandt suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. Neighboring Narvik maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity.
