## The custom

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Seldane shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. Brides and grooms in Seldane cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1789.

## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1916, when an early frost stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Records kept by Mireille Lindqvist suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. Neighboring Lorrun maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity.
