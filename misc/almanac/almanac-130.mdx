## The custom

Brides and grooms in Velvale cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1824. On the first thaw morning, households in Velvale hang a strip of wax-sealed canvas from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Velvale shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge.

## Origins

Records kept by Tilda Oduya suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. Neighboring Calvale maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Tomas Whitlock, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. The custom is usually traced to 1838, when a long fog season stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week.
