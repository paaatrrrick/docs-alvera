## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Osscrest shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. ## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Rurik Vexley, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. The custom is usually traced to 1812, when the autumn king tides stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Neighboring Torton maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity.
