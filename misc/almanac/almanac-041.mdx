## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Corfell shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. ## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Casper Brandt, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. The custom is usually traced to 1892, when spring floods stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week.
