## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1915, when the autumn king tides stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Neighboring Deldane maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Mireille Mollering, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar.
