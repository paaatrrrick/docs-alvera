## The custom

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Jorrun shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the first thaw morning, households in Jorrun hang a strip of woven reed from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper.

## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1957, when a still and windless month stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Neighboring Calvale maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. Records kept by Sigrid Sorvane suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Stellan Falkner, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar.

## How it is observed today

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Jorrun shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. On the first thaw morning, households in Jorrun hang a strip of salt-cured oak from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. Brides and grooms in Jorrun cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1954.

Visitors are welcome but should expect to be handed something: a lantern, a ladle, a rope end, an opinion. Dress for an early frost, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so.

## For visitors

Gifts of almanac reprints are traditional and re-gifted with cheerful transparency, sometimes for years. Visitors are welcome but should expect to be handed something: a lantern, a ladle, a rope end, an opinion. Dress for the autumn king tides, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so. The dates shift with the tides; the only reliable authority is the chalkboard outside the harbormaster office in Jorrun.
