## Origins

Neighboring Osscrest maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. Records kept by Tilda Voss suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. The custom is usually traced to 1916, when the dry easterlies stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week.
