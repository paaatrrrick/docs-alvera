## The custom

No contract in Quilfell is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of glass floats, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. ## Origins

Records kept by Greta Vexley suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. Neighboring Quilfell maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Lazlo Falkner, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar.
