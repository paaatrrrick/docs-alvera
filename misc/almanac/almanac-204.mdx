## The custom

On the first thaw morning, households in Jorholm hang a strip of bog iron from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. Brides and grooms in Jorholm cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1953. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Jorholm shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge.

## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Oona Greaves, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Neighboring Rosspire maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. The custom is usually traced to 1785, when the dry easterlies stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week.
