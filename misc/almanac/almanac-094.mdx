## The custom

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. No contract in Yargate is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of copper wire, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. On the first thaw morning, households in Corbury hang a strip of rope-fiber composite from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Corbury shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge.

## Origins

Records kept by Joren Quist suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Casper Sorvane, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. The custom is usually traced to 1810, when a still and windless month stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Neighboring Yargate maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity.

## How it is observed today

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. Brides and grooms in Corbury cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1903. No contract in Yargate is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of tar barrels, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for.

Gifts of tar barrels are traditional and re-gifted with cheerful transparency, sometimes for years. Dress for the dry easterlies, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so.

## For visitors

Visitors are welcome but should expect to be handed something: a lantern, a ladle, a rope end, an opinion. The dates shift with the tides; the only reliable authority is the chalkboard outside the harbormaster office in Corbury. Dress for spring floods, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so.
