## The custom

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. On the first thaw morning, households in Marvik hang a strip of salt-cured oak from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Marvik shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. Brides and grooms in Marvik cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1928.

Gifts of almanac reprints are traditional and re-gifted with cheerful transparency, sometimes for years. Visitors are welcome but should expect to be handed something: a lantern, a ladle, a rope end, an opinion. The dates shift with the tides; the only reliable authority is the chalkboard outside the harbormaster office in Marvik.
