## The custom

No contract in Yarford is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of glass floats, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Ulmreach shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. On the first thaw morning, households in Ulmreach hang a strip of salt-cured oak from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper.

## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1885, when weeks of sideways rain stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Records kept by Sigrid Petrov suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Maren Marchetti, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Neighboring Yarford maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity.

## How it is observed today

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Ulmreach shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the first thaw morning, households in Ulmreach hang a strip of fired clay from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper.

Dress for weeks of sideways rain, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so. The dates shift with the tides; the only reliable authority is the chalkboard outside the harbormaster office in Ulmreach.

## For visitors

Gifts of lamp oil are traditional and re-gifted with cheerful transparency, sometimes for years. Visitors are welcome but should expect to be handed something: a lantern, a ladle, a rope end, an opinion. Dress for spring floods, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so.
