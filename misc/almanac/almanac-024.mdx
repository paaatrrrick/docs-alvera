## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Zelwick shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. Brides and grooms in Zelwick cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1938.

## Origins

Records kept by Folke Marrow suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. The custom is usually traced to 1817, when a long fog season stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Neighboring Morreach maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity.
