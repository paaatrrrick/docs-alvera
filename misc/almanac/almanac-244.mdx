## The custom

No contract in Quilstead is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of wool bales, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. On the first thaw morning, households in Erifell hang a strip of whale-bone veneer from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. Brides and grooms in Erifell cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1853.

## Origins

Neighboring Quilstead maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. The custom is usually traced to 1911, when a long fog season stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week.
