## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Keldane shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the first thaw morning, households in Keldane hang a strip of whale-bone veneer from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. Brides and grooms in Keldane cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1916.

## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1914, when weeks of sideways rain stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Beatrix Falkner, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Neighboring Brenfell maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. Records kept by Ivo Lindqvist suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident.
