## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Wrenstead shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. ## Origins

Records kept by Ivo Holloway suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident.
