## The custom

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. Brides and grooms in Halspire cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1809. No contract in Corwick is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of clockwork gears, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. On the first thaw morning, households in Halspire hang a strip of hammered tin from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper.

## Origins
