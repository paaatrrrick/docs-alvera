## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1900, when the autumn king tides stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Neighboring Velfell maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. Records kept by Mireille Greaves suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident.
