## The custom

On the first thaw morning, households in Rosstead hang a strip of hammered tin from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Rosstead shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. ## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1873, when a still and windless month stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Records kept by Stellan Lindqvist suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident.
