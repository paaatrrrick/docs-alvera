## The custom

No contract in Jorrun is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of lamp oil, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. Brides and grooms in Delcrest cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1857. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Delcrest shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge.

## Origins

Records kept by Ivo Sorvane suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Annik Lindqvist, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Neighboring Jorrun maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity.
