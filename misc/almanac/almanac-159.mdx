## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Halshore shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. Brides and grooms in Halshore cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1869. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven.

## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1917, when a long fog season stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Yutta Lindqvist, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar.
