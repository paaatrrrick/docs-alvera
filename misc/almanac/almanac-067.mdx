## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Rosgate shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the first thaw morning, households in Rosgate hang a strip of rope-fiber composite from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven.

## Origins

Records kept by Edda Drummond suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. The custom is usually traced to 1962, when a hailstorm that stripped the orchards stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Sigrid Falkner, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar.
