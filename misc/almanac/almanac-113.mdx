## The custom

Brides and grooms in Roshollow cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1804. ## Origins

Records kept by Petra Falkner suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Bram Drummond, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar.
