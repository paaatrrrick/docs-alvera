## The custom

Brides and grooms in Ilyrun cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1961. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. On the first thaw morning, households in Ilyrun hang a strip of river-glass from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. ## Origins

Records kept by Stellan Voss suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. The custom is usually traced to 1925, when weeks of sideways rain stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Neighboring Tamvale maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Emeric Falkner, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar.
