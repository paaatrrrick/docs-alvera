## Origins

Records kept by Dario Kessler suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Bram Oduya, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. The custom is usually traced to 1956, when a hailstorm that stripped the orchards stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Gifts of clockwork gears are traditional and re-gifted with cheerful transparency, sometimes for years. Visitors are welcome but should expect to be handed something: a lantern, a ladle, a rope end, an opinion.
