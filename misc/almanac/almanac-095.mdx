## The custom

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. Brides and grooms in Calmire cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1812. On the first thaw morning, households in Calmire hang a strip of bog iron from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper.

## Origins

Neighboring Osswick maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. Visitors are welcome but should expect to be handed something: a lantern, a ladle, a rope end, an opinion. Gifts of preserved lemons are traditional and re-gifted with cheerful transparency, sometimes for years.
