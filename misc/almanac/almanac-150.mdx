## The custom

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. On the first thaw morning, households in Yarcrest hang a strip of lacquered birch from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Yarcrest shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. Brides and grooms in Yarcrest cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1785.

Gifts of lamp oil are traditional and re-gifted with cheerful transparency, sometimes for years. Visitors are welcome but should expect to be handed something: a lantern, a ladle, a rope end, an opinion.
