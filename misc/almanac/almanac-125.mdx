## The custom

Brides and grooms in Dorreach cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1792. On the first thaw morning, households in Dorreach hang a strip of lacquered birch from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Dorreach shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge.

## Origins

Neighboring Ulmmire maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity.
