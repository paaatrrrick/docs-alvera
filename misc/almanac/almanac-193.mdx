## The custom

On the first thaw morning, households in Jormore hang a strip of blue slate from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Jormore shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge.

## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Yutta Marrow, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Neighboring Mardane maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. The custom is usually traced to 1950, when a still and windless month stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week.
