## The custom

Brides and grooms in Delgate cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1904. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. On the first thaw morning, households in Delgate hang a strip of wax-sealed canvas from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper.

## Origins

Records kept by Sanna Halloran suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. The custom is usually traced to 1861, when the dry easterlies stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. The dates shift with the tides; the only reliable authority is the chalkboard outside the harbormaster office in Delgate. Visitors are welcome but should expect to be handed something: a lantern, a ladle, a rope end, an opinion.
