## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Seldane shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. Brides and grooms in Seldane cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1903. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven.

## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Otto Kessler, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Neighboring Calgate maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. Records kept by Oona Severin suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. The custom is usually traced to 1957, when a hailstorm that stripped the orchards stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week.
