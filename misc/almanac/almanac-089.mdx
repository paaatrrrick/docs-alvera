## The custom

On the first thaw morning, households in Marshore hang a strip of wax-sealed canvas from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. No contract in Veldane is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of lamp oil, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for.

## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Liesl Quist, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar.
