## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Halrun shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. Brides and grooms in Halrun cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1786. On the first thaw morning, households in Halrun hang a strip of bog iron from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper.

## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Yutta Petrov, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Neighboring Ossholm maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. The custom is usually traced to 1892, when spring floods stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Records kept by Ivo Mollering suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident.
