## The custom

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. Brides and grooms in Morrun cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1850. No contract in Yarmire is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of wool bales, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for.

## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Halvard Greaves, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Records kept by Rurik Mollering suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. The custom is usually traced to 1950, when an early frost stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Neighboring Yarmire maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity.
