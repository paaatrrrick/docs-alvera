## The custom

No contract in Ossspire is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of fermented tea bricks, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. Brides and grooms in Caldane cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1833. On the first thaw morning, households in Caldane hang a strip of bog iron from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Caldane shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge.

## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Anselm Vexley, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Records kept by Sigrid Whitlock suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. Neighboring Ossspire maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. The custom is usually traced to 1830, when the dry easterlies stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week.

## How it is observed today

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Caldane shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. No contract in Ossspire is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of spice tins, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. On the first thaw morning, households in Caldane hang a strip of wax-sealed canvas from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven.

Gifts of lamp oil are traditional and re-gifted with cheerful transparency, sometimes for years. Dress for a long fog season, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so.

## For visitors

Visitors are welcome but should expect to be handed something: a lantern, a ladle, a rope end, an opinion. Dress for the autumn king tides, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so. The dates shift with the tides; the only reliable authority is the chalkboard outside the harbormaster office in Caldane.
