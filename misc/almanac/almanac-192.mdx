## The custom

On the first thaw morning, households in Torshore hang a strip of hammered tin from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven.
