## The custom

Brides and grooms in Quilcrest cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1909. ## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1872, when a long fog season stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Neighboring Brenstead maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. Records kept by Halvard Greaves suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Liesl Kessler, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar.
