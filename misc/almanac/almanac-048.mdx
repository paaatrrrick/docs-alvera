## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Tomas Ashdown, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. The custom is usually traced to 1883, when the autumn king tides stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Records kept by Greta Drummond suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. Neighboring Jorgate maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity.
