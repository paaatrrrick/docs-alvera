## The custom

On the first thaw morning, households in Ilybury hang a strip of river-glass from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Ilybury shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. No contract in Corstead is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of copper wire, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for.
