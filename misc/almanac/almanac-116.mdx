## The custom

Brides and grooms in Tamcrest cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1956. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Tamcrest shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge.

## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1857, when the dry easterlies stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Petra Ashdown, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Records kept by Dario Voss suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. Neighboring Yarstead maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity.
