## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Ulmstead shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. On the first thaw morning, households in Ulmstead hang a strip of bog iron from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper.
