## The custom

On the first thaw morning, households in Quilbury hang a strip of wax-sealed canvas from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Quilbury shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge.

## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1860, when an early frost stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Records kept by Lazlo Marchetti suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. Neighboring Calford maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Rurik Halloran, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar.
