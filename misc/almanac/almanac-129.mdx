## The custom

Brides and grooms in Ashwick cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1812. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Ashwick shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the first thaw morning, households in Ashwick hang a strip of pressed kelp paper from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven.
