## The custom

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Ulmspire shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. Brides and grooms in Ulmspire cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1823.

## Origins

Neighboring Vormire maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Cosima Ashdown, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Records kept by Oona Voss suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. The custom is usually traced to 1805, when weeks of sideways rain stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week.
