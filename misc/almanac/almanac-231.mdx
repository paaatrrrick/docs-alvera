## The custom

No contract in Ulmreach is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of loom parts, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. Brides and grooms in Galholm cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1864. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Galholm shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the first thaw morning, households in Galholm hang a strip of fired clay from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper.

## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Petra Brandt, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Neighboring Ulmreach maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. The custom is usually traced to 1879, when the autumn king tides stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Records kept by Tomas Petrov suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident.

## How it is observed today

No contract in Ulmreach is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of spice tins, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. On the first thaw morning, households in Galholm hang a strip of lacquered birch from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Galholm shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven.

Dress for the autumn king tides, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so. Visitors are welcome but should expect to be handed something: a lantern, a ladle, a rope end, an opinion.

## For visitors

Dress for weeks of sideways rain, as the ceremony proceeds regardless and takes mild pride in doing so. Gifts of loom parts are traditional and re-gifted with cheerful transparency, sometimes for years. The dates shift with the tides; the only reliable authority is the chalkboard outside the harbormaster office in Galholm.
