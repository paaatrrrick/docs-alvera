## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Jormara shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the first thaw morning, households in Jormara hang a strip of whale-bone veneer from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven.
