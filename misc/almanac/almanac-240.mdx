## The custom

On the first thaw morning, households in Ilyfell hang a strip of river-glass from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. No contract in Ossstead is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of fermented tea bricks, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for.

## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Rurik Lindqvist, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar.
