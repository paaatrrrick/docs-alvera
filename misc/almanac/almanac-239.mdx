## The custom

On the first thaw morning, households in Delfell hang a strip of lacquered birch from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. Brides and grooms in Delfell cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1796. ## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1910, when an early frost stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Neighboring Galwick maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. Records kept by Vesna Halloran suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident.
