## The custom

No contract in Corholm is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of preserved lemons, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Yarhollow shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge.

## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1782, when the dry easterlies stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Records kept by Rurik Mollering suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident.
