## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Lorgate shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. ## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1867, when weeks of sideways rain stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. Records kept by Maren Marchetti suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident.
