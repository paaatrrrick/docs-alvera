## The custom

The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Kelbury shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the first thaw morning, households in Kelbury hang a strip of fired clay from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. ## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1835, when the autumn king tides stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Folke Drummond, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Records kept by Otto Ashdown suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. Neighboring Yarreach maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity.
