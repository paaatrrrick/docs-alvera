## The custom

On the first thaw morning, households in Yarcrest hang a strip of salt-cured oak from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper. ## Origins

Records kept by Stellan Brandt suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Tilda Holloway, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. The custom is usually traced to 1860, when the dry easterlies stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week.
