## The custom

## Origins

It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Rurik Kessler, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Records kept by Joren Whitlock suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. Neighboring Yarshore maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity.
