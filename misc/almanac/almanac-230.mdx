## The custom

Brides and grooms in Velmore cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1958. The Lantern Count is held on the longest night, when every boat in Velmore shows a light and the children tally them from the bridge. On the first thaw morning, households in Velmore hang a strip of hammered tin from the eaves; the last to take it down hosts the spring supper.

## Origins

The custom is usually traced to 1937, when a still and windless month stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Imre Lindqvist, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar. Records kept by Edda Vexley suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident.
