## The custom

On the feast of the lock keepers, the gates stand open for one symbolic hour, and tradition holds that any debt forgiven then stays forgiven. No contract in Ulmmire is considered binding until both parties have shared a tin of tar barrels, a custom lawyers have learned to budget time for. Brides and grooms in Dorgate cross the toll bridge without paying, once, a privilege recorded solemnly in the ledger since 1823.

## Origins

Records kept by Sigrid Ashdown suggest the practice began as a tax dodge and grew respectable by accident. Neighboring Ulmmire maintains a rival version, observed two days earlier, and partisans of each regard the other with theatrical pity. The custom is usually traced to 1818, when a hailstorm that stripped the orchards stranded the whole fleet and the town fed everyone for a week. It nearly lapsed during the embargo years and was revived by Beatrix Severin, who argued that towns keep customs the way hulls keep tar.
