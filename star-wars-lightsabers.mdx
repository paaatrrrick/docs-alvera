## Overview

The lightsaber is the signature weapon of the Jedi Order and Sith, an elegant energy blade powered by a kyber crystal. Described by Obi-Wan Kenobi as "an elegant weapon for a more civilized age," it can cut through nearly any material and deflect blaster bolts.

## Construction

A lightsaber is hand-built by its wielder as part of their training. Core components include:

- **Kyber crystal** — a Force-attuned crystal that focuses the blade's plasma and determines its color.
- **Emitter** — projects and shapes the blade.
- **Power cell** — supplies energy to the plasma loop.
- **Hilt** — the metal handle, often personalized to the wielder.
- **Activation switch** — ignites and extinguishes the blade.

## Blade colors

The color of a lightsaber blade comes from the kyber crystal attuned to its user.

| Color | Common wielders |
| --- | --- |
| Blue | Jedi Guardians (Anakin Skywalker, Obi-Wan Kenobi) |
| Green | Jedi Consulars (Yoda, Luke Skywalker, Qui-Gon Jinn) |
| Purple | Mace Windu |
| Yellow | Jedi Sentinels and Temple Guards |
| Red | Sith, created by "bleeding" a stolen kyber crystal |
| White | Purified crystals (Ahsoka Tano) |

## Notable variants

- **Double-bladed saber** — twin blades from a single hilt, famously wielded by Darth Maul.
- **Curved hilt** — favored by Count Dooku for precision dueling.
- **Crossguard saber** — vented quillons form a crossguard, used by Kylo Ren.
- **Lightwhip** — flexible plasma lash used by some Inquisitors and ancient Sith.
- **Darksaber** — a unique black-bladed saber forged by Tarre Vizsla, the first Mandalorian Jedi.

## Forms of combat

Jedi codified seven classical forms of lightsaber combat, each suited to different situations:

1. **Shii-Cho** — the foundation form.
2. **Makashi** — duelist's form, favored by Count Dooku.
3. **Soresu** — defensive form, mastered by Obi-Wan Kenobi.
4. **Ataru** — acrobatic offense, used by Yoda.
5. **Shien / Djem So** — counterattack form, used by Anakin Skywalker.
6. **Niman** — balanced diplomatic form.
7. **Juyo / Vaapad** — aggressive form, mastered by Mace Windu.

## Cultural significance

A Jedi's lightsaber is more than a weapon — it is a symbol of their commitment to the Order and a personal extension of their connection to the Force. Losing one is considered a deep failure, captured in Obi-Wan's warning to Anakin: "This weapon is your life."
