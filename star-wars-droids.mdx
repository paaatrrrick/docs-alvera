## Overview

Droids are mechanical beings that perform a wide range of tasks across the Star Wars galaxy. From loyal companions to battlefield soldiers, droids appear in nearly every era of the saga and often play pivotal roles in the story.

## Common droid classes

### Astromech droids

Astromech droids are utility units designed to assist with starship navigation, repairs, and computer interfacing. They are a fixture of both the Galactic Republic and the Rebel Alliance.

- **R2-D2**: A resourceful R2-series unit who serves the Skywalker family across multiple generations.
- **BB-8**: A spherical droid loyal to Poe Dameron and the Resistance.
- **R5-D4**: A red-and-white astromech briefly considered for purchase by Owen Lars.

### Protocol droids

Protocol droids specialize in translation, etiquette, and diplomatic relations. They are fluent in millions of forms of communication.

- **C-3PO**: A 3PO-series protocol droid built by Anakin Skywalker, fluent in over six million forms of communication.
- **K-2SO**: A reprogrammed Imperial security droid who joins the Rebellion in *Rogue One*.

### Battle droids

Battle droids form the bulk of the Separatist army during the Clone Wars. They are mass-produced and typically operate under centralized command.

| Model | Role |
| --- | --- |
| B1 battle droid | Standard infantry unit |
| B2 super battle droid | Heavy assault unit |
| Droideka | Rolling shielded combat droid |

### Assassin and bounty droids

Specialized combat droids are often used by bounty hunters and criminal syndicates.

- **IG-88**: An assassin droid bounty hunter from *The Empire Strikes Back*.
- **IG-11**: A nurse-reprogrammed bounty hunter droid featured in *The Mandalorian*.

## Cultural role

Droids occupy a complex place in galactic society. While they perform vital labor and often display personality and loyalty, they are typically treated as property and frequently subjected to memory wipes. Stories like *Solo* and *Andor* explore questions of droid rights and autonomy.
