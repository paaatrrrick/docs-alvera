## Overview

The Sith Order was an ancient sect of Force-wielders who drew their power from the dark side. Forged through hatred, ambition, and the pursuit of unlimited power, the Sith waged war against the Jedi for thousands of years, rising and falling across every era of galactic history.

## The Sith Code

Where the Jedi seek tranquility, the Sith embrace passion. Their code is a rallying cry for transformation through conflict:

- Peace is a lie, there is only passion.
- Through passion, I gain strength.
- Through strength, I gain power.
- Through power, I gain victory.
- Through victory, my chains are broken.
- The Force shall free me.

## The Rule of Two

After the Sith nearly destroyed themselves through infighting, Darth Bane established the Rule of Two: there can be only two Sith at any time — a Master to embody power, and an apprentice to crave it. This doctrine ensured the Order's survival in secret for a thousand years before its return during the Clone Wars.

## Ranks within the Order

| Rank | Role |
| --- | --- |
| Acolyte | An initiate drawn to the dark side, often before formal training |
| Apprentice | A student bound to a single Master under the Rule of Two |
| Lord | A fully trained Sith who has earned the title of Darth |
| Master | A Sith who has taken an apprentice of their own |
| Dark Lord of the Sith | The supreme authority, ruler of the Order |

## The path to power

A Sith's ascent is fueled by ambition and pain rather than discipline alone:

- **Acolytes** are recruited from the Force-sensitive, often those embittered, exiled, or rejected by the Jedi.
- **Apprentices** train under a single Master, learning forbidden techniques, alchemy, and Sith lore.
- **The final test** for many apprentices is to kill their Master — proving they have surpassed them and are ready to rule.
- **Sith Lords** scheme across centuries, manipulating governments, wars, and entire civilizations toward their ends.

## Sith weapons and artifacts

The Sith built their power on more than the Force alone. They forged red-bladed lightsabers from synthetic crystals "bled" with rage, and amassed holocrons, amulets, and ancient tombs on worlds like Korriban, Malachor, and Exegol. These artifacts preserved forbidden knowledge across generations, even when the Order itself was thought extinct.

## Legacy

Though Darth Vader cast Emperor Palpatine into the second Death Star's reactor and ended the Rule of Two, the dark side never truly died. Cultists, Inquisitors, and Sith loyalists continued to draw on its teachings — proving that as long as ambition and fear remain, the shadow of the Sith will linger.
