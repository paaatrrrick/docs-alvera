## Overview

The Sith Order was an ancient sect of Force-sensitives who embraced the dark side of the Force. Driven by passion, ambition, and the pursuit of power, the Sith stood as the eternal antagonists of the Jedi Order across millennia of galactic conflict.

## The Sith Code

The Sith Code stands in direct opposition to the Jedi Code, celebrating emotion and conflict as paths to power:

> Peace is a lie, there is only Passion.
> Through Passion, I gain Strength.
> Through Strength, I gain Power.
> Through Power, I gain Victory.
> Through Victory, my chains are broken.
> The Force shall free me.

## The Rule of Two

Established by Darth Bane after the destruction of the Sith at the Battle of Ruusan, the Rule of Two limited the Order to only two Sith at any time:

- **The Master** — embodies the power of the dark side.
- **The Apprentice** — craves it.

This rule prevented the infighting that had previously destroyed the Sith and ensured each generation would surpass the last.

## Powers of the dark side

Sith pursue Force abilities forbidden to the Jedi, including:

- Force lightning
- Force choke
- Sith alchemy
- Drain Force
- Essence transfer

## Sith weapons

Sith traditionally wield red-bladed lightsabers, the color produced by "bleeding" a kyber crystal through focused rage and pain. Variations include the double-bladed saberstaff favored by Darth Maul and the crossguard design wielded by Kylo Ren.

## Legacy

Though the Sith were believed destroyed at the Battle of Endor with the deaths of Darth Sidious and Darth Vader, the dark side endured. Sidious's return decades later, and the rise of figures like Kylo Ren, proved that the Sith's influence on the galaxy was never truly extinguished.
